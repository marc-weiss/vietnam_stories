I lifted him up into the helicopter and set him next to me on the door gunners seat. We shared a C ration pound cake and then he curled up next to me with his head on my arm and went to sleep. I could see now how weak he was and it occurred to me that he would soon be dead. He clung to my arm while he slept, savoring a few moments of companionship in the shade. When it was time to go I set him down on the ground and offered another can of C rations. Without hesitation he offered his carving in return but was delighted and relieved when I handed it back. He stood near the spot where we met as the helicopter turned to fly away and I wondered where he had come from and where he would go. There was nothing but moon scape for many miles in any direction.

Chuck Sowards
