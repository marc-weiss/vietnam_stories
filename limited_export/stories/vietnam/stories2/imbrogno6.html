The riverfront park is usually full of shouting and horseplay. But the memorial had generated a hushed zone all around it. People spoke quietly. Wiped tears and hugged. Crouched and touched names. Reverently placed pictures and flowers at its base and stood back.

We live in an age that places little public value on art. Our government spends more money on its many military show bands than it does on funding for all of the arts. It's good to be reminded that art in the form of a public monument can rock people to their cores. When it's true art, you don't even need the actual object itself. That's how powerful it can be.
