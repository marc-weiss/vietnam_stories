Dennis Sherwood

In that early light I imagined those one-hundred and forty slabs as television screens, neatly lined up and nearly blank, but with something etched across them, a blurred image difficult to make out like late at night after stations sign off and there's only static to stare at. They were the way I saw the war, those screens. The only way that most of us ever saw it. So how could we really know? What it was like, I mean, from so little that we could see?

Someone was leaning against the wall trying to decipher it....
