Bill Garbett

Post Traumatic Stress Disorder consists of a set of characteristic symptoms following a psychologically distressing event that is outside the range of usual human experience.

The onset can be anytime from weeks to years and it lasts a lifetime. For the Viet Nam vet, it is compounded and exists on a continuum. The casualties of Viet Nam begin with 58,000+ names on a black Wall in Washington, 10,000 graves at Trung Son Cemetery, the amputees still at Thuan Thanh Institute, 120,000+ suicides by Viet Nam vets, 67,000 Viet Nam vets in jail but don't end with the many, many apparently well adjusted "normal" vets making a continuing contribution to society.
