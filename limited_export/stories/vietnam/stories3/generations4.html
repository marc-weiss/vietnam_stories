...the shame over Vietnam must be put away, not only in terms of our government's conduct of the war but in terms of each soldier's personal conduct, those actions that were elicited from them during a time of great stress, confusion, survival, bonding, and pain. And I beg of you who served in the war, please talk with us, your children and grandchildren, share with us that part of your lives that never leaves you, allow your stories to guide us as nothing else ever will toward a more humane and informed citizenship and community in the world. As part of the Post War Generation, we are in this together.

Joel Hernandez (excerpt)
Submitted to Dialog Area, Post War Generation on Nov. 17th, 1996
