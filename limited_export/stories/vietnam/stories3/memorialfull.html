John Brain


Memorial

At last they have made to the war dead
A fitting monument.
Not a boast, not a victor's trumpet,
But a long black wall
Telling the monotony of their dying,
Not of heroism,
But of honest loss, the fact of their extinction.

Perhaps it needed that bad war, dissension,
Anger, burning the draft cards and the flags,
Riots and bayonets, bullets and flowers,
. To purge us of illusions: war is not noble
They did not know what it was they died for
And neither do we, now ten and more years after,
Except to make true the lies of their leaders,
To win the hearts and minds of their own people.

And maybe there should be another wall,
A long black slab,
Granite reflecting a Vietnam sky, cataloging
The hundred thousand names
Of those who died to liberate their country,
Who also died believing
Their cause was right, soldiers in black pajamas.

City of monuments, of tombs and speeches,
Of marble rhetoric and rampant stelae,
Plush offices where officers gave orders
That grimy grunts obeyed in filthy foxholes;
0 glittering and meretricious city!
How will you greet this mound, this new arrival,
Creeping along the ground in half submersion,
Whose utterance is like an incantation,
Names that will never make the social blue book,
The White House teas, the first nights at the opera,
Names of the poor this death their one distinction?

Today they come in thousands, relatives
And scarred survivors,
Those who for years have felt the unjust stigma
Of being both too militant and losers,
Denied the tools
And told to win a quick and easy victory,
A junkyard army
Reminder of a war no longer wanted.

Yet in the early days, their mission
Seemed only right. Was it not proper
America should stand against aggression,
The slow submersion of a friend in trouble
And save all Asia for the cause freedom?
Is it not right
To save a neighbor sinking in the quicksand,
Even if both are then pulled down
Into the ooze,
Into the black bog where no help is useful?
When do you stop?
When do you say Enough! Enough! It's useless!
And watch him go
Eye-deep in slime, eyes doomed but still appealing,
Into the sucking dark?

And how do you face
The fact that all that hardware, all those tricks
Turned by technology to win a war
The lasers, missiles, chemicals, and fire power,
The most promoted arsenal in history,
Did not destroy the little men with sidearms?
Not the idea
That they could win, despite their awful losses,
Because their book of history had said so --
See, it is written:
Power to the People!

No, it is not resolved,, it is not concluded.
We have not come to terms with all the issues
And made a peace
With ourselves, pulling it all together.
Success proves everything; the victory
So clearly demonstrates the victor's rightness,
But losing, failure, leaves the mind in ribbons,
What did we do wrong? Who made which decisions?
Whom can we blame, whom exculpate? The nagging
Anguish goes on until we bury it,
Until another generation
Says, "Very sad, but we were not invited."

What have we learned?

All that we know is that grief is real,
And grief must be respected.
The man who turns his face away, the buddy,
And the girl dabbing her cheeks,
That they are rightly moved
By an honest statement. Stone is to be trusted.


John Brain November, 1982
