I joined in the Navy in 1979, and after bootcamp I met another sailor in Seaman Apprentice Training. Ernie and I went overseas together onboard the USS Midway CV-41. Although we were several years apart in age, Ernie being the oldest, we soon became very close pals. We spent a lot of time together on liberty, doing the thing sailors do best while in port, that being drinking as much booze as possible and chasing skirts. After we were onboard the Midway for about a year, one day Ernie came up to me and said he was leaving, going back to the states to be discharged from the Navy. I thought he was pulling my leg at first, but then I saw some tears in his eyes, and I asked what had happened to cause him to be discharged. All he could say was that it happened a long time ago, and that he was not suppose to talk to anyone about it. I found this to be odd, but didn't question it any further. We just went out and tied on a good one, and said our goodbyes, then I lost touch with him untill a couple years later when I went back to the states and visited him. It was then that Ernie told me why he was discharged from the Navy. The reason was, when Ernie was 15 years old, he somehow was able to convince the Marine Corps that he was 18, and joined the Corps. I'm not exactly sure on the year he joined, but he told me he spent 2 tours of duty in Vietnam. I won't tell his story, but I would like to know if anyone has ever heard of any other Nam vets that were in country and under age as Ernie was?
