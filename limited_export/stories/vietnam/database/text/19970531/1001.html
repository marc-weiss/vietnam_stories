Drunken Disgraceful Soldier I'll never forget the time I was watching the Memorial Day parade two years ago in New York City and I saw a drunken disgraceful Vietnam Veteran stumbling down Fifth Avenue. He had to be supported by one of his comrades or he would have fallen down. That's how drunk he was. He was dressed in the standard Vietnam Vet costume-Army jacket, blue jeans, sneakers, long hair and beard. I wanted to go right up to him and yank him out of the parade. Oh!, how I HOPE this generation of men will pass away soon! All of this self indulgent crap about the Vietnam war makes me want to puke! The Vietnam war was like all other wars. The reason this particular war has had such a big impact on my generation is because THE UNITED STATES LOST THE VIETNAM WAR! And nobody likes to be a loser. Now, this is not the first time we as a society have lost a war-read about the war of 1812-and it will not be the last time this society looses a war! Yes the men and women who served bravely in Vietnam got a raw deal. Yes this war was planned and executed by a bunch of elitist bureaucrats in our government and it was fought by young men drafted from poor and working class neighborhoods from all over this Nation and yes those kids got a raw deal because they fought bravely and gave their lives while the upper classes avoided the war for the most part (Our wonderful President is a shining example) But what about those people that were our enemies at that time; the people we derisively called "slope heads." Do you remember them? Do you know that our Vietnam enemy lost over three hundred thousand men women and children in that terrible war? Do you now know that we underestimated them? Because they where short and yellow colored and Asian and technologically backward, those ass holes in Washington thought that they would be push overs. Didn't they realize that the Vietnamese had been defending themselves quite successfuly against the Chinese for thousands of years!? Didn't those Washingtonian ass holes ever bother to take the time to read Clausewitz's ON WAR, orMachiavelli's THE PRINCE? Well anyway-the war was fought on our side by a bunch of working stiffs from poor neighborhoods and by poor working stiffs and peasants, on the Vietnamese side. We lost, they got their country back after losing it to the French for so long (remember the French?) You see, we took over from the Frenc after they were defeated by the Vietnamese. So the Vietnamese had to fight to get back their own country, twice! So now what. I can tell you that the younger generation neither knows nor cares about the Vietnam war. That was something that "Dad" did in ancient times! Little do they know what's in store for them. So let's get off this self indulgent, sentimental mawkish mind set that people of my generation are still in and let's get on with living in the present THE FUCKING PRESENT is what counts! And one more thing-I DID NOT participate in that fiasco that was called the Vietnam war and I am proud of it! It means that I don't have the loser mentality that so many Vietnam vets have. I'm proud to say that I always felt and always will feel that the Vietnam war was an immoral, disgraceful and divisive and I am glad I was able to keep my self away from it so I don't have to walk around drunk and acting stupid. Now, I know that only a small percentage of Viet Vets. behave in the way I have described, but that is the perseption our society has of Vietnam Vets-if anybody thinks about them at all. So lets live for the present, or let's live for the future so our kids have a fighting chance. Let's not forever keep indulging ourselves with the past horrors of a war that is long, long gone and fast becoming forgotten

John Mestres
1945
LIC, NY
