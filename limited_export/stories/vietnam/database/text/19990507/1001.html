About a year ago, I went to a book signing for "Shopping Cart Soldiers" by John Mulligan, a Scotland-born Viet Nam Vet. We were talking after his presentation and a woman approached him to have her book signed. She said that she wanted to give it to her ex-husband, a VN vet. She broke down into tears almost immediately, saying that her husband had been profoundly effected by his experiences and that had led to the breakup of their marriage. I was deeply effected by her misery and reflected on the impact that this war had on so many people, and for how long this pain has lasted.

At the same time of this encounter, I was trying to find out what happened to my friend, Tom Briggs (USMC). I only knew that he was killed in February of 1968. My other schoolmates had many ideas about his experiences in Viet Nam---that he was a tunnel rat, that he had been wounded, came home, and then returned to VN. Tom had been a star athlete in high school--track, soccer--and most of our friend envisaged him as the archtypal Marine, fierce in combat, clothed in glory. The truth is much more mundane. Tommy enlisted in late 1967 rather than be drafted. He ended up in Viet Nam in the middle of January 1968 and got sent to Quang Tri when the Tet Offensive erupted. He was dead within days, blown to mist by a mortar round. I realized when I learned this story that this situation was probably more common than most folks know, that many died within a short time of their arrival, without the experience to help them survive, and without the bonds with their comrades which makes survival odds greater. And the greatest irony is that Tommy wasn't an American citizen, and did not have to go into combat. He was also a Scot. But with Tommy, how could he not go?

(I was drafted in July 1966, got assigned to the MPs and spent my term as a stockade guard in Kansas. There were times when I felt that I had to go to VN, but in retrospect, I am glad I was spared the experience. This war will be with us for a long time, and that is both a blessing and a curse.)
