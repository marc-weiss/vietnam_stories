In June of 1966, I not only graduated from high school but, experienced the impact of death- for the first time. A young man I had dated and loved...died in Vietnam. He was killed by a land mine, only four days after his Unit arrived and was put into action. Bruno had received notice that he would be drafted so, being a perfectionist, he joined the Marines. Unfortunately, he soon learned that decision was a terrible mistake. For during his basic training, he was physically and mentally abused...but, that was the nature of the Marine training. We all knew, the military did not suit this kind, gentle, and mannerly- giant of a man. Likewise, his family and mine had an unspoken and fearful foreboding; and knew in our hearts- that he would be slain. But, we could not understand the way his death would alter all of our lives...and take away our innocence and trust. Indeed, his family seemed to quietly suffer a painful deterioration; their sadness and grief was palpable, and ever present. And thirty years later, my family still hurts, and mourns the loss of this wonderful nineteen year old young man. Moreover, we shall always regret the need for a memorial to the death of yet, another generation of America's promising youth. What a devastating and total waste, the games politicians play... inflict upon the patriotic masses.

Billie Precord
