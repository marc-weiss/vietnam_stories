This is not actually a story,but a poem of how the tragedy of Vietnam affected one individual. Entitled "A Vietnam Vet I Never Met." A small brass plaque marks a grave where six feet below Lies the body of a soldier who died many years ago The chiseled out inscription on the stone above his head Doesn't tell the story of why this man is dead Although he fought in many battles and proved he was brave It wasn't an enemies bullet that put him in this grave It wasn't drugs or alcohol that took his life away He wasn't in an accident on that fateful day It wasn't an infection nor some horrible disease It wasn't fear of man that brought him to his knees He didn't ask no favors when they sent him off to war He didn't question the country that he was fighting for He believed in God and country when we sent him off to fight He didn't have to ask if what he was doing was right He didn't run to Canada and forsake his home of birth He didn't riot in Washington and spit upon the Earth He didn't curse the flag and forget its' proud history The flag that made this country great and set our fathers free He didn't stand on soapboxes and proclaim that we were wrong He didn't run away when he faced the VietCong He didn't start that awful war but he's the one we blame He's not the one who lost but carries all the shame He could have been your brother your uncle or your son No one dared to tell him that this war could not be won And to show our appreciation we spat upon his face We made him feel that what he did was a humiliating disgrace We gave no praise or honor,no welcome home toast He killed women and children was our favorite boast We offered no understanding,we were heartless,cruel and mean This country was an awful mess the worst I'd ever seen We soothed our guilty conscience and wore this poor man down Is it any wonder that he's buried in this ground? The anger and unkindness just tore this man apart The reason he is in this grave,is from a broken heart How do I know the way he died when this man I never met? It's something you feel from deep inside when you too are a Vietnam Vet!!!

Josh L. Garner
1940
Rogers,AR,U.S.A
