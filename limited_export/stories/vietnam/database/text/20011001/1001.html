I am a former Marine who sereved with Echo 2/7 in the Republic of Vietnam from March, 1968 to December, 1968 at which time I was wounded. When I returned to The World I was condemned as a killer and worse by people who had never served a day in uniform yet felt as if they had the right to judge me without ever knowing me. I am now a high school history teacher and hold four degrees but I still feel as if my life is not whole. To this day, I will not discuss my expierences with most people and then only if they are Vietnam vets themselves. I guess that I will never fully trust my fellow country men again yet I still love this country. It is for my friends who did not return that I feel the greatest sadness and I pray they will always rest in peace, Semper Fi.

Gary J. Phelan
1948
Corning, Ar. , USA
