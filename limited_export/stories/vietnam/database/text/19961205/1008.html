The war left external and internal scars, some healing faster than others. I wanted to go, was very curious and actually excited about my M.O.S.(11 Bravo). After landing at Bien Hoa, all of my senses seemed to click on, the smells, heat, vibes all around, I remember thinking I will never leave here. In some ways I haven't. The betrayal was our leadership. It was a disappointment, felt the Army left us down, now, when we needed it the most, the best decisions were made during the chaos by us the Grunts, some of us survived in spite of the "two sixes" or platoon leader. Returning, I thought I could change the world, tell people of the horrors, become involved in anti-war movements, but I copped out and let alcohol and drugs try to soothe whatever needed soothing. I have had three failed marriages, a child that grew up without a father, and lots of things I wish I could do over. Are they all because of The War, don't think so. Just another cop out. My life now is much better, drug and alcohol free for 10 years,a good job and a house in the woods, home , retreat, safe, to try to learn more about me, experience life close to nature and friends. I have a memory of a Christmas service in the field somewhere in the Delta. We were in a bad AO with a lot of contact, booby traps, and casualties. We secured an LZ for a visiting priest, and after landing he had to walk 100 meters through some elephant grass to our location. After the service the Priest cotinued on and more troops flew in to support us. We radioed them and advised the new arrivals to follow the path the priest used since it was safe, and to our dismay I watched booby traps explode where a priest had walked less than an hour before. I truly believe that someone was watching over me and I would not have made it without faith. Christmas remains a very trying time, with so many mixed feelings, the Vietnam Christmas story gets me through tough times. I feel a need to help others and I am disturbed to read of so many vets in homeless shelters. This medium perhaps can reach out and assist brothers who need assistance. Thank you for the opportunity to share this.

Bill
