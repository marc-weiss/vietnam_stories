Well, in 1964 I was 17 and I'd never heard of the WAR. My father, a WWII veteran, and career AF, a tail gunner on B52D's (tall tail. He retired and I enlisted in the AF in Dec '65. By then, I'd certainly heard of the Vn WAR. My brother, who is a year younger than me, had enlisted in the Navy and went straight to VN on LSMR USS Clarion River. He eventually did 3 tours in VN and his ship's complement was awarded the PUC and many other combat related awards and decorations. Meanwhile, I was undergoing Air Policeman training at Little Rock AFB, Arkansas. I was there for 2 years and then sent to Thule AB, Greenland. I can tell you I was terribly worried about my brother every day he was in VN. At that time, there was a policy that no 2 brothers would not serve in a combat zone at one time. My brother, Jimmy, eventually got off the Clarion River (a terrible WWII converted ship), and came back to the Phillipines. While there he was sent back to the states to train with SEAL teams as Boat Support for insertion and extraction of SEAL team members. He has many "fun" stories to tell about this training. Such as "not minding eating insects" when truly hungry, and always being mind numbingly tired. Well, I got mail from Jim, but he was living his life in VN and didn't really tell me or anyone else what was going on. In late 1968 he got out of the service and I exited the AF in '69. Two years of civilian life for me and then I was back in again. Forward to 1988: My brother is in the VA for PTSS. So, we fought the disabled veteran battle - and won! So, he hasn't worked for 10 years now. This is a guy who is stronger minded than me, and yet I plod along from day-to-day without much trouble. My heart goes out to all you vets who saw combat.

Just so you'll know, I got back in the AF and retired in '88. (Thailand - Security Police '72-73')

So, you can see I'm writing this as much for my brother as I am for myself.

Value: Sad about the general state of "civilians" Relationships: Divorced (? because of absences) Children: 1 daugher (Rachel) 4 kids Politics: Republican Livelihood: working in hospital Life or Death: trying to stay alive (want to see my brother live a LONG life) Philosophy: live & let live now (before, coming from a 3 generation family it was "bomb 'em." I used to ascribe to the saying, "bomb 'em 'till they glow." And, I was also fond of "kill'em all and let GOD sort'em out." No more. I thing WAR is horrible and I wish we weren't actively waging war at this time. I'm no prancing lib' just answering your question.

Thnx for the opportunity...
