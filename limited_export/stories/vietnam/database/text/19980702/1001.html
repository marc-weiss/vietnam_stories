I can remember the first time that Vietnam was brought up in discussion. In 1965, I was in high school debating the reasons for being in Vietnam. I argued for our presence in Vietnam never knowing that only two years later I would be in the jungles of Vietnam fighting an unseen enemy. Today I often think back about that time when I was so vain. Just two weeks ago my family and I returned from Vietnam. My sixth trip since I left in 1969, while serving with the First Infantry in the Parrots Peak area. Vietnam changed me from accepting many of things my parents and family had taught me. I learned first hand that it isn't a country or army that we are fighting in this life but its ourselves and what we want to do with our lives. We can accept the crudeness of hate and disgust or we can fight the battle for self respect individually and win. We must never give up our fight for others or it will overtake and destroy us and the children who know nothing but hate. I have grown to accept that governments will never change this hate that is in this world it is you and I. I learned after seeing three dead soldiers that life is precious and no one or government should impose a sytematic destruction of any peoples. Six, return trips to Vietnam have given me a much greater insight to how much even though we may live thousands of miles apart we are all looking for the same thing. Curtis Gilliland, Jr
