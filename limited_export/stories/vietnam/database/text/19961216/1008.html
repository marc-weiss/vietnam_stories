I was born in 1955. I was a high school student in Long Island, NY during Vietnam. I remember as a young person, the body counts on television and the turmoil. And the conflict I remember most vividly at my high school how the student who set a long jump record in track who’s name was on the board in our gymcame back from Vietnam with crippling wounds and how he sat in the classroom and talked to us about his experiences. I remember wondering if I’d have to go to college and have all the turmoil going on there with the student uprising and demonstartions and wondering about getting my draft card and I did, I was number 348 in the draft but by that time the draft was beginning to wind down and I wasn’t gonna go and I was really relieved. I was thinking about Canada.

I have a book of stories. It is simply called The Wall and it has testimonies of the men who did fight in Vietnam. I’m only able to read that book about twice a year because each time I pick it up- those 2 times that I can , it takes all the control ...and I can’t hold back and I start to break down and cry. I’m also brought to remember the anger and pain that I felt towards the returning veterans who came back from Vietnam...how much I hated them for what I felt was an injustice to the American people and also to themselves that I couldn’t quite put it into the proper words...

Mitchell Lopate

Matawan, NJ
