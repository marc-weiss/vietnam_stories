At age 50, I rode my bicycle across the U.S., from Astoria, Oregon, to Washington, D. C. The trip took me seventy-three days.

I ended up at the Vietnam war memorial, walked my bike by that long, black marble monument with the more than 50 thousand names inscribed on it, and like a three year old kid, I cried my heart out for my fellow veterans who could never make it to the half century mark as I had.

Life isn't fair, but it's beautiful. Never shall I forget those who died for us who could live.

I am 57 now. I'll never forget that walk.

George J. Smullen
1939
Waupun, WI
