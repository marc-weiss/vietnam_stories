I didn't know anyone who wanted to go to Vietman. They feared being killed or wounded. For years I stayed in school hoping that the war would end before I graduated. The draft board quit giving school deferments and my luck ran out. Luck had a lot to do with it. The draft lottery began 27 years ago and my number was 68. The rest is history.

All my hopes and dreams would end. No longer could I continue down the path of life I had chosen. My anger and pain still remain. And I blame the politicians for it. They are the ones who started the war. They are the ones who kept it going.

I have never been able to understand why. Why was it necessary? Why did so many have to die? Why were so many lives ruined? Why? It all seems so senseless. Why?

Robert Odendahl
1947
New Orleans, LA
