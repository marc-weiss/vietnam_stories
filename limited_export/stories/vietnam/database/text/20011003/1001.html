My dad served in the war. He was a door gunner and received a purple heart for jumping out and saving someone's life in Da Nang. I wish he would talk about it but I can see in his eyes back then when I was too young to understand the hell he'd been through. I don't think it meant much to him. He threw his medals away. I don't understand the politics of it totally, I don't understand what it was for. We didn't win, we didn't lose, we just gave up after every got killed. The moving version of the wall came to my town a couple of years ago. I insisted that my boyfriend (now husband) go to see it. At 11:00 that night, even the sight of an imitation of the real thing made me cry. I cried as read the letters to lost friends bound in plastic so the dew wouldn't ruin them. How can a man do his duty with such constant terror? I can't imagine. I am so amazed that somehow my father made it home. What made him so special that he didn't die? I am so proud that he enlisted and fought for what many people say was a pointless war. He thought he was doing the right thing. The government made him think at 17 that it was the right thing. My dad is a quiet man. If you mess with him though, I think he might just kill you. He divorced my mother when I was 11. Was and is he still torn? Half there and half here? How can that place ever leave someone? How can a person that went through it be sane?

mandy meyer
1975
