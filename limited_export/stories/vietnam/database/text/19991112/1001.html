Have you ver met a soulmate? Someone when you looked at, you saw yourself, so similar that you knew each others thoughts. So similar that you shared the same birthday, except that he was 9 years older and done three tours in Vietnam. I did. I met him four years ago at a fundraiser. Some of his harley buddies decided to show up to help raise money for a friends burial. From that moment, I have never been able to love any man like him. He was in the Air Force, a forward combat controller and flew for Air America. Being only 8 years old during the Vietnam War, I had memories that I had viewed on television, but never really understood the war. Well, my soulmate, and friend, in time, taught me much, almost too much. I began to feel part of his pain. I began to live it with him. I was there when he cried for his lost buddies, the last ones he allowed himself to love. I was there when he read the names at the memorial site. I was there when he woke up in cold sweats, crying out in fear. Dreams where he relived over, and over the horrors of war. Memories of the smells of death, of loss, of being so very afraid of dying, of trying to save a buddy he would lose. For four years, I stood by this man who relived Vietnam every single day of his life. A man I dearly love, but can't have, because War has killed his spirit and his ability to love again. Damn anyone of you that are so ignorant to see the truth of Vietnam. These babies,these idealistic boys, many of who had no choice, thought they were doing what was best. They didn't take a cowardly way out. Many have given up their lives for us, and still are, as well as my one true soulmate, Ron Perez.

I love you. Please come home to me. Marcie
