During the war I was in high school. In 1966 I graduated and went to college. I was drafted in march of 69. After entering service I became a Warrant Officer and flew Cobra helicopters. I crashed on a mission in Cambodia, june 1970. It was a bit frightening at the time, but the rescue happened so quickly that I wasn't scared until I had to transfer to a second helicopter. What frightened me most was worrying about that helicopter. I didn't want to crash again. That fear was a temporary lose of confidence in the machine and not the fear of being shot. I experienced personal fear only once more in Vietnam. That also involved another helicopter crash. My most upsetting moment was learning about the death of a friend that I had known for less than one year. Patrick and I had become good friends during our final months of training in flight school. He was outspoken and enjoyable to be around. He had a flair for life that seemed as innocent as a child and as full of energy as a young pup. The loss was sad for his family and his fiance. It was the first time in my life that a death had made me cry.

Kenneth E. McGill
1948
