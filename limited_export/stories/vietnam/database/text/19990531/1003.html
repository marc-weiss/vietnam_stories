Love, Peace & Flowers, Not_war! Hippies, Gurus, and Vietnam!

Love, Peace, and "if you're going to San Francisco, be sure to wear some flowers in your hair" the lyrics of one famous song of the late 1960's; also, Aquarius, a song from the Broadway hit "Hair", about the Golden Age when Jesus will bring much Love, Peace, and Freedom, without war; and also "Blowing in the Wind"are songs that typify the era when War and Peace were definite opposites, with the great military powers fighting in the Names of Peace and national self-ish interests while killing, imprisoning and cruelly crushing those who peacefully fought with words and pen protesting the wars in Vietnam and Indochina S.E. Asia, a long war which killed millions of poor innocent defenseless men, women, and children, even burning them with napalm in an attempt to exterminate the poor majority who had voted for their own form of government under the rules of the Geneva Convention for self-determination. See "The Vietnamese Victory of Right over Might", Letter No. 342, excerpts on pg. 121 in Back to the Basics. I must begin with these thots because 1965-70 were years when my life completely changed in so many ways, nearly 180ş from how I was raised as a military Navy brat, from a family history steeped in the American military stories of war at the expense of the poor and the innocent, the meek of the Earth who shall inherit it, Jesus said. My Great-great-great Grandfathers and Uncles were soldiers under General George Washington & General Green in the American Revolution of 1776. They were succeeded by more great Grandfathers and Uncles in the Civil War years who migrated further West, some to take over the Indian lands in the great Oklahoma Land Rush, such as my Great-grandfather Nathaniel Johnston & family in wagons swimming their teams of horses across the river to beat the other wagoneers being ferryied across. Then there was my great Uncle Edward Johnston, a Rough Riders veteran with Governor Theodore Roosevelt attacking Cuba, actually the sovereignty of Spain, in the Spanish-American War of 1898. Uncle Edward became a Vice President of the Rough Riders Association. We have one letter written to him, the original here at home, in answer to Edward from Theodore Roosevelt dated from Albany, New York circa 1900 from the Governor's mansion before he became President of the USA. My pioneer farm-homesteading Grandmother Pansy Johnston C. in New Mexico wrote about her brother Edward and his history to my cousin Jeannie. Then there were the World Wars I & II, my dad Donald Johnston C., a Chief Warrant Officer2 veteran of almost twenty years service as a medic-nurse with the 4th Div. Marine Corp in the Pacific on Iwo Jima and Hawaii, leaving Pearl Harbour about Sept-Oct. just before it was attacked on December 7, 1941. My oldest brother Donald is a retired Aeronautical Engineer from Lockheed Space & Missile Co. where he helped to design Nuclear Submarine Trident Missile engines for the soon coming all-out Nuclear War of the Super Powers - the USA versus the rest of the Whole World. He supervised younger engineers. So you thought there was Peace at last? Vietnam Two of my other five brothers, Alan second oldest and William slightly younger than myself, were both Vietnam war veterans from'65-'70 in the Air Force and Navy respectively; Alan a warehouse man, and William a gunner's mate. I was in my second year ('67) at St. Mary's College of California at Moraga when I attended one of the largest peace marches and rallies against the Vietnam war in San Francisco where I left my heart for awhile and caught a flyer dropped out of the sky giving options for action. I chose to become a Conscientious Objector. A draft notice soon arrived for me before school ended. I had to go! It was a terrible trial feeling stuck between the devil and the deep blue and dark sea, or between a rock and a hard place. I was resigned to it. But I felt so angry at my parents for not supporting me, that I then intended to go into the army and get lost and never speak to them again. On the day I was to sign up, the mail arrived with a notice postponing my induction. Whew! A great weight was lifted from off of me! But my Selective Service Board denied my application for Conscientious Objection. However, when I entered the Fresno-Monterey Ryan Diocesan Seminary College in 1967, the board reclassified me as a ministerial student. After one year I was thrown back out to return to junior college. Uncle Sam did not call me again. My 317 Draft Lottery number in 1970 kept me out of the draft permanently. It was the best lottery I ever won! When I realized how God had protected me years later (Feb.'72), as I told my story to Lil' Andrew my new shepherd in the Lord, I broke down in tears crying very emotionally without control. I had just received Jesus into my heart and was beginning to see how God's Love was with me for many years without realizing it. But, of all things, I was born into this family, chosen to be a priest and king of God from before the Foundations of the World. Even a local Catholic priest told my parents that I would be a priest, and so I am, ordained by God Himself. You can't even imagine how that could be, but that's the rest of the story, to be told later - how I could go from the red, white & blue to the Blood of Jesus Christ. This is dedicated to Macy, the woman I met yesterday at Barnes & Noble in San Luis Obispo, Ca., saying to Macy that I should add somethin' on this concerning Vietnam, since I just handed her a printed home page of my weaving art. It is much more, than just my story, as you will read as you return for another visit. There are other links scattered on my pages giving you lots more to contemplate.
