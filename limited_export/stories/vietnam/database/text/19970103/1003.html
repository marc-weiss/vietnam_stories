The most profound effect on me of the American tragedy in Vietnam is my conviction that the U.S. Government should be far less willing to commit American troops or American money into “Nation-Building”, “Humanitarian”, or “Police Force Peacekeeping” activities in the 3rd and 4th world countries such as Somalia, Zaire, Bosnia, Haiti, etc.

These countries with histories of rebellions, dictators, coups, and tribal, religious differences are all LOSER’s GAMES. Once on the ground, facing day to day events and unfamiliar norms and cultures, U.S. civilian and military officials often apply American standards and goals to an unresponsive foreign culture. The result, as shown in Vietnam and Somalia, is “Mission Crews” with flags appearing on caskets in front of grieving families across the United States - not to speak of the dollar costs to the U.S. taxpayer. Recall those 241 Marines killed in Beirut in Oct. 1983? Those 17 Americans killed in Mogadishu in Oct. 1993?

The Truman and Eisenhower Administrations supported 80% of the cost of the 1945-1954 French war to re-install French colonialism. After the French defeat, American advisers were sent in south of the 17th parallel to do C.P.R. on, and create from scratch in the mirror image of America, a new country called the Republic of Vietnam. Mission Creep began and “the rest is history” and, arguably, the beginning of the Budget Deficit in the U.S. Treasury.

Nor should we forget the early 1960’s crises in Laos where American advisers were sent, and millions of dollars vanished in the intrigues, rice paddies, jungles, and Swiss bank accounts.

For 30 years, 1945-1975, over 50,000 American lives and billions of dollars were lost in South East Asia.

My hope is that our leaders in Washington will study and remember these events before sending our troops and money off on ill-considered military adventures, Peacekeeping activities, and Humanitarian missions.

Harold G. Moore, Lt. General, USA-Ret.

Auburn, AL
