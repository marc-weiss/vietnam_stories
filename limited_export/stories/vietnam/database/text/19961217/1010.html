To My Siblings: *** You didn't know him before he went over there. You don't remember how fun he was, or how much he loved to play with kids. You don't remember the man who told mom he loved her, and kissed her, in front of me.

I remember, but it's hazy, and gets more so all the time. *** You have only seen the man who drinks, the man who beat up on us, the man who served overseas and came back not knowing how to relate -- let alone love -- anymore.

I was five when he came home. You were two; the others weren't born yet. *** I had a nightmare, and wandered into the bedroom, and he jumped out of bed ready to attack...it took me a long time to understand he thought I was Viet Cong. *** He started drinking when he came back, and hasn't stopped. He never talks of the war or any other military service. It's a huge secret, as most things have become in our family.

I have a New Testament I found in a box in the garage when I was a teenager. I dug it out of it's place awhile back. The inscription haunts me now that I am older, and fully understand:

"To Rich -- Come back to us safely, Our Darling Son -- love Mom & Dad, 1971"

I've not yet asked my father who Rich was, but I'm sure he didn't return as safely as his parents prayed. *** There are times I wished my father had died over there. It sounds truly horrible, but you didn't live with him. "Post-traumatic stress" is a nice way of saying alcoholic and abusive in our family.

What I truly wish, however, is that I hadn't known him before he went. That I was more like you, as young as you, and didn't know there was another person he took with him to Southeast Asia, and never brought back.

I'm 30 years old, and I want my Daddy back.

Michelle
1966
Albuquerque, NM
