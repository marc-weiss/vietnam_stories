As I was colllecing oral histories of the Vietnam War, I came across a seemingly unverifiable story. Two brothers-in-law went to Vietnam in '68. One was killed; the other escorted the body home. At the San Francisco airport, several war protesters somehow got onto the tarmac and attacked the casket. Airport security stopped them. The incident was reported by other family members and the escorting brother-in-law became the object of investigation by the Army. They concluded that he was lying to keep from returning to Vietnam, indeed, that the incident never happened. I believe him; however, there is no way, it seems, to verify the incident. This happened 30 years ago, and DOD, Army, FBI, the airport claim to have no records. I want very much to prove this happened. Although I realize it probably is nearly impossible to verify this particular incident, surely this type of protest happened at other times and at other airports. Any stories out there about attacks at airports? Renate W. Prescott: klibi@juno.com
