On a visit to "The Wall" in 1989, my then 16 year old son wrote the following poem:

THE WALL BY JEFF BROWN

Black and endless it stretches toward the iron men who gaze upon it. Silence follows every man, woman, boy, and girl to it. The names shine as though in glory. Cards and flowers line the base and wither with the memories of the men who gave their lives for a war which was not their own. It stands unmoved by time. Generations that look thereon no longer sense the sorrow that was once felt by others. And so, all that is left is name after name carved into massive stone. Why?

Jeff is now 23 and lives in Boston. His occupation is computers, but his love is poetry. He has self-published two books of poetry.

Kenneth L. Brown
