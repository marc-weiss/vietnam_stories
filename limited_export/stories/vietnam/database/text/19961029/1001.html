Point of View

The Wall is a frozen whisper of names that will forever echo throughout our history. Thanks to Maya Lin, that wall will stand as a monument to the men and women who lost their lives in Vietnam.

Etched in black granite are not only the names of white Americans, but names also of those of Asian, Hispanic, Filipino, Native American, African, and other non-European ancestries. They were all Americans, each life a thread cut short, snipped from the colorful tapestry that was and is America.

As a former Marine of the Vietnam era, I have tried to capture the story of an Asian American combat veteran and the great sin he committed in Vietnam.

"I killed because I was Asian fighting in an Asian war. How else could I prove I was American?" David Wong, protagonist of An American Sin, asks.

The novel explores the issues an Asian American must face when he is asked to kill Asians and how someone of that ancestry may go too far in his desire to be American.

Toward the end of the novel, it is at the Vietnam Veterans Memorial where Wong achieves a catharsis. Excerpts of An American Sin are online at my website, http://www.vena.com/authors/1027bi.html.
