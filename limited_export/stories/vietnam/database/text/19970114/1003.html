Reflections On The Experience Of Life During The Vietnam War Era - Then And Now

I was one of many young people in America who came out against the war. The war hurt all of us, our parents, our planet, while at the same time opening our eyes to an innocence we'd lost during the 1950's. Yes, that "wonderful" time in the United States was really a period in which we'd become bloated and arrogant to the extent that we felt that we were liberators of all the world. Vietnam awakened us to the hollowness of our deluded self proclamation by becoming a wonderfully complex bloody mess ... out of control.

I had gone through U.S. Marine boot camp at the end of 1965, just after we were really into the war. We were lied to by the media and our own higher ups in the Marine Corps. We were "kicking ass". In reality we were taking heavy casualties. This lie was hard to forgive. It's hard to forget what "our own people" did to us. I can still say this from the pain I feel when I say it. I was a reservist, so I was spared the face to face physical reality of the war directly. Many of the people I went through "boot" with weren't so lucky and a lot of them are no longer alive on this account. Learning of the casualties was hard... And you know, in modern warfare it doesn't matter whether you're a competent soldier, sailor, Marine... When a mortar round hits it takes anyone near it.

So I became anti-war, and in a "different" sort of way from many. I was fighting the war on this side of the world. I was doing so, carrying a lot of pain accumulated from a close association with so many whom lost everything on its account ... My brother in law was killed there in 1970... It went on and on for us. Hating and angry - distrusting parents, older people who had short haircuts(!) - upset by anti-war people who were insensitive to the situations of our own military people, many of whom were not involved by personal choice. So some of us felt "scalded" from all sides. And who remembers the casualty reports of "light", "medium" and "heavy" (what the hell?! Are they advertising Gillette razors?) Then later on in the war the numbers... and could you believe them?! Well, we've "healed" a bit (some of us) to the point where we know (sometimes) we have to defend and take care of our own. Some kind of "new and different" realization, huh? At any rate it hurts, but life must go on, and so it hurts a little again to realize there's still controversy about something like the Vietnam Wall Memorial. (Just like our own people doing it to themselves again, isn't it?) My own point of view is that any and every kind of memorial should be okay without a lot of hassle and dirty double dealing. But then, as I said life goes on. We've got to take care of ourselves and our own. Please excuse a Marine who said "Hell no!" He'll always stand by his own in whatever way seems right. Excuse me Mr. Nixon who thought we needed to be pardoned, in an "Amnesty Program". Just because we acted freely in good faith for our own enlightened self interest... And one more thing - God rest the guilty souls of the war criminals who knew better and furthered the extension of the war anyway. Will it do any good to put Robert MacNamara on trial? He has to live with what he's done... He IS a criminal though. Well, we carry on. God bless the Wall! We all need something to believe in on this sometimes treacherous journey of our lives.

Standing Guard, Gordon M. Browning
