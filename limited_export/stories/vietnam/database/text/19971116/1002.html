Drafted, 8/68, age 19. Vietnam 69-70 1st.Inf. Div. III corp. , involved in military counterinsurgency operation Phoenix. (hearts and minds venture). I usually hit a block at this point as I have for the past 30 yrs. Vivid memories, getting off the plane in VN then getting on the plane to leave VN. Getting that little gift wraped package for the beautiful blond haired girl in Oakland CA went I went into the air port to go home. I opened it as I waited for my plane. It was a lovely turd, with a little sign " This Is YOU". What happen to me in Vietnam couldn't be written in the next 5 yrs. I don't have the smarts to explain it on my own. Never again will I be asked "did you kill anybody" or "What was it like" I wish a thousand times to be back in the conflict, there everything was simple, clear. Survive or die. People you were with, honest, dependable, would die for you or you for them. Rich , poor nothing mattered , your goal the same. To live. Get to go home. Everyday there was seconds ago, lived and relived with all the vivid stench of reality. Compounded by the attitudes and reactions of a uncaring public, we withdraw to our shell of those past times. Went back to collage, wonderful jobs, and wonderful jobs, gone. I have ran from Tennessee and my homeland to Tx., to Montana and now Wa. state. Trying to outrun the those guilts of so called duty to serve my country. I left an eye, part of a hip and one of the family jewels in that blood laden paridise. Here I am today in all my warriors glory. Rated 100% worthless by the Veterans Administration , I slug along , trying to make sense of another day. I write this only because I burn sometime to get on my soapbox and scream to the world, the truth. God, I wished I could relate with precision what the 30 year blight means to me. Would you like to know how it shaped my values? What I'd give to be able to cry . Cry at a funeral, at someone's misfourtune, to feel pain with others. To care to hear about others happiness, and feel it with them. I pray I knew what my values are. But, since 87 when I tried to ram a log truck, I have been in therapy for cronic PTSD, a nut to the common person. After coming such a long way. I'm now homeless, even though I have a house, my children know only the burn of the symptoms, and very little of the cause. It reminds me of living with a old grandparent. You know they're there you just work around them. Funny thing, I have to go to the kids school sometimes and bail the out of problems they get into for being "just like their dad". Well enough of this babbling, Sorry for the clutter of you web. I just happen on this sight and this was one of those "daze" I felt like letting loose of something or somebody. Just push delete and you would be better off. Thanks, lg
