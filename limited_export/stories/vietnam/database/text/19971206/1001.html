Vietnam as is it's people was/is most beautiful.I wish I could afford to take my family there today.In 1966 I was a soldier there to help the Vietnamese people gain there freedom,I must admit that I felt pretty much alone,many soldiers actually did not seem to have any idea of why they were there or who was who!We were assigned to Co.B 4th. Btln.31st.inf.196th.lt.inf.Bde.at Tay Ninh & then to Chu-Lai.We were not well informed,on missions,so we simply done our best to survive,thought I did,some purple hearts,other medals but Charlie didn't get me congress did,got terminal cancer and a 100 percent disability,can't work,sleep,feel!I wish I could feel,not a drunk,do take pain meds. not from VA though,they want me to serve the rest of my time in the hospitil,won't do that,have children & grandchildren & this small voice via the internet,thank YOU,I am building a swimming pool for our family & the neighborhood kids and a trail down to the cove so they can go and fish.Also 1969-1971 served with the 175th.Avn.Btln.Vinh-Long,hope some of you are still living and mabe see this!To The People Of Vietnam all I can say is that we young Americans believed we were sent to help YOU,Sorry,Mabe the future will bring about a better platform,one of truth and mutual respect!God bless each and every one of us,may we seek wisdom and travel through the future with a spirit of Love combined with the true knowledge of the past.Sincerely The Knight Family
