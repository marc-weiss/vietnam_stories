My story occurred during the monsoon season of 1970 while on patrol with my unit of the 101st Airborne Division.

We had stopped for a break and I had just slipped out of my rucksack and lit a cigarette when some movement caught my attention.

I jerked my head around just in time to see a small piece of topsoil slide down the side of the hill I was facing. I immediately noticed that the sliding soil had uncovered some regular looking lines. I got up and inspected the lines more closely. What I discovered were huge stones, cut and fit perfectly by human hands. I stepped back and took a more panoramic view of this strange hill. What I determined was that this hill was four-sided, approx. 200 feet high and 100 feet per side. The sides tapered inward and upward! It was a pyramid! I went back to the area exposed by the slide and began scraping away the topsoil along the lines delineating the stone blocks and discovered more and more stones, each approx. 4 feet by 3 feet. I walked around the hill and discovered that it was situated between two other larger foothills. I then noticed that the distance from either side of the pyramid to the larger hill on that side of the pyramid was equal. I further noticed that the larger hill on the southern side of the pyramid had an unusual appearance. The larger hill was actually a cliff face rising high into the sky. What caught my eye about this area was that as I approached it I noticed that the cliff came straight down to the ground but a trail appeared, cut out of the cliff! As I climbed it the ground fell away and after a short distance I found myself with a sheer rising cliff on my left and a sheer drop to the valley floor on my right. The trail was cut out of solid stone but had been so heavily traveled that the side sloped inward toward the middle of the trail making the middle of the trail approx. 6 inches lower than the edges. The width of the trail was a constant four feet. I noticed that the platoon was readying to head out so I went back. I told my platoon leader what I had found and he had me show him. He told me to forget about it, but he wanted to follow the trail up the southern cliff. We humped up that trail for a day and a half. Four feet wide, sheer on both sides all the way up into the highlands. I have thought about this often over the last 25 years. I would like to be able to go back and search for the temple I found so long ago. Oh yeah, almost forgot. There was triple canopy growth covering the pyramid from top to bottom. This is how I knew that this place was no longer in the memories of men. I had discovered a lost buddhist temple, a temple that had been waiting centuries for the opportunity to reveal itself once again. I imagine great secrets and unspoiled scientific treasures still lying where they were placed a millenia ago, still waiting for me to return them to the light.
