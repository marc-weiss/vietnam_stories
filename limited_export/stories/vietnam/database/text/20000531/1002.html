I went to Vietnam as a Marine officer, after almost 5 years enlisted.

They offered me a commission. That meant one was part of about 30$ to 40% that made it through Officer Canndidate School - OCS.

Then they sent me "across the Interstate highway" at Quantico, VA to The Basic School, and finally to Oklahoma for Artillery School. Turns out everyone in The Basic School then (6 months) was either made infantry, artillery and a few went to flight training. Unusual for the Marine Corps.

Most became infantry. A few went to the U. S. Army's artilley school at Ft. Sill, Oklahoma for six months. That was six more months of training.

As far as I know, we all went to Vietnam. Those assigned to infantry, like every other officer, received all their infantry training at Quantico, VA in The Basic School. So by the time I got to the 5th Marine Brigade (bits and pieces all over the place) in Vietnam it was time for "Khe Shnn" - what a strange event. We spent about 3 months in western Quang Tri Province - pretty much as far north as one could go at the time. They told us we "won" - we torn the base down and continued.

Vietnam for me - at the "6 months" point where someone went on R&R (Rest and Relaxation) - was a good effort, but a looseing effort for America. The Frence tried, America tried, and many other nations tried to support a divided country.

I was in Hawaii (1st Marine Brigade) and all fighting forces were out of Vietnam well prior to the end.

As the Assistant G1, I made a decision. I would resign my "regular" office commission, go back to the "mainland", and finally get a college education.

I talked with the Commanding General (I was on his sraff) about this. He listened kindly, suggested some short sentences to the Prisident, and shortly thereafter orders arrived for my discharge. I flew to Treasure Island, California for discharge. On the first of May 1972 I was handed a check for "severnace" pay as a regular office and flew home.

By the middle of May 1972 I began college. It only took me 24 months to get a Bachelor or Arts. Later I continued to Ph.D.

Vietnam - as a war - was begun for reasons. But it quickly became a "war" in which few wanted to go, and many back in the "states" objected as the casualty count increased.

Does anyone remember Jane Fonda, then an actress? Does anyone remember her standing in downtown Hanoi and "protesting" US involvement? I do.

So I tried to keep my Marines alive so that they could go home. Most did make it home alive.

Vietnam has a history. Today it welcomes most anyone wanting to do business.

I made in through alive, but not wihout costs. Today I am totally disabled due mainly to being wounded on several occasions and walking on and continuing to fight.

Today Vietnam in one country. Today Vietnam, at least most of it, is stil trying to "do business" with the world.

I have memories, but that was just one "war zone" I served in with the Marines. It was probably the one war zone I will always remember. Always.

Wayne Neighbors, Ph.D.
1944
Florence, SC
