I was a lost soul at 18 with not much Direction in life. I received my draft notice and claimed I had asthma and tried to get out of the draft. I realized it wasn't going to work so I went down to the Armed Services enlistment building. As I walked in this guy grabbed me and pitch the U.S. Marines to me. Well...to tell the truth I didn't know Marines from Navy or Army, they all seemed the same to me. That is until I hit boot camp!

You see, being a civilian during the late 60's, we lived a different sort of life. Smoking pot and taking LSD was as normal as getting gas at the service station. I didn't see any reason not too. Getting high was a way of life then. I felt the pressure of the draft from my relatives and piers saying I will never become anything of myself. They pushed me into joining the military.

OH! When I took my pre-physical I thought it would be fun if I took some LSD with me....hehehehe...well I passed with "flying colors" one of my best trips.

Next thing you know I'm in boot camp. WOW! What a reality check. I had no idea I was a scum sucking maggot, low life piece of shit until my drill kindly pointed that out to me. These guys were with out a doubt the meanest, cruel, sadistic, assholes that ever walked the face of the planet. If fact one of them was court marshaled shortly after graduation for unethical treatment. And back in the late 60's boot camp was VERY tough compared to toady's standards. One positive note. It did teach me self-discipline that carries over with me today. With out that who know where I would be.

Next was infantry training. Nothing real special to talk about there. All the stories are the same.

Then we're of to Vietnam. When I arrived and placed with a unit someplace about 50 miles from Danang call AnHoa. Again I was told informed what level of the food chain I belonged to. The technical term was "Shit Bird!" My very first duties were filling and stacking sandbags, cleaning out the shit houses and taking tons of garbage to a dump just outside a Vietnamese village. GOD please help me! There has to be a better job than this. Going into the bush was the better job, so off I went.

I spent the next 6 months in the bush. We were always on the move. Looking to engage the enemy. But who was the enemy? They were anyone with slanted eyes and funny hats that looked just a little suspicious. They could also resemble someone who was scared to death. Any age or any sex. We interrogated everyone that we came in contact with or just killed them. What ever the C.O. felt was appropriate. In most cases I felt it was just a numbers game. Let's get some stats to send back to headquarters. I could tell some real horror stories but I don't feel like going there.

To this day I never understood why we were ever there in the first place. Was there a threat to national security? NOT! Were the South Vietnamese needing our help to combat the evil forces of the North. NOT! In my view our government wasted billions of dollars but more important, wasted the lives of thousand of young men (boys) our children. Crippled a few million Americans with scars that will never heal.

Yes!! I'm very proud to have served with my bothers at battle and would come to their rescue again in a heart beat. BUT!! I will never again just do what the government says just because they says it the right thing to do. I guess that why they took us so young. We just didn't know better.

.

Don Lavetter
1949
