I am an immigrant or rather a refugee like hundred thousands or millions of other Vietnamese. We were displaced because of the war. We lost our homes, relatives, friends and country because of the war. Yet, I have heard little about the ordeals that we, refugees, have to endure.

We have seen many stories and movies about the horribe, and indeed they are horrible, stories about the U.S. soldiers in Vietnam and when they came back home to the U.S. However, little has been documented or publicized about the many thounsands of Vietnamese lost at sea and land when they were trying to escape from the communist Vietnamese. How many stories have been written about the "boat people?" Many of us have met pirates at sea, lived in the most dispicable living condition in refufgee camps, mistreatment from the local authorities and discrimination when we arrived at our adopted country. Many women were raped or taken from us by pirates. There are many stories about cannibalisms. Has anyone ever look at these stories?

Personally, my story would be too long to tell here. But it is suffice to say that like may other Vietnamese refugees, we work hard to earn what we have today.

Quynh Bui
