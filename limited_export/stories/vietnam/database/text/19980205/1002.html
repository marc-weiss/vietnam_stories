As a young British Paratrooper serving in Malaya during the final throws of the Viet Nam War during 1974 I was curious, if not jelous, can you believe that? jelous of the soldiers I seen on R & R in Singapore that were fighting 'up country'. These soldiers were from Australia and New Zealand. We were training in the Malay Jungle for the very same war, but, for some reason, thank God, we never took part. At the time though, I wished we could join in and help our allies. Who could have imagined, that, eight years later, I would be fighting in a war in a another far away place called the Falkland Islands. No jungle, no trees even, just a barren, cold, windswept place which held a sort of wilderness beauty. The people we were fighting for are British, mostly people who moved to this far away colony to escape the 'rat race' back on Englands green fair lands. At least they were British and held the right to the defence of the realm. The Viet Nam Vet had no such grace of purpose, except the purpose of 'looking after your buddy'. I began to understand the horror of kill or be killed. The futile screams of dieing men in the black of night, things you dare not tell. Even our 'grand right' of self determination, for Queen and Country could not make any sense of it all....afterwards. The most important thing to me was the longing to be home again, the war film images of John Wayne were never further from my mind now. I now UNDERSTAND. How foolish I was in Malaya, how unfeeling I was for all that suffering. I am now a proud veteran, with the wisdom of battle burnt into my every moment. Even sleep can not hide the memory, for I dream of my friends still alive, to wake and remember they are dead...of course. To talk with my comrades is my only solice, for they only, can UNDERSTAND. I'm sorry viet Nam Veterans for my stupidity, I weep for you now in complete understanding, the print a blur through my tears. Your heros ok, fear not your not alone, even a victors army has a down side. "Well done my boy, you did us proud!" I supose its better than "you did your best", but, the war mongers buy you a drink and slap your back, when all you want to do is crawl away, alone, in the dark...to cry.

God Bless you, from a "knowing" friend.

Denzil Connick
