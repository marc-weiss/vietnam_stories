I am the child of a Marine who served in various battalion sized recon units during his 19 months in Vietnam.

I will always remember the intense agony that persists within my Father. There were countless times that I witnessed his sadness and anger. During most of my childhood I never knew what caused his pain. Confusion and fear always clouded my mind. On many occasions I recall him crying in the middle of dinner or other family activities. He is perpetually depressed and guilty about the atrocoties that he was involved in.

Growing up in such an environment has caused some emotional problems of my own. I deal with these problems and they are not a cause for concern. Who isn't adversely effected by child- hood events? What does concern me however is the frightening manner in which the Government took advantage of it's young people during the Vietnam war. My experience has caused me to be wary and untrusting of our government in every aspect of my life.

My Father and most of his comrades were raised in a setting that promoted patriotism and devotion to the causes of our Country. Objection to these causes was simply unthinkable. The people who claim that these young men had the choice to consciously object are sadly misguided.

The combat veterans of the Vietnam War paid dearly for thier blind allegiance to our unjust government. They paid in a way that we cannot begin to imagine.

In closing,I have two questions for the C.O.s who are bitter about thier lack of recognition and respect from the public. Did you fight for what you believe in the same way these men did? Did you take up arms against your enemy and offer your life and sanity to defeat him?

Philip
1971
Quincy, MA
U.S.A
