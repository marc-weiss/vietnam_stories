I was never in Vietnam...but I worked at a Government military facility supposedly in support of the war, and I gradually turned against it; and began to despise myself as working for something that took my hard work and turned it against everything I ever believed in....If one can shave in the morning, and look themselves in the mirror, okay...but I never could, so I took up shaving in the afternoon after I came home...in the tub, so I didn't have to look at my sad, oppressed eyes in the mirror. I am sorry that the opposition to the war on the campus where I attended University were so snobby about ordirnary working people from the forties, whom they called 'ethnics', but who were not ethnics, but good, loyal American citizens, often first or second generation Americans who had gone thru World War II and learnt to obey orders, therefore it was hard for them to believe that this war was different, and was murdering their sons and daughters for a useless war, in a useless cause...many of the 'best and brightest' never went; it was the sons and daughters of the American working and lower-middle classes who ended up having to fight the war in Vietnam...I had been given a college deferement, later a medical deferement, and so I was never threatened, except in my imagination with going off to Vietnam, but I later learnt a buddy of mine in high school had, two tours! I was fairly surprised, but then he had a terrible need to be accepted; maybe he could not accept the world he found in the U.S. during the War, and had to go back to make some sense out of things, I don't know....another buddy of mine had a nice cozy staff position in Saigon, so he was never threatened really with being a grunt and carrying on the war from that aspect...I felt that bringing the War home, nonetheless, to the people of the Forties and the Fifties on the job site was an important witness, and a powerful influence later on me becoming a Catholic Charismatic...nonetheless, a lot of anger was generated, on both sides, and I questioned everything about the war that I could find....I found a fairly revolutionary atmosphere on campus that I could not duplicate on the job site...many forties people were oppressed by their lack of status on the job site, yet they supported the war when maybe they had sent their sons or their daughters off to it (?) or maybe they hadn't...it really didn't seem to make much diffrence, nor could they hear me speaking honestly against the war...without becoming paranoid that the Government was listening in (free speech, right?) to them, so they wanted to be angry at me and tell me I was a Communist...no, I wasn't a Communnist, nor was Jane Fonda; today, she is married to one of the richest men in this country, so one knows now that she never was a Communist...nor was I...but the way the period people from the fifties and Forties were paranoid made me act out, rather than want to patiently explain to them anymore why the Vietnam War was wrong, and was being lost...as indeed it was! It was a rather childish act, but they gave me no other way to witness that the war was lost, and should be ended at once! I was persecuted by the military and at one point given an examination by a doctor, who had been throughly cowed by the military system, who declined to find anything wrong with me, other than I was a normal young man; maybe to her I seemed somewhat immature, but I was trying to witness that the WAR WAS WRONG, and also was BEING LOST! (as indeed it was!) Nonetheless, when the North Vietnamese tanks entered Saigon, and crashed down the gate of the Vietnamese capitol building, and ran up the Viet Cong flag, I put my feet up on an old drafting table in an empty room, and knew that my day of celebration (to myself!) had come...I did not tell anybody I had been right....there was no need to...there was not a single noise out of all those people that formerly had been mad with me for opposing the war....all was dead quiet! And I was not prone to flogging a dead horse....nonetheless, later the persecutions started again, when a more politically correct military leader was appointed to our work site....but I again had a lot of pleasure when Nixon had to resign because of Watergate! I knew that Watergate was only a way of getting that paranoid little bastard not because he had fought the Vietnam war, but because he had suceeded to the Presidency when our generations' hero, Robert Kennedy had been assassinated! Another President Kennedy would have ended the war, and put the Forties people in their place who used to tell us the Kennedys didn't deserve the Presidency anymore (in spite of the fact they had kissed Jack's ass up and down when he was in office!). We had to wait many a year until first it was Jimmy Carter who won; only he rewarded ONE GUY with an official office who had opposed the war, (Sam Brown!) all the rest of us who had acted to end the war, marching in countless demonstrations, in spite of working for the Government, got nothing at all! I felt this was vastly unfair, that a whole generation of people, including myself, of course, should have been awarded big jobs and big promotions for daring to oppose the Vietnam quagmire when it was NOT so evident that the war would be lost....one might connect the Vietnam war to the site of a whole generation lost in World War I. so that nobody really won, and everybody lost World War One.....equally, did a whole generation lose over Vietnam; only now are the dues starting to be paid by those who disapproved of the war finally succeeding to the Office of the President of this country (Bill Clinton, a man of our generation!) whom I voted for, and THANK GOD did not have an old man of the Forties to ever beat him in this quest for the Office of the Presidency, a sign that someday our generation will be paid back in FULL for the courageous witness that they made, against the WAR in VIETNAM...

Lee C. W. HutchisonII. Kew Gardens Apartments, 2700 Que Street, N. W. Apartment #246 Washington, D. C. 20007
