I went to Vietnam without a clue of what I would find. The Army had trained me but I was not ready for what I would find. The waste of human potential the lack of choices and the fact that death was just one more thing that happened like eating or breathing affected me greatly. I was a little older then most when I went, I had graduated from collage and had a little more experience then many of the folks I met and of course I thought I could handle what was handed out. What I didn't know was that within one year I would forget most of what I had learned about civilization and I would become a very base animal whose main objective was to survive. After being put in this state I was picked up cleaned up and fed and then dropped into a society that spit on me, didn't understand me and didn't seem to care. It has been a long time and things get better but it was never easy. The experience made me an advocate for people who have no choices, it has made me distrust those in power who try to take those choices away. I became a teacher and have been one for 24 years I fear for the children and I fear for the rest of us. The values we should have learned we have forgotten, the people we should take care of we ignore. What happened to the people who spit on me and protested that I was a baby killer? Are they out there collecting stuff and leaving their babies in day care? Why do we seem less human then we use to? If this is what society learned from Vietnam I have learned the opposite.

Bill Gale
1946
Webberville, MI, Ingham
