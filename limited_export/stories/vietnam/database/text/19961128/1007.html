Being a young, dedicated High School teacher during the years of conflict allowed a perspective that still haunts me. I acted out my opposition to the war in both action and words. I demonstrated at the Avenue of Stars yelling, "LBJ, how many more you gonna kill today?" I spoke often and convincingly of my opposition to my 11th and 12th grade History and Government students, many of whom were to be drafted. One student (I still remember his shock of blond hair and crisp, blue eyes made a quick transition from Student Body President to Huey Pilot. He often parroted my opposition with vigor. Three years later he returned to set with me in an empty classroom to ruminate. The emotions were all gone and he said, "Mr. Herbert, you were wrong, the President is wrong, the army is wrong, and the Viet Cong have it all wrong." I asked, "What is right?" "My Huey and a clean weapon." He cried.

I was all wrong. The President was wrong. The army was wrong. The Viet Cong were wrong. May all Hueys be gone and all weapons rust.

Hal Herbert
1940
