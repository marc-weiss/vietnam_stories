First let me say that I think that this site is very well done, and is a definite "must" for my friends to visit.

I would like to say that I think the content of the site, is excellent. I know that there are many people who only have bad, negative, and often abusive remarks about Viet Nam, but I wanted to comment on the articles, especially the article contributed by Michael Little.

So many of us went through the Viet Nam conflict (another word for an unofficial WAR), and will never be the same because of it. That is very much like the war that my father fought in France and Germany (WWII).It changed his life, and made him appreciate this great country of ours much more. Like him, Michael Little expressed himself about the family he left in Viet Nam. These are the experiences that cause us to become better people... There is always so much more to a war than just killing innocent men, women, and children. We see the effect of our aggressiveness (or in the case of Viet Nam, our lack of aggressiveness through reconnaissance exercises) upon the lives of innocent people.

My thoughts are turned to the many friends that didn't come home, and to the people who were left suffering in a war-ravaged country forced to continue to live and rebuild without our help. Upon visiting "The Viet Nam Veterans Memorial Wall" in 1994, I had to reflect upon my own fortune. I was assigned in Germany during the Viet Nam conflict, and while many of my friends were levied to Nam from Europe, the action stopped when I was "senior" enlisted man at my site. I often wonder why I was passed over....

I have friends who fought, were wounded, returned home, and have made good lives for themselves and their families. They witnessed atrocities, participated in the needless slaughter of humans (ours and theirs) but still came home and have prevailed over the horrors that haunt them.

My concern is for those who continue to use Viet Nam as an excuse for us to feel sorry for them. I know that my close friends who served in Viet Nam are repulsed by the “scum" who feed on our society with the excuse "I'm a Viet Nam veteran. You owe me...".

The enemy no longer exists. It's not society that is at fault. It’s not the American people who are at fault. If you want to put the blame for Viet Nam and what it did to Americans on a person, then blame Lyndon B Johnson. It was his fault the war went as far as it did. He got fat and rich from it (Lady Bird owned Brown and Root, the only company contracted to work in Viet Nam).

I want to know more about the people of Viet Nam, like the life-story contributed by Michael Little. This war has made us a better people for having been involved, even though I say that it is a lesson that we should not have been forced to learn.

My hat goes off to the Webmaster of this site, and to folks like Michael Little, who contribute enriching articles about their life in Nam.

Earl "Skeet" Williams
