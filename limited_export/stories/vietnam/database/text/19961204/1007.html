I was only a boy when the Vietnam "conflict" War ended. I don't have any memories of what happened. When I was in high school, we spent 1, maybe 2 hours on the Vietnam War. I didn't know anything about it. I went to college, and was probably considered somewhat of a liberal leaning far left. War is stupid, War is meaningless, We killed babies so our country could have bragging rights, How could anybody in their right minds do this? Then came my wakeup call. A man I call "uncle Don". He was their in Vietnam, he was in the middle of it all. He killed a baby, at least he says it was his fault. He was a tormented man, like many Vietnam Vets. But I didn't understand him or why he was the way he was. I listened to him, I cried with him. He is a Native American, who has gone back to his traditional ways. He lives a lot better now. He has confronted his past and acknowledged it. Seeing the war through his eyes, I have learned about the Vietnam War, I still don't like it, but I no longer blame the soldiers. "Akicita Wicasa", that is what I call my "uncle" now. That is warrior in Dakota Sioux. He is more than a warrior now, he is my uncle, he is my friend, he watches over his family and his friends. He takes care of them, that is what a warrior does. He will always be a warrior. He will always be my "uncle". To all of the "Akicita Wicasa", Thank You for doing what you thought was right. Thank You for making it possible for my son to grow up. Pidama, Wopida, Mitakuye Oyasin. Thank you, Thank you very much, All my Relatives.
