I have nothing of real significance to say really, just some thoughts and feelings.. I learned about the Vietnam war at a fairly young age, at school. We watched documentaries, read real letters written from soldiers who fought at the time. It bought me close to tears then, the sadness and horror that these young men felt, not much older than I. Years later I studied war poetry and the horrors were bought forth in my mind once again. Last year I visited the Wall, and even though I did not know anyone personally who was in the war, I looked at this monument to the dead and felt connected to these people, as one human being to another, and cried, not only for these people, but selfishly, feeling lucky to be alive and breathing, to have a family who love me and are still there for me, and I went away, feeling that I need to live life to the full, because one day it might all be gone. After reading a lot of the comments on this site, I pray all these people can find peace in their hearts, one day.

Ritu
1975
London, England
