To all who read this. First i would like to think all the vets who gave their lifes to keep this country free.

I am a brother of a vietnam vet. My brother died 4 yrs ago this april.

My brother didnt talk about the war, but you could see it in his eyes what it did to him. My brother was 17 yrs old when he joined the army. When he came home from boot camp i was so happy to see him. But when he left to go to nam, i lost a part of him. It was good to hear from him, when he could write us,are when he had time.

Larry Dean is my brother, he has told me about the lions,tigers,snakes,leaches, that he seen are woke up with in his sleeping bag when he had one. He has told me about talking to a buddy next to him and when he turned around the buddy was dead. He has told me about some of the units in was in,and what they did. But there is alot he did not talk about,and i did not ask. I had this respect for him. Larry has told me the metals really didnt mean much to him as much as his friends and buddys have meant to him, this i understood as i got older. I write this letter to you Larry, to say thank you for all u did for this country and me. I will always miss you. Love your baby brother michael

Mike Dean
1958
