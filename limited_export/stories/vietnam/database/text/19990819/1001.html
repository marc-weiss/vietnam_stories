Vietnam to me showed that a person has to be part of something bigger than himself. Like everybody else in my 'generation', I was not crazy about fighting a rich man's war, or a white's man war. To me, defining part of why the US was fighting in Vietnam is happened about 2 am outside Da Nang. The North blew the hell out of an evacaution hopsital. It was full of wounded men from both sides. That showed me why we were there. That made everything come into focus. Every generation has to pay in blood for freedom. We are only standing on the backs of those who paid it before us. Anybody who does not agree to that, better dam well take a look at what he thinks a man is.

Joe Rich
1951
Dothan Ala
