Lee's Story about Vietnam, where he Should Have Gone.....but didnot!

...it's not as if because I was not in Vietnam, I was not affected...it has made all the difference in the World! It affected my values to the point I finally became a good Christian the way my mother and my father had wanted me to, only I had felt previously that I had to show up their moral cowardice over Vietnam, the lack of courage and will to defend their children that they had brought into the world and given almost every advantage, EXCEPT the advantage of supporting them in their just decisions against the War in Vietnam. I feel partly that they were hypocrites for this reason...it affected my livelihood in that I could no longer work for the military- industrial complex, but today I support myself by being a writer) as well as joining the Catholic Charismatic Renewal in order to be accepted and to find reconciliation with working class older Americans, by being friendly with all of them whether or not they are members of the Catholic Workers or not... but I am very friendly disposed toward the Catholic Worker philosophy, though not a Catholic Worker...personally. I am trying to get married now, so I can't do that, else my girlfriend would never have me, but I feel that a partial answer to the working class people of the Forties that I rejected would be to treasure the Catholic Worker philosphy on the part of all working class people... and thus maybe that represents my would-be reconcilation with my Catholic working class uncle Joseph, who didn't like 'hippies' (not ACTIVISTS! Hippies! Egad!) Anything I can do to become reconciled to the working class of this country and be accepted by them, now, is not enough for me to contemplate...but I must do it! Nonetheless, I still have repressed anger toward my parents, one who is dead, the other sick a lot, so I can't express it at all, but it kind of bothers me....Lee C. W. Hutchison II.

Lee C. W. Hutchison II.
1942
Washington, D. C.
