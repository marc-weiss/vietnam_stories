A TIME TO HEAL By Jan C. Scruggs, Esq.

"...let us strive on to finish the work we are in ... to bind up the nation's wounds." ABRAHAM LINCOLN, Second Inaugural Address.

No event with the exception of the War Between The States so divided America as did Vietnam. Massive protests and a bitter national debate raged during America's longest military engagement. The very unity that preserves us as a free nation was torn asunder as that war dragged on. The entire affair ended ingloriously in 1975 as the world watched U.S. Marines rescue Americans from atop the Saigon Embassy.

The war's tragic consequences are well known. Over 58,000 Americans died and 300,000 were injured. No yellow ribbons greeted the young soldiers who fought the unpopular war in Vietnam.

I began the effort to create The Wall in 1979. Dedication was in November 1982. "The Wall" is now the most visited Memorial in Washington D.C. and is internationally recognized as an American architectural triumph thanks to the efforts of Maya Lin, an Asian American woman who designed the memorial while a college student. The graceful wall of names has helped the nation remember and reflect upon the difficult years of Vietnam. Those who fought the war and those who fought against the war have a Memorial that has helped to bridge a wide gulf.

Few who went to Vietnam came back the same and, I suspect that for most veterans, the changes have been useful. Vietnam gave young people an opportunity to experience life and death and to see the best and the worst of human condition.

While the war hurt my faith in the 1970's leadership, I have a better understanding of the strength of its people. Living in bunkers and shedding blood with people of all racial and ethnic groups is something that only those who go to war can have, especially in this increasingly balkanized and segregated society. I, for one, feel richer as a human being for the experience. Some of those who missed the experience of fighting for a society which they publicly proclaim is the world's best include Bill Clinton and Newt Gingrich. Had they gone through the ordeal of Vietnam would they be better leaders? You bet!

Jan C. Scruggs, Esq. led the effort to create the Vietnam Veterans Memorial
