The question I have been wrestling with since the fall of the Berlin Wall and the end of Communism, is that: if the West, and in particular, the United States, won the Cold War, doesn't it beg the question that Vietnam was part of that war. So? People who fought in that war are to be considered warriors in the defense of liberty; what of the folks who protested against the War. Their heart was in the right place. They fought for what they believed was true, but how do protestors and those who left the United States for Canada become anything less than traitors. I wish someone could tell me the answer to this question. People might say that these people did what they thought was right, but haven't those who protested against the war or left the country, suddenly lost statue--since the Cold War has come to a successful conclusion?

Vernon Brown
1944
Greenville, SC
