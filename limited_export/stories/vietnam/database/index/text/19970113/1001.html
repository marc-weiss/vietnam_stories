The Wall

This simple place etched with graffiti naming the dead was designed by the daughter of a woman I know who tried with her husband to keep their child safe from political opinions and controversy not knowing the young woman would be the architect of hatred frustration envy

and a healing blackness

-------

To Tell You The Truth,

the sky was bluer than kitten eyes. The clouds whiter than new Kotex. Night jet take offs using afterburner were angels' matches burning a spotlight bright moon. Choppers sounded like bass guitars. Booze and women were cheap, good and transitory, negatory, in the morning. Life was exciting as the drop before your chute opens, intense as the opening shock. When it wasn't boring you stone stiff. You could play God, kill like insecticide and get decorated and paid for it. Cordite smelled better than fresh pussy.

If you had the right attitude, and didn't give a damn, it was good practice and a high taller then sex, dope and salvation combined If. And I still miss it.

Horace Coleman--Vietnam "class of `67" born 4 may 43 6551 Warner Ave.; #35 Huntington Beach, CA 92647
