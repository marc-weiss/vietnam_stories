The enclosed “essay” is something I wrote for my High School literary magazine in 1970, when I was 16 years old. It describes what I then conceived a typical American soldier might experience on patrol in the jungles of Vietnam.

To those of us who grew up with the war, it made a deep and lasting impression. Like many young Americans of that era, growing up in the 50's and 60's, television was reality. And as we knew, Vietnam was a “television war,” in that it came directly to our living rooms in a way no other war has, before or since, night after night (after night).


ESSAY ON EXISTENCE

1971


The sun looked down on my vulnerable body and gave a shrill, merciless laugh. I paid him no mind, even though every bone of my tortured frame cried out in defiance of his killing heat. I trudged on. The underbrush twisted and writhed beneath my feet as if screaming in protest of my alien presence. The steaming swamps reeked of death and decay as my eyes futilely scanned the trees for my nonexistent enemy--
