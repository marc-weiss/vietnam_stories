Dear Dr. Spatz,

None of HAD to go to 'Nam? I beg your pardon! I enlisted in 1965, I was sent to Okinawa six months later. On Oki I sought the "HELP" of a service Psychiatrist for Gender Dysphoria. After a year of therapy he helped - he had me transfered to the 'Nam as a cure! Big choice. I experienced that horror - learned I could kill, learned I could hate. You and many others did what you considered right and I honor that but let's NOT FORGET the sacrifices made over there; the lives wasted, the stolen youth and innocense - the purity - of all who served. It is we who HAD to be there, we who feel guilty about coming back while other's did not, who bear the RAGE, the heart-wrenching pain of having to remember, of having night- mares, the physical quaking and flood of tears.

Bien Dien Province, RVN 3/68 - 7/69 http://www.accesscom/~dulcinea/Intro.html
