A mere rock, unearthed, cleaned, cut and sold for profet. But it posesses an awesome power to heal, to remember. No longer is it a stone, void of life. It has become life, while reminding us all of death. There's nothing divine or supernatural at work here. No outside of unseen forces exist. Its power exist only in the people who come to view it. An inorganicm unfeeling monument, a monument to heroes. Heroes who will never see it majesticaly set off against the clear blue sky. These heroes won't come home. Husbands, sons, brothers, fathers and friends. They are all names carved upon a stone, listed in order of death, and bearing no dates, This stone is a fortress made from war, polished almost mirror like, and reflecting a world ( our world ) we cannot or will not see. It's cold, unfeeling and void of human emotion. It is silent, it is stone, but it has a memory. A memory inposed upon it by those who created it. Those who carved the names, forgot the dates,and by those who come. In droves they come, like cattle called to feed. Families, friends and the curious. They come to unlock the pain and the wall it seems is the only key. With crayons and paper they will take their piece of the wall home. A touch somehow comforts the lonely. Fingers gently run over letters that alone have no meaning, but combined they are meaning. This stone shaped into a wall and dedicated a monument, rest. At twilight ( the time where it is neither day nor night ) it weeps. Kissed with dew a million tiny drops are wiped away. Momentos that are left carefully and with love, are picked up, bagged and carted away. The people are gone, but the burdens they carried remain. As for the wall, it to remains. It is a reminder of things we must not forget. Nor must we forget the 58,156 burdens, carved in stone, and carried by the wall.
