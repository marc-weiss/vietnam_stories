Most of us went over there as kids, we came back older than our years. Those of us that were lucky enough to make it back. Instead of going out on dates we were going out on patrols. Instead of watching war in the movies or on television we were in the war, we were the ones doing the killing and being killed. We left a war zone and 24 hours later we were back in the world and were expected to leave the war behind in the span of one day. we were met with the angry crys of baby killer and the spit in the face. No welcome home except from family. The vast majority of us was able to cope with the world we returned to, but a lot didn't. We were able to contribute to society. We were able to work and raise families. Today we face a much more understanding America. Most of us did nothing but get drafted and obeyed the law of our land. We were not criminials. Today we hold our heads a little higher. Maybe it is to hide the tear that is still in our hearts.

Robert Foster
1942
Rogersville Tn, USA
