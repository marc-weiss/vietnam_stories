I think the Vietnam experience had a profound effect on me as a young boy. During the late sixties and early seventies, I remember vividly the daily news from southeast Asia. The grim footage of that grisly war was constantly in our consciousness. My parents opposed the war, but were careful to to be clear about their support of the boys in the field. They just didn't want any more dead. After becoming an adult, I became friends with many veterans. Most have permanent psychological damage that they cope with on a daily basis. Of course others have permanent physical disabilities as well. What has been related to me is the sense that no one wants to hear about their experience, and they are silent. What experiences my friends have recounted have been stories of atrocities beyond human comprehension. We wonder why this war just wont go away. It should not go away. This war continues in the soul of this country. We are still haunted by it; the images, the psyches of the traumatized vets, and the denial in our history books all attest to the persistent nature of Vietnam. It is our most recent great American tragedy. So many lives were damaged and so many remain damaged.

Jeff Jumisko
1965
Seattle, WA
