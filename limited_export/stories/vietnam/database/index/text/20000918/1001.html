Long ago, at a time when there was little news about the conflict in Vietnam, I fell in love for the first time in my life. The year was 1965 and I was barely 18 years old. The object of my affection was a Navy FMF Corpsman. He was in school ("A"..."B".......can't remember) at Bethesda Naval Hospital, not far from Washington, DC.

I had little idea of what being a corpsman meant, even as my corpsman left for Vietnam. Nothing much was being said in the media about America's growing involvement there. All I understood at the time was that my corpsman was going to Vietnam and it would be a year before we would see each other again. I couldn't envision my corpsman being shot at, wounded or killed. I knew nothing about mortors..........mines......booby traps.........or punji stakes. I knew nothing about guerilla warfare.

Then came the news that my corpsman had been wounded....His leg I think. They sent him to a hospital in Yokuska, Japan. We talked to each other by phone several times while he was there. One day though, I called and the person I spoke to said that my corpsman was not on the patient lists or the duty rosters. I learned that IF my corpsman's wounds had healed and he was not disabled, he would have been sent back to his unit in Nam to finish out his tour of duty there. If the wound were more severe than that, he would have been sent back to the states.I tried so hard to find him. All my letters to him were returned unopened and I didn't know where in the states he might be, or even if he was in the states at all. I didn't know if he was alive......or dead. But, for me, he ceased to exist, because I never heard from him again. Years later, I did learn that he had called a co-worker at my former place of employment (Rigg's Bank in DC.)

After a while I gave up, started dating again and married another man. Also in the Navy...a deep sea diver. We will celebrate our 34th anniversary in January. My husband went to Vietnam too. He went to Cam Rahn Bay for a year. I knew that since he was not in combat, that he would be fairly safe.......I hoped so anyway. He missed the birth of his second child, born January 1, 1971, by just three days.

So.....why am I writing about things that happened over 30 years ago? I have two reasons really.

Reason #1: When my corpsman left for Vietnam, I gave him an ID bracelet. On the back I had engraved, " I'll be waiting, Tiger". Obviously, I did not wait.

I want him to know...should he happen to read this, that I so regret not being there to say "Welcome Home". I want to tell him now, even though it is 30+ years after the fact, that he, along with my husband and all those who served in Vietnam....are all bonafide heroes to me. I want to tell him how sorry I am that he endured so much pain, physically and emotionaly, because he chose to serve his country. My husband and I visited the Traveling Wall several years ago. There were vets there who had a roster of all the names on the wall. My corpsman was not listed as one of them. For that, I am truly grateful. He survived Vietnam. It is my most fervent prayer that he also survived the emotional wounds that he probably brought back to the states with him. I have lived a contented life since then....I hope he has, too.

Reason #2? Simply because..........I can. Because of the internet, I can finally do what I have longed to do for many years now. At last I have the means to thank our veterans for giving so very much, above and far beyond what would seem reasonable. Please forgive me for not understanding....then... as I should have, just how courageous you all were. Please accept my gratitude and a very belated....."Welcome Home!"
