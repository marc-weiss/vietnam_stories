TEMPERATURES

I arrived In-country at Cam Rahn Bay on 15 March 1969 it was about 90 degrees of breath-arresting heat. Thirty years later, when I touched THE WALL it was cold as ICE,Temperature 10 below, a flash of cold shoot up my arm to my shoulder. But when I walked out and up away from the wall I felt a COOL BREEZE, Temperature: CLEANZING...

submitted by SMSGT (USAF Retired) Larry T. Fryske

1st tour: 15 March 1969 Nha Trang AB then Toy Hua AB left Vietnam 15 November 1969. Mechanic, 9th SOS (Special Operations Squadron) C-47's 2nd tour: 21 December 1970 Phu Cat AB left 15 November 1971, Mechanic, 361st TEWS (Tactical Electronic Warfare Squadron) EC-47's (Electronic Goones) I since completed my college degree at Louisana Tech (Class of 84 on base program Barksdale AFB La.) Graduated after going 9 years part time I have worked at Lockheed Martin for over 11 years. Married 28 years 4 children and 3 grandchildren. I was discharged medically with Post Tramatic Stress Disorder, since my thearpy, I have never (thank GOD) had another nightmare, flashback or waking up screaming in cold sweats. But from Nov 71 until Nov 84 I lived with those symtoms almost daily. Our Government needs to get the Vets the medical attention they need. It works. Total service: 4 years U.S. Navy, 18 years U.S. Air Force. Epilog: Vietnam taught me to live each and every day as if it were my last on earth and thank GOD for surviving two tours to see my grandchildren and see the seasons pass with each year. I'm one of the lucky ones that returned, But I try and not recall those tours. The temperature was too unbearable (emotionaly)...
