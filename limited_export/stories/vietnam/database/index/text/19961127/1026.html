I don't associate myself much with the war externally unless I can tell a funny story(I have plenty). I guess we remember the good stuff and leave behind the bad. I fear discussing it (the war)as I am somtimes afraid I'll remember somthing that will bother me in the present and frankly I do not want to give it much attention or time. I have become a "softy" since my return to the states. I abhor violence and destruction (imagine that!). I hate hate and hate nothing else. One of the worst things I do is that for some reason I cannot control myself when I hear the national anthem, I have to walk away and cry. It's very embarrassing and it doesn't always fit the moment (baseball games). I hate to admit it but it does feel good for some reason. I really enjoy meeting Vietnamese people who have migrated to the US. I like to talk to them about their country as it is one of the most beautiful places my eyes have ever seen. I've learned that we Vets really do not fit the stereotype. There are many of us on the left and the right but most of us are somewhere in the middle and we are rather normal. My son was born on veterans day and I like to think that he is my reward for going. I do not regret one minute I spent there. Politically I ignore it. It was a major adventure in my life that has a beginning and an end. I do not allow it to ever consume me as the world is full of adventures and I intend to 'venture to my last breath. I really don't have much time to give thought to it. I'm doing great, so is my family and I love being in America. What else can I say.

Jerry Horton
1951
