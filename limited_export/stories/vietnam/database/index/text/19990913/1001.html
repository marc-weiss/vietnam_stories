I just happened across this site this evening and it struck chords inside me that I've needed a long time... My father served in vietnam and was proud to have done so. However, as a kid I never knew what Vietnam was or what horror was endured over there. you see, my father never spoke of the war. Not even to my mom who saved all of his letters home and gave them to us after his death 3 years ago. I know he did say that he believed in what he did and he would love for us kids to serve if we wished to. After reading the letters I am reminded of when my brother and I would play "war" in the yard with dads old army clothes he kept way back in the closet. We would even pretend to award medals to eachother with pins and stars and things that dad kept in a box inside his big green bag. He used to watch us play in the uniform he never dragged out yet always hung onto and never said a word. About a year before he died I was in college and working in a psychology class on post-traumatic stress and so forth when I really started to get what I thought was an understanding of the war and the horror most endured. I had wanted to ask dad about his experiences because I had found it fascinating that he was involved there. I assumed he didnt see much fighting because he never spoke of bad things and he watched platoon with me without saying much more than it was a film that had a lot of similarities. Dad was in some heavy action from time to time and he never wanted to let us kids into that part of his life. I now know from others a few stories of bad times as well as some humorous moments he and his buddies had and I only wish we could have shared our love and pride for the job he did. I feel bad wondering what he thought watching his sons play with medals and a uniform he wore in combat. I wish for any father out there to know that I would have loved find the part of my dad that was distant about that time and I am so proud to have a father that served in nam and I wish he could have felt my love and respect for him as I read other letters on this site. You are all amazeing people and I hope you understand how full of respect and love your children feel for you and the jobs you preformed. Thank you for this site....It makes me feel a little closer to my dad.

Andy Lucas
1972
