Generations, You must wonder what I have to do ,even remotely with Vietnam war?! Well in fact not more than memories from the news when I was young. My reaction is not related to the place of action. It is related to the action; WAR! And how I am different now as a result of war! How exactly war affected my life considerably I will not go into here. But I want to give some facts about what happened, facts about the point of entry where the stone hit the surface of the water and caused rimples, rimples in the life surface as a third generation war-connected person, that I am. As some people here in Holland are unlawful children of American and Canadian liberators, my mother is a unlawful daughter of a relation that her mother had with a high ranked officer, a dutchman, but working on the wrong side as we say here in Holland, yes working for the Germans during the 2nd World War! So when my mother was six years old and Holland was flooded by Noth Americans, liberating Holland ,may 1945, there was no liberation for her.Instead toghether with her mother and brother she was put in a concentration camp run by angry Dutch people, where she was mistreated and abused badly, not understanding what and why these things where happening. During her growing up as young girl she carried a stigma. The stigma of being the child of parents who where wrong in the war. A war that she had nothing to do with , but she was carrying guilt for, all her life. Nobody could be trusted. Because people might find out who where her parents. Up till now I don't know myself. The damage to a young soul in this conditions are considerable as you understand. When my mother was twenty she married my father and was already pregnant from me . Three years later my sister was born. My mother never really managed anymore to trust anybody ever in the whole world including her children .For several years I'm not in contact with her,mainly because of her distrust . Her abusive behavior during my youth have roots in her youth that was misformed by war! That is why a war is not stopped when the guns are stored away. The real war ends in me, if I am able to, if not I will pass it on to the next generation, in which some unaware person thinks the enemy is outside and eventually will pick up a gun again. We need to understand war and therefore to understand ourself. Hoped to have made a small contrabution, eventough it was not about Vietnam. (feel free to react) Sjoerd/Amsterdam
