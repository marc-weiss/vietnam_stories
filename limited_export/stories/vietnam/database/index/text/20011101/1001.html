I was only in Viet Nam through my ex-husbands letters. I was 17 & we met right before he was shipped out @ in San Francisco near where I lived. We new each other briefly as did many others but fell in love, he needed to have a girl back home and I needed him as much.

I wrote to John every day and sent packages to him every week and always put in extra goodies for his buddies that didn't have anyone back home. John was a medic in the 4the Infintry Devision. After him being gone for alomst a year we met in Hawaii for his A & R which is when we got married in May 0f 69. I remembered how dirty he was and how he stayed in the shower for almost an hour. We didn't know each well except throuh our letters but I knew I would love him forever. After retuning from Hawaii to the jungles of Veit Nam he spent another year and a half over there until he was released to come home.

The man I married and the one who came home were not one of the same. We struggled through 8 years of marriage, his nightmares, yelling and always keeping me @ a distance, until one day he came home packed up and vowed never to return. We've been divorced for over 26 years but my heart is still with him.

John would never admit there was a problem although I experienced his nightmares as well back then, he only wanted out just as he did in Viet Nam. I don't think any of us envolved with Vets ever forget either. I'm now 51 and still unmarried after 26 years because the soldier I loved never came back emotionally. It was an awful war and we all lost out, John, myself and our daughter who is not 31. Where have the years gone for all of us baby boomers, and what the heck happened to all of our hope and dreams?

I guess we're all still hurting just in different ways. I don't know if I served any purpose, but I remember John's mother once saying she knows he came back because Everyday I wrote to her son and gave him a reason to want to come home. Maybe my only purose back then was just to help one more soldier make it back alive. Maybe it wasen't suppose to be forever!

So for all you Soldiers who came home, please know many or us have not forgotton you or the sacrifice you make for our country! Those of us that were connected in one way of another still care! May God bless each and everyone who may read this, I'm a Christian now and I know God can heal your wounds inside and out. God is still healing my heart and he can help heal yours too. Please read JOHN 3-16.......... Sincerely, Sherry Boyd
