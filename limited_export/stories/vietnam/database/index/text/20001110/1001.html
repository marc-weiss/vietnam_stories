41MY father moved to this country in 1966 and enlisted in the Marine Corp shortly thereafter.He believed that since this would be his new home he should show his patronage and fight for his country.He wasn't aware of just what a fight that would be.He was assigned to the 1st Marine Div.11th Engineers Motor Transport in Dong Ha.When he came home to his new country he was spat on and called a baby killer while carrying the colors in a local parade.That was the beginning of his silence.I was born in 1972,and it wasn't until I was 13 that I can say that I heard my dad say I Love You.It was hard growing up with a father that was angry and frustrated with the world. MY memories of him when I was young weren't pleasant,until he became involved with a local Vet's group.He became a totally different person.He showered our family with love,love he hadn't known how to show for many years.He learned that what he and many others had sacrificed was just and he realized he wasn't alone in his fight with the "ghosts".As a result of his involvement with the Meriden VietNam Veterans Assoc.he has started to become whole again.I wish to thank the men who gave me my daddy back and for my freedom as an American.God Bless You All!

Catherine McDonald-Bell
1972
Cape Cod Mass.USA
