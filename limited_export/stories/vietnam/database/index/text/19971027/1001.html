As a college student I watched the war on television. I marched in protest of the images of horror I saw. I questioned a world where violence settled affairs. I felt powerless to stop the madness. I decided to be an artist. I vowed I would not destroy anything as I set off to explore the adult world. I could find no niche. I carved my own. Out of the ashes I became a dancer, a musician, a storyteller. The war blasted me tumultuously toward a peace path.

Heather Forest
1948
Huntington Station, NY USA
