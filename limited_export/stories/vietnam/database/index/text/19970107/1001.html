Viet Nam....the name of a city that brings much pain in many forms. No, I was not part of its tumultuous history, but it did have a great impact on my life.....and still does on a daily basis. I am the wife of an American Vet who served in the U.S. Army. He was a "boy", as were most during the time of the war that was never really declared as such and had to "grow up" so quickly to responsibilities most of us try to avoid all of our lives as it is painful in any form. It is the loss of life, whether personally involved or not. My husband was maybe 20 years old when he went to bed one night with two legs and before morning had an injury due to a surprise attack on his group of young soldiers he was part of. Most of those boys died that night and my husband was thought to be among the dead when he was accidently found the next morning. This story has perhaps more detail than is really necesssary right now, but Viet Nam made a major impact on his life and brings about a certain bitterness at moments, as due to the loss of his leg to a senseless war in many ways, he has faced many limitations in more areas than I could name right now.

Viet Nam also reminds me of losing one of my very best friends....Robert Green of Waco, Tx. He was probably not much more than eighteen years old when he had to go. I remember so well the night before he had to go to Dallas to catch the bus that would take him to a destiny he feared.....death in a place that was not familiar, or friendly. He talked with me all night long; actually he cried all night long repeating over and over how afraid he was and that he knew he was going to die. I remember telling him that there was no way he would die. He had too much gong for him. He was smart, funny, a good student, very involved in school functions. He was popular and always laughing. I can still see his smile in my mind today and my heart grieves for the loss of this special friend to my life. We never had the pressure in our relationship of highschool romance...other than his with "whomeve." We were just great, great friends. I feel so honored to have known him and if I could only see him again, which I will not unless it is in Heaven someday, I would give him the biggest and warmest" bear-hug."

Viet Nam altered so many lives in so many ways and I feel it has to bring much heartache to familes and friends alike in both the U.S. and Viet Nam as well. The loss of life through death and through the POW encapturement has had a profound impact that will last through more generations than many may think. How are "we" different today as a result of the Viet Nam era???? The list I read on the opposite side of this screen mentions values, relationships, children, parents, politics, livlihood, attitude towards life or death, and philosophy of life. I don't think there is enough space or time for me to include the responses in the detail I could in each, but all is affected I think for each one who is or was part of that era, be it family, friend, participant, or just someone that was there in that time period.

Viet Nam.....just the mention of it is profound in itself......

Nancy Nolan
1949
Waco, Tx. (USA)
