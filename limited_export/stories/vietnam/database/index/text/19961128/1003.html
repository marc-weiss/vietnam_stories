Right place, right time. As I turn 36 years of age next month, I realize I grew up in an era of great fortune... too young to know first hand the agonizing horror of Vietnam, and too old to know the glorious horror of the Gulf War. My sense of appreciation for those who served is profound for there, but for the grace of God, go I.

My father flew Air Force transport planes across the pacific during the 1960's and early 1970's. I remember stories about how he was hauling supplies to the troops in Vietnam to help win the war. I also remember him bringing to me various toys and other gifts when he arrived home after each trip. In later years I learned that he had other return cargo as well... the caskets of those being brought home from Vietnam.

In October 1996 I visited the Wall for the first time... accompanied by my four year old son. Try explaining to a four year old the meaning, the pain, and the sacrifice represented by the Wall. Yet, without much help from me, he seemed to understand the overpowering sadness of the place. He did not ask "why did these people have to die?" But he saw his dad cry for the first time. Later that day he saw his dad cry for the second time... as we walked amongst the AIDS Quilt being displayed in memory of another 50,000+ dead Americans. I will pray for the families of those who grieve. I will pray for a brighter future for my son.

Michael Lawell
1960
Washington State
