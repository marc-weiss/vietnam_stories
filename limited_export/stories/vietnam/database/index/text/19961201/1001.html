WHEN I ARRIVED AT TRAVIS, AIR FORCE BASE IN JULY, 1970, I WAS GREETED BY AN ANGRY CROWD. THEY SCREAMED "BABY KILLER" AND OTHER EQUALLY DAMAGING PROFANITIES. THEY EVEN SPIT ON ME. I GUESS I HAD WATCHED TOO MANY PATRIOTIC MOVIES, WHILE GROWING UP, AND EXPECTED BANDS, JOYOUS CROWDS OF WELL WISHERS, AND PARADES. NONE OF THIS WOULD COME FOR MANY, MANY YEARS. HUGS AND HAND CLASPS ACCOMPANIED BY A SINCERE "WELCOME HOME!" FROM SOLITARY COMRADES WOULD HAVE TO SUFFICE. THIS IS WHY I WAS PROUD TO BE ASKED TO CHAIR THE SUSSEX COUNTY, NEW JERSEY WELCOME HOME PARADE FOR OUR DESERT STORM VETERANS. IT WAS AND WILL ALWAYS BE MY MOTIVATION TO WELCOME HOME ALL VETERANS OF ANY AND ALL WARS. LET US NEVER FORGET ANYONE WHO SERVES THE DEFENSE OF FREEDOM. LET IT NOT MATTER THE LENGTH OF THEIR CAMPAIGN NOR THE "POLITICAL CORECTNESS" OF THEIR EFFORTS. REMEMBER THAT EVEN A MOMENT IN "HELL" BURNS JUST AS HOT AS AN ETERNITY,REGARDLESS OF THE MOTIVATION.

WELCOME HOME!!!!

JOHN C. GRATTAN
1949
