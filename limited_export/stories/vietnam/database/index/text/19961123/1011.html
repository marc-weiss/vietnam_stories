I grew up in Saigon, in a two-story villa on Phan din Phung, in a house with bars on its windows. My youngest sister was born there; Saigon was our childhood. When families were evacuated from Vietnam, my father sent us to Bangkok, where I eventually graduated from high school. My father was in Vietnam throughout most of the war; my sister is a Vietnam- era vet; my husband a former Vietnam Marine. That explains much of my interest in Vietnam, but my work with women's writing on Vietnam, specifically, is due to the absence of poetry available to me when I wanted to write my sister's story, my father's story, my husband's stories, through the eyes of a sister, of a daughter, of a wife. But I could find no such stories. The anthology is my attempt to make sure that others can find--and write--their stories. You are welcome to use the poem and any portion of the letter.

Thank you for editing such a critical project. Perhaps we can share our experiences and find healing, finally.
