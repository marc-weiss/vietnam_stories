I had a pivotal point since my time in the Vietnam War. I was not in Vietnam. I was a stayed-in-the-USA, as a trademarked friend would say. By and large, they didn't send women to Vietnam unless they were nurses. I'm not, so I wasn't. I was on the homefront, a term I have never heard anyone else use about this war. Do they not realize there was one? Or was I the sole person on it?

Anyway, I was in, as the provost of my university said, an "unfortunate" job situation. I was ordered to go and get sprayed for static cling. As they led me away, or maybe when I was sprayed, I had severe flashbacks...I thought somehow I had been taken POW. I couldn't figure out the connection for some time, but I had stumbled onto something once that I wasn't supposed to, and I had been captured. So in an instant I go from, well, I'm basically fine, just a mild case of PTSD, to what the f---, over. For a year I burst into tears when anyone says anything at all about Vietnam...or when anything reminds me...and my boss after the "unfortunate" situation was always reminding me, having been there a couple of times himself. And I burst into tears when I have to tell anyone about this spraying, which is part of a legal situation, obviously. It is just convergent in my mind. And I had absolutely no idea for 25 or so years that this was so raw and powerful and just waiting to burst like an appendix.

Since that time, I have totally rearranged my life. It's not voluntary simplicity, more like mandatory simplicity now. But if I ever had any doubts as to whether I qualify to be a "real" Vietnam veteran, they were put to rest by this.

Mary Garvey
