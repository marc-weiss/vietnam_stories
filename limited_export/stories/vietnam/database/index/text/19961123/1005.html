In the Abattoir

"Mama, Mama," New York shrieked. I told him not to cry-

The Rabbi cuts the kosher throat in the abattoir.

Whirling blades beat flaming air as med-evacs arrived.

Saw-tooth blades sever bone in the abattoir.

An enemy's corpse crawled with fat, lazy flies.

Warm flesh hangs from steel hooks in the abattoir.

I pinned my unit patch to an unblinking eye.

Memories swim beneath my skin, get cut, filleted, wrapped in butchers paper, and weighed in the abattoir.
