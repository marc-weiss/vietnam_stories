I remember the deaths of John Kenendy, his brother Robert and the death of Martin Luther King, Jr.

Then I joined the Army in 1970. I didn't have to. I had a deferment through a federal job training program. But join I did. I beleived what we were doing in Vietnam was right and just. After all, we were trying to give a nation the chance at freedom we had long since come to expect and demand.

I spent 18 months in country. Two Christmas's, two birthdays and a lifetime. I was a crewchief with a charmed life. I was never shot down. The helicopters I took care of and the men that flew in them were not shot down. The rockets that "charlie" fired at us day or night destroyed miuch but never harmed me. some of those rockets did come close. My tour was nearly over when the charm ended. It was before the spring offensive of 1972. A friend and his pilot were shot down near Quang Tri. I knew him when I was a child. /we hadn't seen each other for almost ten years. Now we were in the same unit. I came home in July of 72. He did not. For over twenty years I had blocked out the man's name. I didn't want to remember him dying that way. Beating the flames from burning fuel trying to get out of his helicopter.

The years go by. I still see his pain. I still punish my self for not being able to help him. He lives on in my mind today. His courage to be a soldier is alive today in the soldiers of today. His name is on the wall with all the others that gave up their lives. Even today them people who protested the war forgot him then and now. For their actions only led to the needless deaths of men. To them we remind them of thier own weakness to stand for truth and justice as they claimed they did. I they were, the protestors would have sought out the men that sent us to Vietnam. Instead, we the soldiers would not have recieved the brunt of the hatred of the war.

Did the soldiers loose the war? I say no! The people of the US lost the war and we the soldier are still paying the price. My own family didn't know me when I had returned. After I had sobered up, I didn't know who I was. Today I still struggle with who I am and where I am going. I drove my first wife away and my second remains at my side. In the years between those women I found God. He to remains with me and continue to guide me towards an inner peace I lost over the jungles of Vietnam. My friend, I am sure is with God now. He awaits my joining him when it is my time.

Alex J Hernandez
1952
