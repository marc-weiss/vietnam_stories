I hope some of what I am writing helps some of you. What I have read has helped me. I dropped out of high school to join the Navy. I had to get away. I ended up in Vietnam on an LCM-8. You know, those boats you see dropping Marines off on the beach, except that after landing in Da Nang in March 1968 we rode the rivers like the Perfume River at Hue, and the Cau Viet river between Dong Ha and Quang Tri. We hauled helicopter fuel, dragged the river for mines, got ambushed, dealt with the NVA,. primarily, stuff like that. Have I dealt with my stuff enough? Probably not.

I have been through two marriages, several relationships, a lot of jobs, alcohol and drug abuse, a lot of self-destructive stuff. Today, I have almost 13 years of being clean and sober. I have had the job of my dreams, counseling men and women who suffer from ptsd, or trauma. Or I did...my own trauma got so bad I am being given a medical retirement, and perhaps I will get some C&P. I still have sleepless nights even with all the psychotropic medication, which I am grateful for. I still don't do relationships very well. But, almost everything is better than it used to be. I have friends who actually like having me around. I actually have women friends whom I have been able to keep due to NOT "whispering in their ear, and losing another friend." I am continually amazed by the spiritual path I am on. Watching Bill Moyer's "Genesis: A Living Conversation" was pure joy. What I am dealing with now is "Do I retire at 49 years of age? Or, do I get my clinical social worker license, and do the work I love? Reading someone else who wrote here about doing groups was instructive. Time will tell. God bless each of you, veteran, or non-veteran.
