Early in July 1967, my unit Co B 1/12 Bn First Air Cav Div. was operating the Bong Son Plains located on htte coast of Central South Vietnam.

During the cousre of one evening our battalion had surrounded a small hamlet and waited for dawn to break before we started to clear it. Standard operational procedure required that we call in an air strike if the enemy was suspected of being there. We had received small arms fire, and an air strike was called in. We watched in horror as one F-100 released it's payload of one 500 pound bomb. Instead of falling like a normal bomb it fell end over end and land about 25 meters in front of a friends foxhole that was dug in haste. Having seen where it landed we ran over to htte foxhole and found his weapon and helmet in total disaray. We started digging through the hole with our bare hands, some of us crying because we knew that we were only going to find pieces of him. To the rear of us was a small hootch had was sort of out of the way. Our friend came out of there buttoning his pants and asking what had happened, When we saw him we were in total surprise and dum founded. We didn't know whether to kiss him or kill him for the greive that he caused that day. It wasn't funny at the time but over hte yearss that dump (if you know what I mean) saved his life.
