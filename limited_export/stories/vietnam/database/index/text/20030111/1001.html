My name is Joseph A Francis I am a full blooded Mi'kmaq Indian from the Big Cove Indian Reservation in New Brunswick,Canada..I volunteered to go to vietnam..My family and I moved to Manchester,N.H. in 1960,at that time I was 14 years old and just getting started in amature boxing.I had boxed for about 5 years and done quite well at the sport winning the golden gloves title in two(2) different open classes plus a couple of other aau titles I had compiled a record of over 90 fight loosing just a handful and was on my way to to turning professionel,something that I always dreamed of since I was a kid in the 50's watching the friday and wednesday night fightson a small black and white t.v.. But all that was about to change when Viet nam started to show it's ugly face in the early 63.In august of 1966 I decided to for go a pro career in boxing and joined the United States Marine Corps.five and a half months later with my mos 0311(grunt) I found my self with F-co. 2nd. bn. 26th. Marines 3rd.Mar.Div smack dab in the middle of the I corp area in the thick of all the fighting that I had only been reading in the papers a scant few months prevous.My tour lasted 13 months 26 day's we had been on over 36 major operations all over the I corp area from danang to the DMZ.The one particular operation that comes to mind is the ones that you are wounded on or the one's you took alot of casualties.Operation Hickory was one of those operations.We were on that operation Just the other side of Con Thien when all hell broke lose a couple of weeks later when it was all said and done we were half the batallion that we use to be we lost a lot of good men that left a big empty hole that you can never fill again.looking where they use to stand leave's a big empty space..too many losses and not enough time to heal,just go onto the next operation and try to put time between you and what's happening all around you. A couple of months after that operatoin we went on operation Rush in the mountains just west of Phu Bai I was walking point and came across an enemy position that was set up in an L shaped ambushI spoted them almost too late so I had to open up on them. before they hit us with the element of surprise.All hell broke loose, they opened on me from all directions with automatic weapons fire and hand grenades. I was the only one that was out in the open that they were trying to nail but I wouldn't stay still long enough for them to get a beed on me.I went to each of my men in my fire team and layed down cover fire for them so they could get out.I got shot in the back of the leg covering for the second one and the third one I got hit with a chi com grenade while covering for him, it knocked me ass over tee kettle.all I saw was a bright flash,the next second I'm picking myself off the groung with my fatiges just about blown off me and I was full of hot shrap metal. I was in a complete daze for a few seconds, I started to look arround for my weapon,I found it with the stock blown off and the barrol grip half gone but it still worked I picked it up and started to fire it again ,I think charlie was just as surprised as everyone else in my outfit to see that I had survived a direct hit from a hand grenade that landed right beside me I continued to lay down a heavy volium of fire I had about 9 rounds left in my last magazine when I shot my way out of there.. I got medevacked to daNang From ther I went to Guam Navel Hospital where I stayed For 3 months.Did not want to go back to stateside,so I volunteered to go back to Nam to Finish out My tour of four and a half more months. they didn't mind that there was a shortage of man power out in the feild, most units were seveerly under manned so it was back to the DMZ for a few more months.In late Feb.68 I rotated back to the world (states)and started to work out again to help me rehabilitate my self from the wounds that I had recieved in Nam.I got stationed at Quantico, Va. Marine Corps Base for the rest of the time that I had left in the corp being a small arms and squad tatics instructer, at the same time I worked hard at rehabing myself would hit the punching bag shadow box and run relentlesy,looking forward to the day when I would get out of the corps and continue my boxing and relize my dream to be a pro boxer.I had been out of the ring for about three and a half years befor I would get to relize that dream.One war and what seemed like a life time had passed it all seemed like a night merrish dream and I have yet to wake up.Sudenly I woke up and found myself back in the ring again winning but it wasen't the same alot had changed that fealing that I had before the corps was gone I won severl more titles including the New England Title and was selected to the nationel team.won the outstanding fighter award in that tournament but still there was something missing that I could not explain..I did turn pro relizing my life long dream.I had a total of 8 fights loosing one. I am married to the most wonderful woman in the world she put up with me for 29 Years and we had 9 children and are the proud grand parents of 15 grand children,we had 2 boys who went into the Marine Corps and we are very proud of them.What was missing was the 3 plus years that Tried to make up for but couldn't it all seemed like one big time warp and I was in the middle.what I did find out is that we can't make up for lost time time that we assumed we lost because it's not lost we just spent it on something else.As for in my case it was dam well worth it.and I would not change one line of it and would do it again in a heart beat.for my expierences in vietnam I was awarded the Bronze Star with the combat V for valor,the purple heart,cross of galantry,the combat action ribbin the presidentel unit citationand the other medels that go with being over there.the war affected me in alot of way's,more possitive then negative.it made me more keenly aware of life and all that affects it.My values are strong my relationship with my family is very good we are very close.My politics are more concervative.as is my aditude and philosophy.I still have alot of animosity for the people who protested against us and blameing us for the war,we were doing what our country asked of us nothing more.I am 100% service conected permanent and total for wounds recieved while in Vietnam I became a loner and kept primarly to my self,did not trust anyone.to this day it continues to have an affect on me in many ways the smell of fuil the sound of a chopera song from that erra.the smell of a hospital.fire works drives me crazzy.looking back on everything I don't think I missed a dam thing and we are not loosers we came out of that war with our heads held highand in a very nobel fashion Today I'm a boxing coach teaching the young kids how to box and talking to them on a personel note about their values and their future and always putting to them just how important their education is and how important it is to finish High school and go onto college and get that degree.I tell them boxing is great but theres life after boxing and we must prepair for it,there's no time like the present,boxing will provide you the disopline to see it through

Joseph A.Francis
1946
Manchester,N.H.U.S.A.
