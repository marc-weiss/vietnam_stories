I believe that the Vietnam Memorial is truly a monument or Monolith,as judged by each combat veteran who visits the "Wall",and must contend with the insanity of that war.My values,about the inherent goodness of people,evaporated after the war, into a belief that malice and destruction, were the core values of mankind.With that considerable change of heart, I proceeded to isolate myself from my family and friends. My children have a father,that they do not understand, that they cannot communicate with, in terms of fatherly love,and above all, they have no comprehension of what I, and many other 11B40's ,endured in order to survive. Tim O'Brien's book "The Things They Carried",illuminates the human issues of war so painfully and accurately.In an effort to eliminate the emotional pain of war,I proceeded to strive for excellence in my career,which meant longer hours,and that was perfect for me,since I could escape the memories of those I left behind,their families,and all of the battle damage to the living,whether it was physical or mental. Maya Lin built a perfect Temple of Doom,by which all Americans can pause and reflect on that period of history when average people,were called by their government and served,while others had the opportunity to avoid the draft.

Greg Warren
1945
Williston Park,N.Y.
