I am a retired Marine I served in Vietnam 1967-68. Last spring while in a resident PTSD treatment program in the VA Hosp. Vancouver Wa. I had the occasion to meet a Vietnamese girl that is going to school in the US (Portland) She describes herself as from the New Generation, born after April 1975. She seemed very well programed. I had painted a plastic model of the "Three Fighting Men" which included a Caucasion, Hispanic, and an African American. She asked me , "what is this" pointing her finger to the African American. I explained to her he also faught in Vietnam. She did not believe me and stated, there were "none of these in Vietnam". Myself I am Caucasion, but it did disturb me to think she had been programed as the "new generation" to not see the whole picture. She told me she had never seen any photos of "black people" in the Vietnamese Museums. She lived near Saigon, or as she calls it Ho Chi Mein City. Has any other Vets or anyone else had this same reaction from any of the New Generation? It's a shame they are not given the true picture of the Vietnam War.
