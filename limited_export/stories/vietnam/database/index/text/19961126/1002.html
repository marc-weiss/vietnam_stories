I didn't serve in VietNam... I was of the millions of women...the wives, sisters, mothers, girlfriends, classmates....an "army of women" who loved and too many times - lost the men they bid goodbye.

Some images and memories are more vivid than others...and decades later continue to torment or remind...like the issue of Life Magazine with page after pages of small black and white photographs of the KIA's for a given week. Small photographs that represented shattered lives..lost dreams..surrended time. Or, the first sight of the hammered metal coffin with wash tub handles that carried loved ones home...back to "the world"....a coffin with stenciled phrases "head this end" "nonviewable remains"...final declarations...the "secret socieies" that grew in small midwestern towns...widows and mothers meeting on the street and understanding/knowing the hollowness in one another- the heavy, sad weight loss. "Sisters in Grief". A family of unknown members formed...a sub-culture. Mothers, wives, sweethearts, daughters...Sisters in Grief.
