I served in the Vietnam War from February 14, 1968 to March 15th 1969. I was in the 15th Special Forces Division under the command of our superior advisors in South Vietnam. We handled sniper type warfare and sbatage. Also assassinations and tunnel patrol. Basically what that was is we went through tunnels that the VC had dug and cleaned them out. Just all the junk the Infantry Platoons didn't have time for. One day on the 29th of September 1968, a platoon that was ordered with us as we camped along the Dung Lai River just outside of the now famous Mai Lai town. The platoon went to patrol a suspected VC area 2 miles to North. After a whole day we noticed they never returned. Finally the next morning our sergent orderd about 10 of us to go look for this platoon of 15 soldiers. We walked for hours along the hot and very humid jungle. About 10 minutes later we found the entire platoon hung from trees and skinned alive.
