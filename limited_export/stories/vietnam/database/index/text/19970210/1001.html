I was born in 1962 and grew up not far from Fort Meade, Maryland. Behind the elementary school my brother and I attended was a spacious field bordered by woods. We called this place Mock Field and were told that training excercises for Ft Meade were held here. We would wander the field after school finding ammunition boxes, KP rations and empty shells smeared with sticky red Arundel clay. We would collect and trade these little treasures like baseball cards.

I remember seeing army men fighting on TV and not thinking too much about it because it was on TV and didn't seem very real.

My brother and I wore aluminum POW bracelets with blue stars on them. I remember taking mine off while watching officers disembarking from airplanes on television. I was disappointed because this was the last time I would ever wear it.

It wasn't until I got older before I realized that the men in that field had gone to Vietnam, and those army men on the screen had been fighting a real war, and each bracelet represented a human life.

Paul Schiebold
