I arrived in Viet Nam on Feb. 14,1967. A new marine, fresh from the states. Sence I was in Communications i was stationed at firebases. I had thoughts of transfering to a line unit after arriveing in country, but after talking to a friend , who was a grunt and had been in country for a month, his suggestion was to stay where i was at. " No need to get in the bush when I didn't have to." I spent 19 months in Nam. extented for 6 months in 1967, came home for 30 days just as the siege of khe Sanh had started, the 68 TET offinsive was under way when I returned. i finished my tour and " GOT THE HELL OUT OF THERE!!" This was a place where you could get killed! I spent two more years in the marine Corp and was discharged in Feb. of 1970. Comimg home was something i had been looking forward to. I found that I didn't talk to anyone , except other Nam Vets, about Viet Nam. After three failed marriages, I found myself with thoughts of takeing my own life. SSDD! I was damaged goods, some thing was wrong with me. After talking to my mother about my feelings, I called the Vet Center here in Tulsa, my responce to them was " My life SUCKS" I seem a councilor the nexted day, got involved with the other Vets, started 12-step programs and spent the next five years looking into myself for answers. I had never acknowledged myself for the job I did in Viet nam, forgave myself for not being a grunt ( not getting killed for my country) and corrected several character flews from being raised in an alcoholic family. today, well my life is pretty boreing, I like my job, like myself, pay my bills and play golf. Viet nam has left me with the exsperence of peoples ability to survive the worst possible conditions. My friendships from nam are the best of my life. Today I take pride in being a marine, Viet Nam Vet and the best person I can possibly be. Semfer Fi

andy L Morris
