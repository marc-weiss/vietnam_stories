I the early 1990's I was reading the Readers Digest Journal about Latinos in a B-52 Bomber that went down over Cambodia. I don't know the year this happened during the Vietnam War, but I'm researching all information on Hispanics in the military to write a script for a movie.

This story caught my attention because Latinos served in all branches of the military.

This during the Civil War of 1860-1865, over 2,500 Mexican served in the military. Most in the Confederate Army, but some made it all the way North and joined the Union Army because they opposed slavery.

Other Books have been written on Hispanics in Vietnam, a book called "Dogs From Illusion" written by Arturo Trujillo

Other titles are "GI Juana", I'm still trying to remember the authors name. The author is a Latina who was once in the military.

How about Squadron 201, a group of pilots that served during WWII. This pilot were all from Mexico and were trained in Rio Hondo,Texas. This ACES saw combat in the South Pacific. Nothing was heard of them until now. A possible movie is in the making , maybe like the movie Pearl Harbor.
