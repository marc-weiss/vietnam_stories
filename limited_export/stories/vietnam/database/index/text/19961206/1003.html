After seeing the news coverage of the VM(Wall), I knew I must see this creation. The war memorial with a soul, a search engine and a way of providing a release for all the whys, why nots, should haves and should not haves. What a vision! The creator gave use something, the polititians a destiny for futre "actions" involving troops and the global vision of how conflkict should be handled in the future.

I finally visited the Wall. The situation was sobering, rewarding and touched me forever! My cousin, retried regular Army(West Point '66). Two Purple Hearts and a great sense of "Duty, Honor,Country". Respectful, quiet and inward are the words to describe this family icon. At the same time, a book was on the best seller's list....Long Grey Line(not the old book and the movie) but the newer one written by the Washington Post Reporter. Book tracked the lives of several West Pointer's of 1966...and their lives. Some of the names are on the Wall. I mention this because of the dicotomy I saw in my cousin's eyes as he viewed the Wall for the first time...he had been to visit the Wall until I asked him to join me in the pilgramage. His wife,thru his career from graduation day, was also against the War, was a "good Army wife" but still hand her own soul, vision and mind. What a moment of closure! My cousin's reflection of his combatr experience and understanding purpose, my cousin's wife and her reflection of some of the same situations plus the reflection of her reasons she oppposed the war and my pondering of how I could have been part of this monument had I joined the Army in 1969 right out of high school.

After I returned home from my trip, enlightened by the experience and full of a new deminsion to life and character, as set by my role model cousin. I also read the popular book...started on Friday night and read thru the weekend. I could not put this down....then I got to the section of the book that told the story about the Wall, the concept, the process and the funding. Now I was PISSED! If you read the book, you find out a group of WP'66 classmates organize, initialkize the process and acquire funding. The initial funding was thru a bank officer(another WP grad) and this group based on a hand shake. Duty, Honor Country! I promise to pay youu back! That is unbelievable! Then after reading of this fantastic show of character, youth in action and the American way you discover that there was opposition to the Wall.....another group wanted to havew a more traditinal monument...in hind sight what a stupid idea....anyway...one of the tactics the oppostion used to attempt to get their ideas excepted was to have the IRS audit the funding of the initial WP group, challange their tax status and audit all receipts and expenses. FOUL! Guess who was a key opponnent?......ROSS PEROT! You conclude yourself based on these facts....would you want that man as Commander in Chief ...mentaly charged to act the same way the traditional WWII philosophy of foreign policy. I know I would not!

Conclusion...I learned alot about what character really is...I went to the Wall with a dedicated son of our country...watched is life unroll in front of me and finally "got it" about being a person of character, and understand what that means. I wish that could transfered to everyone.

The only unanswered question in my mind is the lack of closure the Korean Veteran must have. Now that teh garden monument for these Vets has opened, I felt sad about the Vets the survived Korea, came home, didn't say anything about the police action and died without this closure. To those relatives, I say "God Bless you family/father" for this sacrifice.
