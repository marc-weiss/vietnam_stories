This was he sadness day of my life I am going to write about and still to this day it brings a tear to my eye. The thing started when I met a clueless soldier called John checker ("Johnny"). I met him whislt in trainning and he was small and the rest of the soldiers make fun of him. He tried to make it look like he was not intimadated by this but I could tell he was scared and wanted to go home. I felt I should take him under shoulder and look after him because I knew he could not handle much more torment. Over time he became more confident and i could see him turning into a descent soldier and best of all a good man; you no someone you could trust in battle.

Them it came to the day we went out into battle;it was a day of celabration and also of regert, it suddenley hit people they might not be coming back.

One day on a daily patrol Johnny and I were walking guns in hand around our jungle surrounded base then suddenly from the trees a shot was heard we both dropped to the floor. After I thought it was safe I said to get up and then looking at Johnny I noticed that he was not getting up and he was laying face down in the wet mud. Thoughts went through my head and I turned him over. I saw that a bullet had entered his side and blown the front of his chest off and he was white as a ghost. Then it hit me he was dead. "Johnny noooooooo".

I will never forget that day he was my best friend and I always think why wasn`t it me and what would life be like if he was still alive.

THIS IS TO YOU JOHNNY REG NUM. 4693

Alex Heaps
1947
Houston,texas,USA
