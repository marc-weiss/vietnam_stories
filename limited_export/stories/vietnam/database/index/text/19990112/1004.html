this is open to all the vets from nam. first of all i want to say i'm so proud of all of you and i just wish that i could have been able to go to nam also. i wrote to presdent kennedy about going and he advised me to stay in school and finish. i wish now as i have all these years later that i had of gone. i feel that i let the country down and myself for not going. i did't always agree on the reason for fighting over there but i believed in serving my country. i've had a good life four children and married to the same guy for 31 years. i have always talked to my kids about the vietam war and i lost some classmates to the war. i have two sons now who will read everything they can find on the war. i finally got to the wall in washington d c two years ago with my 15 year old son it was something that i wanted to do for a very long time. i saw some vets there and did'nt know what to say to them but i sure felt a great since of pride just to know that i was so close to them. be proud of yourselves and of the job you did while you were there. its time that you hold your heads high and say hey! i'm a veitnam vet. my heart goes out to all of you and i want you all to know that maybe you don't know me or propaly never meet me but i will always hold a special place in my heart for and i love you for what you did and for what you are. i'm proud that i can even get to write this telling you my feelings. may God bless you all he put us all here for a reason and you gave your all to him and your country so i'm proud of you. i lost some friends to nam also and to finally see their names on the wall gave me a feeling that i will never forget. may GOD BE WIT H YOU margaaret gary mattawamkeag maine
