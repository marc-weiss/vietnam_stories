I just visited the memorial for the 1st time this fall. My uncle matthew ruden was killed in Vietnam before I was born. I could not leave. I did not want to leave him alone. Then I saw all the rest of the names and knew he was not alone. I was crying for a young man i never knew. I thought about what i had done with my life. At 20 my uncle was dead. I had flunked out of college. I often talk about what i would have done. Enlist, be drafted, flee, protest? While i admire the courage and strength of those who fought, i also admire those who protested. They were trying times and everyone dealt their own way.

Russell Clark Hess
1971
Iowa City, IA USA
