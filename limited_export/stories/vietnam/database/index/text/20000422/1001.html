Dear Sue Johnson and Alison Cornyn: I am a vietnam veteran...I have sent several pieces of art to Maya Lin as I felt an enormous amount of alliance with her....My art has always had elements of the orient in it every since I was there...as a helmsman on an Army cargo ship l969-70...It was just recently I had a thought as to why all these old veterans get teary eyed...and I thiink it is that time has integrated them with the exterior chosen enemy...and the enemy within....I have had many healings along the way and my first major healing came about after meeting my wife Robin of 17 years...she plays electric cello and I write music on a double-neck guitar...I wrote a portrait song that was a theme song for a documentary about Will Rogers...I was a street hobo for around seven years....The story on the Url below was written and illustrated by me for a friend who has a young boy who can't grow hair...which there is a large organization for Alopecia...I feel lucky to have come this far so may vets had no way of finding their pain...being connected to art has been my ladder...even professional help through the Veterans hospital in Topeka Kansas who at the time were utilizing information from Menningers mental hospital also ther in Topeka...All these injuries that multiply in our minds left unattended are fatal...An old man in a dream Told me quite lovingly that he knew I could do it and ...at that moment I forgave myself...that was a great relief I am not sure anyone else could understand how those words in a dream could mean so much...Thank you vietnam veterans and all those who gave you love after the tragedy of war...below is a story about a boy who couldn't grow hair
