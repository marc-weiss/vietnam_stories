One of 10 thousand memories... First patrol. My unit landed in Vietnam in July of 1967. We were immediately bagged off to a defensive area outside of DaNang to relieve the 4th Marines who were participating in Operation Hastings. Our first patrol was a unit in country involved a pretty standard evening ambush exercise in which I was the point man for my platoon. Our gunnery sergeant got a little disoriented and had us marching 180 degrees in the wrong direction, it was his first night. I was following a compass point man. He took us on a course into a village. The man immediately behind me, a fellow named Jim Coy, lost his nerve and wouldn’t follow me into the village. I was so nervous and concentrating so hard on what was in front of me, I never looked back until about 10 minutes had gone by. I was in the middle of a village, looked out over the rice paddies in the moonlight and realized I was completely alone. The platoon had gone off without me following Coy who was totally lost but didn’t have the nerve to go into that village. I was afraid to rejoin the unit because I thought I’d be shot coming up from behind, they were pretty nervous too. I tailed them at about 200 yards back and watched my platoon walk right into an ambush by our own first platoon.
