someone pointed out to me that the names on the wall are chronologically placed. And the earliest names they noticed were 1959. I was the TINENCO with the 266 army band in Fort Red Texas in 1959 and we were teaching classes at that time about Cambodia, Laos, North and South Vietnam and the names first started coming into daily news at that time. We knew that there was hostility at that time and GI’s were losing their lives there. I had that confirmed, I believe, when the earliest acknowledged deaths if were in ‘59 and the date on the wall being ‘59.

I’m just wondering there are any other people who served during that period of time who have been denied benefits because they fell before the next window of acknowledgment which was ‘64. And we are ineligible for certain benefits, we are ineligible to join certain organizations. The VA does acknowledge membership back to ‘59 and they were amazed and concerned that those of us who served before 64 were denied benefits and membership in the American legion, and VFW.

I just wondered if there was anyone else out there, who has had the same experience. The legislative process ground to a halt, I was told that there weren’t enough of us out here to amount to a hill of beans as far as votes were concerned.

Harvey Redak
1938
Chester, CT
