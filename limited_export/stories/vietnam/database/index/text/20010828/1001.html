I was too young to have served in vietnam. However the pain of the war is felt by many Americans including myself. 3 years ago I visited the wall in Washington. The feeling I experienced was unlike anything I ever felt before. As I stood and witnessed the names of our fallen brothers, I couldn't help but feel the incredible sense of loss combined with hopelessness and anger. I looked at the wall and saw a generation of young boys that were slaughtered. It was almost as though it was sureal, like a dream. How could it be? A generation of fine young Americans erased from existence. It is maddening when you think about it. If Vietnam never happened, and hollywood made a fictional movie about it, no one would go see it, because the American public would say, it is so far fetched and unbelievable. But the sad relaity is that Vietnam was real and it did happen. It continues to reach from the past and haunt us like a nightmare that never ends. My heart goes out to everyone that served and the families that suffered as a result of their love ones serving. I sometimes feel ashamed of my generation, that others who served had to suffer and sacrifice so much to make a better life for all of us Americans. We will always be forever in your debt.
