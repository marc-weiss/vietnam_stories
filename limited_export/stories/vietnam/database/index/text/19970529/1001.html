Vietnam by M. D. Hodges

Missionary David H. of Hong Kong and myself, had the joy of visiting the country of Vietnam. We were met by a missionary to Vietnam. I carried Vietnamese Bibles with me from the U.S. While in Hong Kong they were left in a Taxi Cab. There are over thirty taxi companies in Hong Kong and it looked like an impossibility to retrieve them, but God wanted the Vietnamese to have these Bibles. The Bibles were found a few hours before leaving for Vietnam and now we would have to pray that the communist government would not pay attention to them as we went through customs. Praise the Lord, they made it through. The missionary (TE) said that he knew we would get the Bibles in because he would always give a Bible to people when they accepted the Lord and he was down to one Bible. You can not approach people about the Lord nor speak in public anything about God or you probably would be deported or arrested. When people accept the Lord, they bring their friends and relatives to your home to hear the Gospel. In the three month period that missionary TE has been in the country, over thirty have accepted the Lord in this fashion. One day we took a trip up the country from Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon). At risk, we were taking Bibles and Bible lessons up the country about three hours to another province. There we met with a group of older men who had accepted the Lord. Many of them had been South Vietnamese soldiers who fought with the Americans during the Vietnam war. Some of them had escaped to other countries and they were placed in detention camps for years and were eventually deported back to Vietnam. Their government looks down upon them and will not grant them a citizenship card, therefore they cannot get a job. They live in poverty (physically) but these men were rich because they have trusted Christ. Before we left town, we stopped at a furniture factory (very primitive) and David and I stood outside wondering why we were there. A few minutes later missionary TE came and told us that he had won this furniture factory owner to the Lord about two months ago and that he wanted all of his factory workers to hear the Gospel story. They all sat around and for the first time heard that there was a God in heaven who loved them and cared for them. Thirteen men and one woman excepted the Lord that day. I found that the Vietnamese people were so friendly and receptive to Americans (of course we were only in the southern part of the country), yet the government is very skeptical and condemning of any religious activity. "The harvest truly is great, but the labourers are few:" The need for missionaries in Vietnam is so great as the people are ready for the Gospel. "....Pray ye therefore the Lord of the harvest, that he would send forth labourers into his harvest."
