I remember several weeks after i arrived from viet nam my dad was cooking for me in the back yard i was sitting drinking a beer and kicking back listening to con junto music playing on the radio what station i dont remember ? but it was good sounds. My dad and i hardly ever talked like father and son he was distant and i learned to except that.As i watched him fliped the chicken leg on the pit the flames flared out and at that moment i was back in nam at my last and final fire fight.This was the worse i was in we lost two men and five injured.I was taking count and briefing my squad when the choper landeded i was told i was going home to pack up and to get on the chopper "now" it was a shock to me we just got over a fire fight and im going home "now" no time for goodbyes or take cares bro's, just a goodbye stare that was it never saw them again no time relax no time to unwind.I snapped back to the real world when i heard my dads voice calling for me , mijo you ok ? I realized i wanderd off again. Yea im ok dad and i stared off into the distance again.There was silence for a while then my dad without looking at me he said son i know what your going through i know that stare nothing was said after that. My dad fought in wwII he never talked about it to me or anyone. All i remember was his drinking and his long stares.That day i was as close to my dad as i would ever be . That night i slept without going back.

andrew c. duarte
1949
