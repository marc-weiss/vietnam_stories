During the war I marched and demonstrated against it. I don't ever remember being really patriotic, but the war ended whatever was left of that. Permanently. My country was doing stuff- I saw it every night on tv of course- killing civilians burning villages, napalm, "defoliating" the forests- things that made me hate the government, the military: it hurt me too. It made me cry to see that to know that was going on, and the lies! I have not believed anything they told me since then. After the war sometimes when I talked to the vets coming home I might feel sorry for them a little bit. But I could never really forgive them either. There are some ways I feel like I got wounded in that war. I did. I got hurts from that war that will never heal. I'ts from feeling the agony of the people of Vietnam.

The biggest thing for me though is not anger or old wounds or being forever antipatriotic or any of those things from when the war was going. Because what happened to me was this. When the first war refugees began to arrive here, it was a family, a big household of Lao, Lao Hmong people, I went and asked themif they wanted me to teach them english. It was all I could do for them but it was a big thing. It was incredible to me how much they wanted to learn. Then I met another group I started teaching in a house in Brighton. I really did not know much about language teaching so I took a class to learn how and finally got to be a certifiead teacher of ESL. And that is all I have done since then, and it's all I ever will do. Byt that time the Vietnamese "boat people" were coming in large numbers and I was teaching them. I always loved teaching English to all those refugees.

You know I never thought about this like I am going to do something to help my country's victims or anything like that. It just happened and once I sterted doing it it is so great, to get to know all those people and to be able to do something for them they really wanted- I mean really wanted- language was what makes the difference, how they could live a good life, it meant everything to them and to me too. Someday I will send more stories about my students, and their stories.

Paul Siemering
