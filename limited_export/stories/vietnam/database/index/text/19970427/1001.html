PSYCHIATRIC CASUALTIES Sun Apr 13 11:47:59 US/Eastern 1997

I served stateside from 1965 to 1968, trained at Ft Sam as a neurospsychiatric medic. At Letterman and later at Tripler waves of military and dependent staff came through our wards. My thoughts about the war were confused at the time, but my thoughts about the men and women suffering trauma that would like last a lifetime were not confused. Nor are they today. Several memories stand out: More than a few infantry personnel arrived at Tripler after the tranquilizers they were given proved ineffective in a combat setting. Marines on Thorazine. On another occasion a young soldier from Philadelphia arrived mute, in a catatonic state unable to retell, until weeks of hospitalization, the chase through the jungle that left his squad dead or captured and caused him to commit desparate acts to survive. Where is he now? How is he now. The most bizarre memory is of a Navy navigator who had been assigned to surviellance duty over China/Vietnam to snoop and gain intelligence data. While on R&R in Hawaii he found himself at the officer's club in Waikiki...having a few drinks, one of which at least was accompanied with LSD. Knowingly or unknowingly. Naked and quite high we took him with SP assistance back to hospital, where within a day or so things seemed normal. Shipped back to Oak Knoll he received a clean bill of health and dropped in a month later to say he was going back to duty. Flying over SE Asia.

Am interested in hearing from vets or medics about the topic.
