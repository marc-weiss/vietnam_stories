I'm still a nurse and I still love the profession. 28 years ago this month I had orders to Vietnam (volunteered). I want to talk about what my kids call the "mommy thing".you see the "mommy thing" has something to do with Vietnam. My boys were always interested in the part of my life during the war. When my youngest son was 4 he asked why I didn't get killed over there.I told him I had to come back to be his mommy. I suppose the "mommy thing" is related to what some folks call PTSD. My boys kind of accepted my idiosincrities..hearing helicopters miles away, running to look where in the sky it was, crying at the National anthem., sitting up on the roof at night, over-reacting to the little things, seemingly fearless in the face of adversity. They also loved it when I stopped drinking any alcohol!! Now that they are very close to the "draft age" themselves I respect them for their "guts" and innocence just as I did so care for love and respect the soldies in Vietnam 28 years ago.

Mary Nichols
1948
