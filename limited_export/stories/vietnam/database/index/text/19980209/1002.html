Vietnam affected me in many ways,today is a borrowed one,I have terminal cancer and I fear life not death,when I arrived in Vietnam I was very much in fear of death,after the first life I took life became less important,the more lives the less important,I remember that when those few times when there was enough time to reflect,I felt sad that although I was raised to place a high value upon life that I had become someone who had lost hope,at that point in time I knew that for me I would never be the same.God had made allowances for soldiers that were in wars that were fought for noble causes,but this war was in no way able to lay claim to anything close to a noble cause,only greed.I to this day see the nameless faces of the dead,they cling onto even some good memories,though dead they were not without voice,I remember hearing the crys of people,ours,theirs crying out in pain,begging to die.Between tours while home I remember how people here looked at me,I felt very dirty and although many showers each day I could not feel clean.I had to return to Vietnam,I had two brothers and did not see any way I could let happen to them what had/was happening to me.I remember how the government treated the war and those who served and those who observed from their televisions,we were misguided and they were missed led,this has made me aware that there are people who will lie/kill/destroy for political and financial gain.I truly believe this,My death was planned,it was included into the cost control of the war,Agent Orange was put into our water,I during my first tours in the infantry and our water came from rice paddys and bomb craters,all these years later they continue to refuse to test those of us who were exposed the most,the people in the Ranch Hand group did not drink the water,also they wore protective clothing,flying in the sky and spraying to the ground.People today are no smarter than before Vietnam,I hear on the news often about trains/trucks & so on spilling chemicals and the news people say that reports are that there is no danger,this makes me sick!I sure do not see them going out getting samples and finding out the truth.I believe this,Veterans are probebly the greatest Americans,they go then they"if They"return and tell the truth,the average citizen lacks the courage to make any valid attempt to seek out and address the truth,they just want what they want and fail the fortitude to ask for accountability.They also allow the politicans to use Veterans,the Elderly,the sick,the Poor to move their greedy agendas through and most do not even care enough to vote.You do not see the congress/senate call to hear from low ranking soldiers/civillians,they call the generals,big bussiness,the heads of organizations representing the lower public.Why Do WE Spend BILLIONS each year for less than 60 million veterans,this is simply a means of taking from the poor/middle class and giving to big bussiness,such as pharmacutial cc's and contractors,ect.I would rather them give me a million or two and forget me and I would be more than happy to forget them.Makes too much sense.I am tired and before I close I would like to say this,The Vietnamese People are People of The World,They Are In Many Respects The Same As Us,I Have a Profound Respect FOR EACH AND EVERYONE OF THEM!

Daniel Knight
1945
Belton,Texas U.S.A.
