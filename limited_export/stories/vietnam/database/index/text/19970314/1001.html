I often wondered quietly to myself what effect the Vietnam War has had on my life. I've wondered silently since I was nine years old, without a word to my family or friends until recently.

I have wondered... What happened over there that makes two distinct memories of the man that was my father? How is it that my eldest sister was so loved by him before the war, before I was born. So loved by him that he adopted her and wanted her. Loved and laughed with her and my mother. Why after the war, are there only memories of loud words and silences... and then he was gone? What made him leave? What made him disappear and not want to see his kids .. one of whom he had once loved enough to adopt as his own even though she was not his own. The other one he stayed up all night praying that she would live after being born pre-mature. How could a man care so much one moment and then not care at all? Divorce is one thing, utter silence is another.

I think about what kind of a man he would have been had he not spent that time in that war called Vietnam. I think about what has been taken away from me and I wonder what was taken away from him.

I've also thought that I must be crazy to attribute all of this to a war. I've thought that perhaps I was being maudlin and buying into the drama of an Oliver Stone movie. How do I know? I don't speak with him. I was too young to know this man who first loved my mother and half-sister. I only know the man who left us. And plenty of men have left their families before.

I have read some of the stories here and my heart hurts and at once lightens while I read. They tell me that perhaps the man who made me was not always the cold, distant man I have seen. Perhaps there was love in him and that's why I'm here. Perhaps what happened after the war was out of my control and out of his, too.

I wish I knew some of his war stories to tell you. I don't know them. But thank you for sharing yours.

Vickie
1967
New York, New York
