I can not consider myself as the post war generation. However, my mother is certainly part of the post war generation that was forgotten, discriminated, and have no definite homeland.

My mother is one of the supposely called "Asian American" children. The memory of my mother has of her father is not a name, not a hug, not a kiss, but only a picture. Her father left Vietnam a couple of months before my mother was born January 1961.

To the Vietnamese, my mother was "my lai". During and afther the war, the communist literally hunt down all of those who they considered "my lai". My mother could not come the the United States, and yet, she was not accepted in the country which she was born.

We finally had the opportunity to come to the United States in 1988, and are now American citizens and residents of Virginia. The memories of my mother's childhood have haunted her for all these years. Until now, she still have never met her father.

All I am asking is please do not forget all of those who are like my mother.

Nina Ho
1978
Reston, VA, USA
