On Raising Girls and Boys:
Raelene, a teen mother, talks about her baby girl

... I want her to make her own dreams. I want her to do what she wants to do. I don't want to make her do anything.

I want her to go to school and get a good job, you know? I want her to become what she wants to become. And I think it would be easier if she was a boy. I don't know for what reason, but they just seem like they got it more... like he does, my brother.

[Boys] don't really ...have to do anything. Like, they could just sit around and not get in trouble for it. The girls always got to clean and cook and do all that kind of stuff, so I think it would be easier if [my daughter] was a boy. But then she would be lazy, if she was a boy, so I don't want her to be lazy, either.
