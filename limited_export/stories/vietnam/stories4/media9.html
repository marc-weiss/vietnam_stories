My chief frustration today is that we, Americans, have learned nothing from the terrible losses we suffered during those years of unforgivable folly. The deaths of more than 58,000 American young men, the maiming of many more, the killing by bombardment, by napalm, by disease and starvation of two million Asians, the loss of more than $250 billion in national treasure, the ceding of our place in the markets of the world to Germany and Japan who overtook us during those precious, lost years of needless warring, all of these losses have had no consequences for those who were responsible for the war.
