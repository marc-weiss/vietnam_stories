Jana Everett

My most vivid memories of that era have to do with being involved in the anti war movement-- Probably my most searing memory is being with a friend at the pentagon march and having her freak out over the soldiers with fixed bayonets and she started screaming at the other demonstrators, I realized she needed help and really it seemed no one on either side could be very helpful--That really brought home to me that this was a terrible conflict that was tearing people up--not only the soldiers on both sides, but the people back home, whatever their positions on the war, it was a very sobering experience.
