It was odd reading the Far East Time edition in the summer of '67 and seeing Detroit's and Cleveland's shattered streets--knowing I had left a battle raging here. It was strange coming back during '68 Tet, feeling then that we weren't going to win. It was/is good "fighting" here in this "war of liberation" with different "weapons" and a certainty of how this "war" comes out. What doesn't kill you, strengthens you. Some of us even learned something. Twenty-four then; almost 54 now. And still raging in a mellower way.

Horace Coleman
is the author of In the Grass)
Huntington Beach, CA (excerpts)
