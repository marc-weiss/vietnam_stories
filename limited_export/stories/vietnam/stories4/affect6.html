Jana Everett

My involvement in the antiwar effort changed me in terms of the kind of career I sought. I went into college wanting to work in the foreign service, I changed to teaching in college. It made me much more cynical about state power. It also opened me to involvement in feminism.

The war was the decisive event in my early adult life--I think it turned me toward a belief in grass-roots politics and trying to live my values, however partially I have done so. It also turned me inward--seeing all the anger and hate from both sides contributed to my wanting to heal myself.
