THANK GOD

This is to the people who feel guilt about not going. I spent two tours in country and all I can say is "Thank God that you didn't have to deal with the crap that stayed with me for 30 years." The real thing to remember was that at that time a uniform was a uniform. I'm sure we all know someone who was affected by it directly or peripherally.

Thank God, you don't wake up every two hours to walk the house and then go back to bed. Thank God, you don't have survival skills that have or have not turned into "coping skills." Thank God, that you have pride in your country and respect the government. Thank you.

Chet Botkins
Submitted to the Dialog area Postwar trauma: Kicking it Dec. 2nd, 1996
