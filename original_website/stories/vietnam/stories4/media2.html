Renny Christopher

I was six when Diem and Kennedy were assassinated. I don't remember Diem; I remember not Kennedy's death, but his funeral: the black horse with the empty boots backwards in the stirrups.

I was eight when the man who would become my husband fifteen years later went with the earliest Marines to Chu Lai to build the landing strip there. I remember vividly four news events: the deaths of Bobby Kennedy and Martin Luther King, "Tet" and the Lunar landing.
