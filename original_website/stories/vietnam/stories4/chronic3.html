Duane Hoffman

I am bitter and can not resolve my grief over Bill's death. You see, Bill did not have to go to Vietnam. He enlisted as a volunteer. He was decorated with a Purple Heart for a wound that he received while under attack. Bill did not believe that the military of that era was justified in their actions. He served anyway and died for his loyalty and service. His name is not on the Wall. Until now you, reader, are among the few who know what he gave and did; and, have a sense of the futility of his life. He was never the same after Vietnam.
