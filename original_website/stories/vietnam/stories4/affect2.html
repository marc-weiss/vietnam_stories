Matthew Cardwell

But all that disappeared as I read the story of My Lai. My own personal transformation from patriot to disillusioned cynic began with that book -- it began, unbelievably, when I was ten. My own loss of faith in the inherent goodness of America that resulted from that book must have paralleled, in some ways, what America experienced as they watched Vietnam unfold. I finally understood why no one wanted to talk about it. I now understood why some people treated it as a four-letter-word.
