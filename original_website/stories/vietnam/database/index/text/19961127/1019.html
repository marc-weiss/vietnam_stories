1. I was a child during the Viet Name War. I was born in 1960. 2. My most vivid memory was my brother possibly being drafted. Other memories were the bombings, and the portest music. 3. The protest music conveyed values to me that I incorporated with my Christian upbringing. I took Christianity and the message of nonviolence, love and forgiveness a bit more literal than my parents would have liked.

Viet Nam also delivered up lessons of government lies and duplicity. It caused me to distrust the military primarily and the government beyond that.

4. The war and the events that followed led to my developing a sense of dedication to social change. I've dedicated myself to doing this through my career. This has caused strains with my rightward leaning parents and led me to choose a more difficult road than if I'd "only" tried to survive and thrive in this economy.

My atitude towards life is that we must engage the battle for justice and equality. This is my goal, not making lots of money or getting power. (I do OK with money, though).

5. I've moved beyond Viet Nam. We need to move beyond trauma. But, I wasn't as affected as the older folks.

What also got to me was Watergate. Right as I was noticing this government out there, the President turned out to be a total crook.
