In 1968 I was 17 and considering accepting an offer from my congressman to appoint me to West Point. Driving around Austin one night, I picked up a hitchhiking college girl who told me about her relationship with a West Point cadet. She described the social life at the academy and the rules aginst "public display of affection", and the strains on her relationship. I dropped her where she was going and knew West Point wasn't for me. I was apolitical at the time and was attracted to the image of the military and the free education. Funny how the thought of not being able to walk with my arm around my girlfriend probably kept me from accepting the invitation to join. I've always thought that if I hadn't heard that young woman's story I would probably have been a late arriving platoon leader in Nam.
