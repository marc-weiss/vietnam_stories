When I left Vietnam in 1969 (Jan.) and got on that plane, it was the beginning of "gravey time". We used to say, if you survived everything else is gravey. I served the entire time of 1968 with the 1st Air Cav. I survived TET , Khe Sahn , 22 days in the hospital with jungle fever (I was told Murine Typhus) and everything else connected with my tour.

I had Army helicopters dump Agent Orange on me and was shot at with everything the enemy had to offer. I made it to gravey time. My greatest disappointment looking back is the way the US treated its real Allies, the hill people in S.E. Asia. Towards the end of my tour I was an RTO at a Montegyard village near Cambodia, LZ Jake. These people earned my respect.

Something the South Vietnamese never got. When we left the area on their own, these people were left behind. We brought home our buddies the govnt officials.

We also left behind our own troops. (POWs/MIAs - kissed the boys goodbye) I dont think I would go fight some politicians war again. I will fight to defend my home.

Since Vietnam - I had a period of time, about 12 years , I didnt talk about Vietnam period. Lately it is always on my mind. I have friends that served in the Nam that have children with birth defects, I checked some of my platoon in basic to see how many made the wall, so far (havent checked them all) 10 are on it.

My opinion of the news media and especially Walter Cronkite went to the basement in the way they did not support the troops (Ever). I have been in law enforcement since getting out of the Army, attacked several times, shot once. Married 25 years with two children. Jane Fonda (Communist) traitor was never put in jail and the Agent Orange issue went on for years (read the book Waiting for an Army to Die) still not resolved.

I have supported the Wall financially and someday hope to visit. Finaly if I new then what I know today, I dont think I would have laid my life on the line for the Wash. D.C. bunch without a clue of what the hell they were doing!

John Pardee - (Sgt. 1st Air Cavalry -1968)
