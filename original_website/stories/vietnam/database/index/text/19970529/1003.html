The Wall May, 1996, my first visit.

I returned today to where this begins for me now, to the symbol of the War in Vietnam, its essence, its memorial. Yes, my old friend, I am here with you and you with me, on this journey in search of myself. The stark truth dwells here, the bottom of a well, a nexus, a focal point. I long to feel that truth, to grieve with it, to seek peace within its embrace, to seek peace within myself.

It was early in the morning, the night scent still rising from the earth, the grass, and the yellow cast of the new day had still not fled. I stood at a threshold, a gateway, a path, to a time, a place, of the past, my past. I knew this would lead me to my longing, a pathway to my soul. Do I have the courage, I asked myself? Is this a rite of passage? I think it is. I hesitate, I enter, and I begin to cry. Lonely, empty, confused, afraid, I enter, and it enters me.

Three statues dwell in this place, a place of power, energy, a thousand charms, a thousand spells. Three bronze statues of men in Vietnam, frozen, in time, in space. The polished surface of their skin glistens like bodies long drenched in the sweat of fear. Their faces are drawn, tense. I want to join with them in my agony of this encounter, this moment.

I reached out and touched the arm of one of the men, the one on the right, I remember. His slick bronze skin was cool, but beneath rolled a pulse of fire, my fire, my yearning. I cry, again. Weeping. Afraid. Come with me, please, and help me. I follow their frozen gaze to a form, endless, eternal, breaking and rising from the earth. Go there, they said, softly.

For a long time I sat under a tree on a park bench, staring. My thoughts fold in upon themselves. A thousand questions. A thousand fears. They say this is a wall, a black wall. This is no wall, my heart speaks. It is an entity, a being, a mystical folded form, dihedral, a wing, a phoenix struggling to rise from the earth, slicing its green bonds to escape, to freedom, to the sky, to release. Its feathers are the names of men who died, and the souls of those who lived. The souls are invisible. I know mine is there, somewhere.

I enter upon this wing, slow steps, tears mark my face. I go to the center. I ride this wing, this phoenix. My bronze friends gaze from the distance as I feel the wing move, its tips fold over me, and draw me in. I am with it, joined. This is where I belong. I am. I am here. I am here with my soul.

I journeyed into the bosom of this wing twice. Twice I soared, twice I joined, twice I journeyed back to the beginning. Bittersweet, torn, tormented, the agony of its time, of my time. The curious crowds of people with blank faces not knowing how to act fade from view. I am with my soul, and a thousand souls. Its all right, we know, we understand. The embrace is soft. I feel the pain, exquisite, hot, agony. We were connected, this wing and I. It left a piece of itself lodged in my heart. A gift. I will be back. I have promised.

I sit writing this in a lonely hotel room. Tears mark my face, again, again. I do not yet know what to do with the feelings I have found in my heart. Time, friends, patience, and understanding will tell me. In time. I am weeping now. I feel. I feel. Faces fill my mind, my thoughts. I will cry myself to sleep, tonight.

Tomorrow is here. It is very early. I did not sleep well. I read this over and over, searching for its meaning. My mind says its is just a wall. My heart says differently.

I will never forget. Once, a long time ago, I walked near this place, and fled. I am now with this place, forever.

The Wall May, 1997, my second visit

Once again we meet on this field of battle, my old friends, the field of battle that now only exists in the recesses of my darker mind, and given life across the green field of time.

A bronze image, the three are now but one, still gaze as I left a year ago, transfixed upon this wing of hope, joy, and sorrow, now and forever to be and yet be no more.

My gaze moves to the Wall, the wing, the place of my inner trial. Voices becon to me still, the rising feeling wrenching me to join with the souls within. As I walked within and down, the crowd melted away into colored shadows, and once again the sweet softness of pain softly enveloped my soul, an embrace of all within. I was one again with the Wall. I had kept my promise. I had returned.

In the pain, they gave me peace, my friends within the wall. Between my sobs of fear, guilt, and longing to connect with that time and that place where part of me still dwells, the voices longed to put to rest my torment and theirs.

Offerings of love come into focus, a flower here and there, a letter, and a can of beer enrobed in a zip loc bag, all tribute to the eternal and immortal connections within us all. A man on my right shares my pain. He said he put the can of beer there for his buddy. It was the wrong brand, but his buddy understood, and did not mind at all. I wept. The man said he lives nearby, and always comes here to remember and to cry.

We parted, my new friend and I, with a knowing shake of the hand. I climbed back into this world, and wandered through the trees drying my tears. I feel better for my trial. I will return again, for a piece of the Wall remains lodged in my heart, forever.

Arthur Varanelli
1946
Chester, NH
