My big brother, Dennis, served aboard the USS Coral Sea CVA-43 from the summer of 1964 to the summer of 1966 as part of his reserve duty. For most of 1965 they sat in the Gulf of Tonkin.

I've just been researching the troop increases from 1964 to 1965 and it jumped from a few thousand to almost 200,000 - and started being on the news every night at supper time. This really unnerved a certain young man (me) of 8-years old.

It's also about the time the protests and attacks on returning servicemen began. I was 8 and couldn't understand why the anger and abuses to our returning heros. At 8-years old I already knew the servicemen didn't make the policies - they just went where they are sent and did what they were asked to do.

I was always very proud of my brother, even though he only served a supporting role. He still served.

I'm sorry you weren't appreciated and honored at the time, and what you get now is so little and SO late.

Allen Sharkey
1957
