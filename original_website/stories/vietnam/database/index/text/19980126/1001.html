I was a seventeen year old enlistee in the Regular Army in 1968. My father was a career Air Force pilot and veteran of World War Two and Korea. I spent my first year in service training. Basic, infantry AIT, infantry NCOC and Ranger school. Two months after my 18th birthday I got my orders for Vietnam. Upon arrival I was assigned to the 1st Air Cavalry Division. My most vivid memory was the Cambodian invasion and the aftermath and consequences of the Kent State tragedy. To say that I am different Today because of Vietnam I can only say that I will always be different and am continuously evolving due to those experiences. Everything about me, politics and all is predicated upon my war experience. I was sick when I came home and it has taken 28 years to get a little better. At first I attempted to hide in the Pacific Northwest and California and as much as I despised myself as well as being convinced that other people felt the same, I could bear the sheer loneliness of the wilderness. I would very much to share my experiences concerning operations with a rifle company in the 1st Air Cavalry Division 1969-70.

Michael D. Collins
1951
