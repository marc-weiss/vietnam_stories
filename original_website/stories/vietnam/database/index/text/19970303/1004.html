GHOSTLY TAPERS

Working by the light of ghostly tapers,
My shovel bites the earth with a solid "Shuenk!"
I lift the dirt and pour it, Streaming the sandbag's open mouth.
These motions are repeated man after man, time after time.
The sandbags slowly pile up,
Creating the shelter to keep us safe
>From mortar rounds and rockets.
Safe from the dangers of war.

Ghostly tapers are our mark of tardiness.
Earlier we had light supplied by our mortar crews.
Parachute flares shooting into the sky and
Dropping, swinging lazily as they burned incandescently.
But the light ended. We were supposed to be done
We weren't. We only started on our shelter near dark.
First we built the command post. Now we won't stop
Till we can sleep under sandbags
No way out but to dig in t he dark

No way until we light our ghostly tapers.
Wax layers peeled from artillery boxes
Twisted into candles, they give light, weak but useful.
They give the satisfaction of ingenuity, a problem solved.
And the feel of some low budget horror movie.
But there's nothing low budget about this production.
American wealth and muscle and lives thrown into a
Misbegotten attempt to drive a stake through
The heart of Vietnamese nationalism and pride.

Ghostly tapers set the scene for this misadventure.
In the flickering light our shovels echo
America's effort to bury a nation.
We are the acolytes in this violent last rite.
But the Vietnamese do not well play the corpse;
They will not die to be reborn in our image.
And we are not soldiers, heirs to a proud tradition.
We are charicatures, serving in a military parody,
Unwilling attendants in a reluctant funeral.

Mark Fleming

Phoenix, AZ
