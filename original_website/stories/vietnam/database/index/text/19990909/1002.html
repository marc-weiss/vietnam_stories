I am the wife of a Vietnam combat veteran who spent his tour of duty (1967-1968: Tet Offensive) in the most hazardous assignment known. He can't tell his own story because it is too painful for him, so I will tell it. He was a "tunnel rat," crawling down into VC underground tunnel systems and blowing them up. Today he has such severe claustrophobia that he cannot stand to be with more than a few people at a time, he sufferes from severe PTSD, and for four years we were divorced because he wasin rage and couldn't admit it. He was dangerous then, but that was becasue he held in so much anger toward our government for what it made him endure in the war. Not only that, but the government then abandoned him when he came home. He was wounded several times, and together with his wounds and PTSD he was so disabled that he couldn't work regularly for many years. Our family suffered severe financial hardship because for over twenty years the government refused to authorize his disbility pension. He was wounded in Cambodia at a time when President Johnson was telling the American public that there were no American troops in Cambodia! He has little self-esteem, and he doesn't care whether he lives or dies. Sometimes he says it would be easier to die than to live with the guilt of surviving Vietnam when so many didn't.

Amy Snow
1949
