Wes Vinyard
1941
Myrtle Creek,OR-U.S.
Wes@Pioneer-net.com

Although I'm not a Viet Nam Vet, I have to say Viet Nam had a very profound effect on my life. In January, 1960 at age 18, I enlisted in the Army. Even though Basic training at Fort Carson, Co,was hard,and the weather was brutaly cold; I loved it, and was very proud of the fact that I was Regular Army (R.A. all the way!) I had two good friends in basic that had the same M.O.S. as mine. Motor Transport, and we were going for are second eight weeks together , and, on to Germany. At the end of Basic while waiting for bus to take us off base for our 30 day leave, I was called back and order to report to C.O. Wondering what it was all about,but not in the least bit worried about the outcome,I left my spot in line telling my friends that I would be back. Reporting to the C.O., I was informed that I would be held back and placed in a withholding company till certain issues regarding my inlistment into the service was cleared up. The issue being fraudulent entrie into the service. I was very shocked, and confused, by all this.Especially so,sense they wouldn't explain to me at the time exactly what I done wrong. I was keep in the withholding company for at least a month before they finally called me back to the C.O. All they wanted to know from me was if I was ever arrested and convicted for any crime. I explained that I was arrested and placed in a boys reformatory at age 15 for petty thief. I told them that I also informed the recruiting officer at the time of my inlistment, and he informed me that he would take care of it, and for me to go ahead and sign the papers,but not to put down that I have ever been arrested. While being held back in the withhoulding company,I would pull details every day. As time went on, I also became to feel sorry for myself, and started to slip off base every day to get out of the s--t details. After awhile of this, I was finally caught and confined to the base After about 7 weeks, I was called up again and told I would be getting a Undesirable Discharge for fraudulent entry. I was discharged May5,1960 and came home with the feeling that the military did me a great injustice. Five years later,I married ,and by the end of 1965, my wife was pregnate with our first child. By then the war in Viet Nam was heating up,and I was watching it on T.V. I remember feeling,I should be over there, helping in some way, and feeling very angry at myself for not being there. By the end of 1966, I informed my wife that I was going to do every thing that was possible to have my bad discharge changed so I may re-enter the service and, go to Viet Nam, to do my part. By that time, our first child had been born and, my wife did not want me to go. She even threaten me with divorce if I didn't let it go. Well, I can say now that the Viet Nam war has deeply effectly me . Over the years, I've grieved for the men that have lost there,and feel guilt because I should been there to help. Since the late 70's to early 90's, i've collected a great amount of books and documents on Viet Nam. Last Summer, I was fortunate to visit a mobil display of the Wall. I went with a friend, who is a veteran ( 3/1 Marines, Motors, 1967 ) . I remember as we pulled into the parking lot at the city park, that I could not get out of the car. I told my friend to go ahead and I would catch up. As I sat there,a great sadness overwhelmed me and I could not move from that car. Finally, I gathered myself up and got out of the car. I got within 50 feet of the Wall, and collasped in a sobbing mess. After awhile, my friend came back to where I laid , and tried to comfort me. It surprised me that other Vets came to me and patted my back,giving me comfort. It was also very embarassing to say the least. I also think I embarassed my friend by my display by he tried not to show it. I was able to get next to the Wall and touch it. It was a very profound experience to say the least. I has been this last winter that i've finally gathered up my library and sold it. I don't want to forget about the war,but then again I don't to feel guilty forever for not being able to not do my part. Thank you for letting me have the opportunity to express my thoughts and feelings about a time that we as Veterans, and non-veterans, will remember the rest of our lives.
