The Corkscrew of Fate

Copyright by Lewis Downey 1995

You never heard the one that got you Crude explosive power hurtling through the pungent air Ragged trail of sound hot on it's trail Were you sleep day dreaming of loved ones back in the world, Or just spinning time sweetly stoned? As it exploded over your bunk No knowledge of pain, flash and you're gone. Two dogs wounded, one man dead, Another soul rising with the dust and smoke, And a niche to fill.

As this mother's sone goes job hunting, and the wise E-6 says: "A guy has to watch out for himself." Didn't know anything and or suspect much While saluting reporting to the officer who With a shit eating grin says: "Boy do we have a good place for you."

The final verdict for you, best friend I never met, Landed me a job running your radio Connecting souls through 5,000 miles of ether "Hi mom, how're you doing, over." Your dogs were good company

An almost guided missle those VC rockets Corkscrewing the sky overhead Propelled by that staccato obligato. The one you can hear is the one that isn't going to get you. Does lightning strike twice in the same place? As I pile sandbags around my hooch and my heart.

Why was my luck so good and your's so bad? No guilt here but I think it's a shame I don't even remember your name.

Lewis Downey Phu Loi, RVN Army MARS AB8AAL '71-'72
