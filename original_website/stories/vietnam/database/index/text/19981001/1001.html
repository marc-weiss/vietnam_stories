I was born during the Vietnam War,But have since learned quite alot about it. I am the youngest in my family I have 2-older sisters, 48 and 46. My two sisters, well especially the oldest was an avid writer to the men in Vietnam, she also recieved several letters and photos from them. My father was a Purple Heart recipiant and Veteran of WWII,a man who I had the world of respect for. But the subject of the war was rarely discussed, if I asked questions, I recieved the shortest possible answer. This is how I became interested in Vietnam. I had letters and pictures this was real to me, being young you hear all kinds of things that people are saying about this horrible war, etc. In school they try to teach you about other Wars but Vietnam always seemed the shortest subject, if any. I wanted to know more, these men in Vietnam they were there for us, fighting for our country, most were made to go, it was not an option to stay home where they could have been safe. Our Government sent them there yet people were criticizing them,treating them like dirt. The older I got the more I wanted to know about Vietnam. Luckily due to my sisters age I became quite good friends with several Veterans, some were wounded in action, some did not come home, and others could not handle home after the year of hell. I have studied Vietnam through these men, through reading, especially books written by Veterans because the best place for information is the source. Although I am only 33 yrs. old these men who fought this war have a BIG place in my heart, I fly my POW-MIA flag PROUDLY, they deserve more no they actually deserve everything our government has to offer them, they did this and they need to help mend it. I want to let all the Veterans know some people do care, the way you were treated was wrong and embarassing even to someone as young as me. THANKYOU TO ALL VIETNAM VETERANS MY HEART AND PRAYERS OUR WITH YOU ALWAYS.

Jill
1965
Clifton Heights,PA
