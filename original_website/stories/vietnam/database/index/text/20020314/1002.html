I met an Ex-Navy Vietnam Veteran at the Domiciliary at the VA Hospital that I stayed at in the Homeless Vet's Program about 4 1/2 years ago. His name was Jack. At first, we were just friends, but over the next four years, we became alot closer, he became a lot more special to me. The last year we were together we both moved back into the same dom where we had met four years previously. His health was getting worse, and after seeing several doctors there, he was told he had a year, maybe two left to live. He told me stories about his life in the Navy during the Vietnam War. I do think that if there was any way he could change anything, he would have not done the drinking and drugging that he did while he was stationed there. That, in a nutshell, is why, I believe his life ended so soon, at the age of 50. Granted, he lived alot longer than some of those boys who went to Vietnam and came home in body bags, but if the Vietnamese people hadn't have taken advantage of the American servicemen, alone and so far away from home and made them all alcoholics and drug addicts, who knows, maybe the war might have ended differently, and I might still have Jack around to be a part of my life. I don't know who else there is to blame for his death. God knows, I did everything in my power to keep him around for as long as was humanly possible. I made sure he took his medications, made all his doctor appointments, and had everything he wanted, to make sure he was as happy as he could be for what time he had left on this earth. He was a good man, too good to go through all the pain and agony he had to live with the last few months he was alive. I may have been too young to actually serve in the military during the Vietnam war but it most definately has affected a major portion of my life.

Kaye D Dyer
1957
