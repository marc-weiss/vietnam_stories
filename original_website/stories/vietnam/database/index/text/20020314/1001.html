I was in the 4th grade the year (1967)that my older sister's fiance went to Viet Nam. I remember hearing about the war on the TV news, but I guess it really didn't make an impact on my life until I enlisted in the US Army in the summer of 1975; after I graduated from high school. I got to know alot of Vietnam veterans in the unit I was assigned to at Fort Campbell, Kentucky as well as those I met in the VA Hospitals where I received treatment after some personal crises. I never realized just how difficult it was for those on active duty in Vietnam, why the Vietnam war was impossible for this country to be able to win. I don't think that we had any business even being there in the first place, not to mention how hard it was to tell the Viet Cong apart from the South Vietnamese. Our military had no way of being sure when they met a Vietnamese person whether they were friend or foe. Who could possibly have been able to tell -- I don't see how the Vietnamese themselves could tell each other apart. All the Vietnamese did was sell drugs to our GIs to keep them so messed up they couldn't tell where they even were let alone which Vietnamese was which. I understand now why the US didn't stand a snow ball's chance of surviving in hell of winning anything in that country's war. Like JFK said, It's their war, let them fight it. That's what we should have done.

Kaye Dyer
1957
Leavenworth,KS USA
