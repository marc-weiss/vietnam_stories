MY GREATEST ADVENTURE

I was a National Servicemen in New Zealand - drafted - and went into the military in 1966 and found that it was great - Sports crazy, highly competitive, minimal bullshit and generally good fun.

Me and my mates did a deal where we would sign on en masse if they would send us overseas for two years and then release us from any further obligation - They did and sent us to Malaya for two years. Then Australia and New Zealand got involved in Vietnam. So we signed on for more Army to go there and practice our skills.

Off we went to Vietnam and it was generally exciting and working closely as a part of a team of professional soldiers out in the boonies. Our posting was one year. Then we went back to the world.

Like me many wished to go back for another time which we did. Most of the time the jungle was OK, sometimes with scary stuff, sometimes with casualties but most of the time because of our long time professional training and practice of our skills before we got there it was a situation we nearly always had the upper hand over the bad guys.

In many ways I'm pleased to have been there and endured the hardship that jungle bashing as a grunt provides. It makes one appreciate life as a civilian and concentrate on the things that matter unlike many who get involved in petty office politics and focus on non-essential matters.

I'm glad I went, eternally pleased to have come out of it unscathed, I'm sorry for the guys who never made it or who were injured and generally regarded it as an adventure.

Morally I don't care, wars have always been fought, invariably started by politicians and we can analyse the thing to death - It still won't make any difference. It happens just like shit happens.

Get rid of the sneaking ways of politicians and their deceit, greed and ambition, inhibit religion to sticking to their God stuff and keep their collective noses out of the rest of the stuff and encourage people to go about their daily business.

Would I go again? No way. Would I support any war? No way. Would I encourage anyone else to go wherever and get into life threatening situations? No way.

Do I support the military - Hell yes. There are regrettably always crazies out there viz Saddam Hussein, Hitler, et al. We have to retain some deterrent to keep them in check. We need high tech hardware to zap from a distance and protect our delicate protoplasm. Kill machines instead of us.

Bruce Isbister
