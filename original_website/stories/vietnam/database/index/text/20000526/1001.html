I spent two tours in VietNam, and to be truthful I am very proud of that fact. My First tour I was with one of the finest of all units in Nam. Echo 50th Lrrps later to become Echo 75th RANGERS. We lost quite a few of our men during the year and a half that I was there. It really burns me when I talk to a wannabe. I can't understand why when they had the duty to go they didn't, and now they want to claim the honor of having been there. In my view they are stealing what truely belongs to the true Vets. I also can't stand those draft dogers including our president. I have a lots of respect for the office,but not the man in it. It Has changed my life in that when I came back from Nam the second time, my life had change to the point that I was an angry man. Angry at our leaders for not having given us the oppertunity to win. As some of the other men that went there, I was able to get on with my life, but not before I had forced my wife to divorce me. I could not confide in her about the stuff I had seen and done over there.And she could not understand why I couldn't. I am on my third wife now, and thanks to Her,but most of all the Good LORD, and the Doctors at the V A I'm able to now control my anger. I pray that no one else ever has to go through the expierence of another VietNam.

Ray J Sonnier
1931
DeQueen,AR, Usa
