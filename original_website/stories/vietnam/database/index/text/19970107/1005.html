I just viewed the PBS show on the architect who designed the Viet Nam Memorial. I would like to share with you my reaction to a visit to the Wall.

As a 17 year old, I was keenly aware of the tradegies of the war. I vividly remember listening to LBJ tell the world he was sending vast numbers of troops into the war and the sickness I felt wondering who would be the ones that wouldn't return.

Shortly after it was dedicated, I went to see the memorial. As I approached it, I felt I was on hallowed ground; the dark polished marble with names of the fallen evoked a profound sadness and respect which cannot be articulated. The conversations of observers of and participants in the memorial were hushed and the atmosphere was one of reverence. People react to the memorial in a highly personal, interactive way, as evidenced by the mementos left by visitors. One is compelled to interact with the spirit of the wall. It serves as a focal point for healing.

Of the many memorials in Washington, DC, none draws in the viewer and evokes emotions like the Viet Nam Memorial.
