This is a continuation of my first installment. On the night of April 30, 1970 we were pulling bunker guard for the fire base near An Loc, S. Vietnam and were briefed by the CO, Captain Loomis that the next morning we were making a combat assault into Cambodia. We knew something big was happening and the only analogy that comes to mind were the same kind of buterflies in your stomach before a football game. All our previous air assaults were performed with five Huey's and two Cobra gunships in escort, requireing three lifts to move the entire company (about75-80 men). On the morning of May 1,1970, the communist holiday of Mat Day, the far distant noise of choppers was unmistakable and different. One of the things that every groundpounder became expert at was identifying helicopters by type and quantity as well as direction of travel. This new sound only indicated an extremely large quantity with no previous comparison. To say that it added suspense to what was already going through our minds would be an understatement. When they became visible, the size of the formation was at once shocking and beautiful. There were 25 Huey's and 10 Cobra Gunships in escort and in perfect formation, enough to lift the entire company at once. There was no doubt now, we were going to Cambodia. to be continued.
