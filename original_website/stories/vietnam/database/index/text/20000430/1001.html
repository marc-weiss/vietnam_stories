I spent two long tours of duty in Vietnam with the USMC (1965-1967, 13 months each). I did what my country asked of me, I didn’t develop a wide yellow stripe down my back and head north to Canada, I wanted to preserve freedom, I wanted to protect the one thing I hold so dear…the right for all people to be free. The namvets of the world did not lose the war; we were sold out by the very same people that want to do trade with Vietnam today. Just think, people are in such a hurry to have trade relation with a country the still holds our fellow Americans hostage, by not returning our dead. God that makes me so proud to be an American If president Ford had not sold out so many young Americans by, giving Nixon a pardon, giving the gutless wonders free passage back from Canada and pulling out of nam for his own gain, (he sure did look good by ending the war, didn’t he?) People were so concerned about the deserters coming back from Canada, the true heroes where forgotten. In a matter of day’s I was taken from killing people in the Jungle to small town America, never given a chance to even adjust, when I asked for help, did I get it…NO. It took years of fighting to get anything from the Government, I was not asking for something for nothing; I just wanted what I had earned in those 26 long months of hell. But all I got was people calling me, baby killer, warmonger, and people burning the beautiful Red, White and blue cloth that means so much to me. The symbol of everything I stand for. My fellow Americans, if you can give me back the youth I never had, remove the memories of war from my soul, give me the sleep that never comes, then I to will join you in your Quest to free a land (through trade relations) that I was, and still am being condemned for trying to free. You didn’t want us there then, so why do you want to be there now? Let us not forget, The American’s Creed, by William Tyler Page.

I believe in the United States of America as a government of the people, by the people, for the people, whose just powers are derived from the consent of the governed; a democracy in a republic; a sovereign Nation of many sovereign States; a perfect Union, one and inseparable, established upon those principles of freedom, equality, justice, and humanity for which American patriots sacrificed their lives and fortunes.

I therefore believe it is my duty to my country to love it; to support its Constitution; to obey its laws; to respect its flag; and to defend it against all enemies.

God bless America Harvey G Stanley USMC Proud to have served

Harvey
1943
CT, USA
