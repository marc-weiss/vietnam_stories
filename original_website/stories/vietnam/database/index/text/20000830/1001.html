My story of stories since the war is quite different. I did not serve in VN but the war was and still is close to my heart. Two of my brothers served in Vietnam. One brother rarely discussed his experiences as he refueled B52 bombers in the air over the war torn land and with the exception of a letter we received accomodating him for saving 5 US soldiers by rescueing them on the ground I don't know alot about his tour of VN, other than seeing his deep sadness and often staring eyes. My other brother was head of security at Tan Sa Naut(spelling - sorry) and Binh Hoa during his tour of VN. He regularly sent home cassette tapes talking about "everything". In particular he told of the children. He told of feeding them, sheltering them and trying to get them to safe places such as the orphanages when separated or orphaned from families.

How has the VN era affected me? Our mission in VN was not able to be successful due to constraints and red tape. So many Americans gave their life so that the future generations of VN would not have to suffer communism. I always knew I would adopt children and I made my choice Vietnam in order to make at least one soldiers life be taken not in vain and at least I would make an impact on one childs life and free him/her from communism. Well, I feel very fortunate to have two very wonderful daughters that I travelled to Vietnam to adopt. My now 3 year old adopted in 1997 from Phu Yen (Toa Town) and my baby was just adopted in 2000 from Dong Nai (Binh Hoa). These two trips were eye openers for me. I plan to return to Vietnam at year end to adopt 2 more children I met there during my recent adoption in early 2000. During this trip, I participated in a day of caring for the young people who live at the dump right outside HCMC. These children "the children of the dump" as they are called live there with their parents. The parents are about in their 30's and look like 80. Their children sport redish hair, brown hair, freckles and are such cuties under all the dirt and grime. I was happy to be able to give them the packages that were prepared for them. We only had 250 packages - not nearly enough to go around. So the era affected me personally in a very positive manner. I would not have these wonderful daughters who are 100% of my life.

Mary
