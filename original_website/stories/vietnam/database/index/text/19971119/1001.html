I was drafted into the Army Feb. 2,1970 and took basic training at Ft. Bragg, NC. from there I went to Ft. Polk, LA for infantry training. At this point I new I was going to Vietnam. After finishing AIT the Army picked the last few guys of our unit and said you are going to Jungle Survial Training in the Canal Zone for two weeks with the 5th Special Forces. Wow. Why me. This training in Panamal was a learning experience and help me survie in Vietnam. When I arrived in Vietnam in July, 1970 I was asigned to a Air Cavalry Unit and thought man this will be neat. I will not have to be a grunt and spend days on end beating the bush. I thought that the Army was going to make me a door gunner on a chopper but no such luck. My unit was B Troop, 3rd Squadron, 17th Air Cav. and they had what they called Aero Rifle Platoons. Our mission was to pull recon. mission on the Cambodian border. To make a longer story short, my unit was based out of Tay Ninh which is about 4 miles from the border. We were the last American Unit based at this base before it was given over the 5th ARVN division. I had a angle watching out over me the whole 14 months I was in Vietnam and I thank God that I did not have to spend one night out in the jungle like so many of my fellow friends did. I have been blessed with being able to go to Washington, DC three times and the see "The Wall". It was the most wonderful feeling of finaling being treated as if my time really meant something to this wonderful country I live in. I am able to forget the war and all the anguish it brought, but my heart goes out to all the veterans who were wounded or lost friends over there. They wake up everyday and can see what the war did to them and can not forget it because of the wounds, lost limbs, etc. that they experinced while they were in Vietnam. What every this country does it MOST NOT LET THE VIETNAM WARD be forgotten for future generations must learn from this terrible mistake the USA made my sending troops to fight a civil war that was unwinable. Thanks so much for your time and please keep your TV specials running on PBS. God Bless America. Joseph E.
