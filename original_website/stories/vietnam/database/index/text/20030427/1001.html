I served as editor/co-founder of the first American magazine to open a bureau in Hanoi, The Vietnam Business Journal. As a New Yorker, I got interested in Vietnam in 1993 because I didn't know anything about the country besides that it was America's only war-loss. The library had no further info and there were no web sites like this one. With journalism skills, I found a publisher and we started the mag in 94 -- by 96 I was living in Hanoi and explaining the ins and outs of doing business in Vietnam on behalf of a 70,000 readership comprised of business and government decision-makers, and members of academia. Fascinating experience (I returned to NYC in 99) and I learned much -- maybe too much -- about international business, Asia, etc. -- as well as how powerful Americans sometimes make the same mistakes during peacetime as they do during war (think arrogance over hearts and minds, and you'll have the difference between the winners and losers in international business). Anyway, given the point of this site I thought I'd share with you a bit of my writing and photos. Cheers, I hope it is interesting and relevant. Any questions etc. , don't hesitate to get in touch.
