My Buddies in Arms

My buddies in arms. So valiantly you fought, So bravely you wore you armor. You left me without a sound, Yet now I hear your laughter, And I look for you, but you're gone. You were so young, and you were here for such a short time. Even now the whisper of your life haunts me, and I walk through time as a shallow sometimes empty man. I've grown older, but My presence is not present. It is with you my friend who died so long ago with a gasp and eyes rolled back. So why can't I feel? Because my presence is not present. My presence is with you. You left me, and I never got to say goodbye to my friend, my comrade in arms. I'm searching, searching for you. Let me touch the Wall where your name is, and recall the face and the voice with a silly grin. Let me reach out to touch you, instead of a black granite slab. I'm searching for you, and searching for me.

Dennis Gardner USMC-Vietnam 1969
