And the silliness. All the novelty of being alive and impressed by the urgency of tremendous trifles." __________________________Siegfried Sasson "Memoirs of a Foxhunting Man"

There was always the stuff about the hill tribes and the C.I.A. I never knew how he got there or what kind of assignment or training it really was. We all heard it a little different. Questioning the details was not conducive to the telling and it took ten years to get that far. I believed that there was a lot of consistency to all of his versions. Something happened. Somebody didn't send air support. Somebody wrote him off and then wasn't too happy when he made it back in... Somebody told him he could never tell anyone and he went to Montreal for a while. The importance and the urgency seem so remote now.

S.T. Beety
