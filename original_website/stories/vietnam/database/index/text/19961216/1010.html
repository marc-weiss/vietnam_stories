Served at the 101 Airborne, ‘67 & ‘68, also with the First Aviation Brigade and the 528 medivac I was a ground medic. Well, I can’t, ...it’s very difficult.. I’m in LA, originally from NY.

I have liver cancer and I’m hanging in there... And sometimes, I am sorry that I survived Vietnam, but then again here I am. I have not gone to the wall in Washington DC, and some day, may be I will. I did go to a portable one in St. Petersburg, Florida.

I really don’t have much more to say. Thank Christ, we’re alive and we’re here. I lost a lot of buddies - I don’t even know who made it and who didn’t make it. There’s a tremendous void in my heart, a black spot. I’m proud to be a Vietnam vet and I display it and I support the POW and MIAs. And I hope all the other Vietnam vets are doing what they can to pick themslves up. We were strong then and we certainly can be now. I love all veterans of all wars and I’m proud to be an American.

Its very difficult...Thank God... Thank God... I don’t know what else to say

John Daniel Schirella

Los Angeles, CA
