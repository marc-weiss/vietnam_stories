I guess I am kinda unique: my Viet Nam experience still stands out as the most interesting year of my life. I spent all of 1967 as a door gunner on a "slick" helicopter. Our company was the air support for Special Forces. Even two emergency landings didn't dampen my enthusiasm for living on the edge!

My experiences reinforce the idea that nothing makes a person feel so alive as living in such a way that any moment may be his or her last. This is the same energy one feels in the urban jungle; just ask any gang member or biker. Or extreme sportsperson. Or any other hobby or occupation which has a possibly fatal outcome. War just happens to be the most officially sanctioned edge sport!

I have always seen life as ephemeral so am not too attached to it. Now, having been within inches of dying (both in Nam and the USA), I find death unfrightening. So it now irritates me no end that all people want is comfort and safety. Our culture is TOO safe. Risk adds character to people. No risk, no growth. Most entreprenuers understand this; the whole "be your own boss" story is a smokescreen. The thrill is in the risk.

Charlie Green
