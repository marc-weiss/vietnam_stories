It affected everything. I was a First Lieutenant in the Army, Signal Corps to be specific. Commissioned June 4, 1968. Trained to be a Small Unit Leader, Early on I specialized in Counter-Guerilla operations. Always my military schooling was proceeded by the phrase "You will need to know this when you get to Viet Nam!". The whole experience changed me - I lost several friends, part of my hearing, when on a training range, the person next to me fired a fully automatic burst from his M-16; it cost me my wife - we divorced over what was happening. . i didn't want to go - but I was caught up in system and received orders from Fort Hood for a "12 month unaccompanied short tour". I withdrew became bitter but turned to God; my marriage was over: children were out of the question - the love of my life was gone; politically I became much more tolerant and liberal; somehow I hung onto a good job on the faculty in a Medical School in Augusta, Georgia - but finally left because my ex-wife was still in the town; I am more hardened to life and death now but my religion and belief in God keeps me soft and in touch with a divine reality. Now i just feel empty and kinda' used up...i survived the whole period...now alone, I almost paid the full price, God saved me. Believe me, their are no Athesists at night in foxholes. Academically realizing that I had been caught up in a Civil War - I realized that I had backed the oppressor instead of helping those fighting to control their own destiny. I learned the hard way . . i was young, dumb and idealisitic - I was lucky I wasn't killed. i did nothing wrong, tried to help and protect. At 52, I am able to look straight in the mirror and live with myself. I have learned the hardway. Now i trust God for everything.


1945
