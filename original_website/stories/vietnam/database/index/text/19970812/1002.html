I've read the stories, looking for fellow veterans, especially American
Indians. I am one, a Sioux from South Dakota. My youth was tinged with
all the tinsel of our times, including all that flows from growing up on
the wrong side of the tracks. The Marine Corps means something, I joined
and found a part that was good. The war came, and I did my part with the
1st Battalion, 5th Marines, 1st Marine Division based out of Chu Lai in
1966. It was not John Wayne, it was sweat, hunger, danger, a time of
searching for the life strands that allow survival. The Marine Corps
changed, so did I. Civilian life is tough, sweat, hunger, danger, and
tears. My brothers, both Indian and non-Indian, have suffered, survived,
died, and yet here I am writing these lines to cyberspace. I wonder
sometimes about the winds of change, luck, those elements that we do not
know which allow us to continue with life, carrying these stories within
us. Survival is a tricky thing, and I am lucky in that way, and I
appreciate the good words I have read giving thanks for our service. I
send my prayers to you that your soul is soothed and you can live with
the shadows of your missing loved one.
We have a ceremony in Sioux culture called "Wiping of the Tears." Dr.
Arvol Looking Horse performed this ceremony three years ago in Mitchell,
South Dakota, at a gathering of Vietnam Veterans. The ceremony
reintroduced many of us to crying, that most simple of all human
emotions. It also reassured us that we are loved, we can if possible
expunge those dark feelings we Vietnam Veterans carry within us, and
pick up as we hear of friends still suffering from the effects of their
service in-country. I refer back to that eventful time and can feel good
when something is bothering me.
I attempt to make my peace everyday, with good thoughts and feelings
for everyone. I use these Sioux words, "Mitakuye Oyasin"--We are All
Relatives for it means that all of us in this universe are related, and
in that relationship we have responsibilities to everyone. I whisper
those words now for all who read these lines, and for all who exist in
our universe. Mitakuye Oyasin.

Tahunska Tanka--Big Leggins
