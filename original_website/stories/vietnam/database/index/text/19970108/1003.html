It was April 1967, My friend and I had arrived in Counry. We were assigned to the 9th Infantry division. My friend and I arrived at the unit, young, fresh from our initial training. We were in the army for six months. Newly traind helicopter mechanics. We dropped off at the Airfield, unsure of where to go or what to do. I was only eighteen years old. Suddenly from across the field came a jeep driven by a Young Captain.He was very aware of our feelings and did his very best to ease our anxiety. His friendy face was what we needed. He welcomed us to the unit, introduced us to our commander and unit personnel. He became our instant friend, a rock that we needed at the time. He made my friend and myself feel at home. The very next day this young captain flew a mission and was killed by the only bullet that hit the aircraft. I attended my first memorial service. This had a significant impact on me. The Friendship that this man expressed was probably his last. This event is eternally burned into my memory. This is just one of my stories and every time I go to a funeral or memoral service this true friend is in my thoughts. Personal friendship is hard for me to measure now. How can anyone be a better friend than that.

Wayne W. Thompson
1968
