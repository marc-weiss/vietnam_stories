I have this need to see your names, to feel this wall that tries to heal. I was barely born when this war was torn, yet I feel as a parent might feel when the loss of a child becomes real. I know not one of you nor do any of you know me. There is only one soldier from Vietnam in my life today. He is my uncle from my mother's home. He served his country, he did his tour. though he leads a normal life, his mindi is quite contour. No stories he does tell, just that he was in a living hell. Anger he builds with skill, blames himself for life's drills. No one to turn to for he talks no more of the days of war.

I have this need to finda a meaning for this war. After more than 22 years, one has yet to be found. So many names on the wall, so many living names to fall. At least twice the count of the dead or missing are here today, morning their family who went on this mission. To serve our country these boys did go, to fight for what they felt was right. They loved their county but the country did not care. How could people resent these boys sent to this place! Our own country blamed these kids, spit in their faces is what they did. These children did not want to go, the draft had come and they had to go. Those who said I must go, went to serve America to do what is right.

So many children without parents; so many wives without husbands; so many parents without children. So many lost, still too many missing.

God bless the nurses, they say the worst; the torn children, their insides on the out. These women had to hear these brave little boys speak their last words, breathe their last breath. So screwed up are they too, sent to heal these wounds of war, left to feel they can heal no more.

These wounds of war will never heal. It's time for this country to admit this war was real. We fought ourselves and in return, turned this world upside down. If you need an answer to this war, well... maybe God needed a bigger army of His own.

(i wrote this 10/1/1990)

Karen La Mar
1967
