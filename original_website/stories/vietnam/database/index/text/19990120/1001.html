Bobby and I were both born in July, 1952. We ran Miehle 50 printing presses next to each other at a small print shop outside Chicago, Illinois. One day in July, 1972 we anxiously waited for the foreman to tell us the results of the Lottery since we knew that the government was going to draw July numbers that day. He told someone in the office to listen to the radio and find out what happened. By that afternoon we were still waiting for the foreman to tell us something. Finally he came up to our presses, and told us that Bobby got a single-digit number like four or something (I don't remember exactly), and that I got number 320. I immediately felt a sense of relief, and just as quickly felt sorry for Bobby. He looked down and nodded his head as if he knew he would get such a low number. After a few words of consolation from his fellow printers, Bobby and the rest of us went back to work. Shortly, I heard Bobby's press make a weird sound and come to a stop. I looked around and saw Bobby with his right hand sucked into the press up to his wrist. He looked at me and repeatedly glanced at the flywheel with his brown Latino face which was now ghostly white. I was so shocked that I froze. The foreman ran over and stopped the press, and then backed off the flywheel, freeing Bobby's hand. The Miehle 50 set press uses steel forms that hold linotype lead typeset that runs up and down a steel roller which rotates as it holds the paper for impression. The steel form moves up and down quickly, about 60 times a minute. Bobby's hand was squished flat and was white, no blood. They quickly rushed him to the hospital. I never saw Bobby again. I left the printing company soon after the incident, and joined the Navy after hitch-hiking and riding the rails for a couple of years.

Chris Stockner
1952
