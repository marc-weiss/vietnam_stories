15 yrs old living on an AFB at Tachikawa AFB, Japan. A friend has a motorcycle accident that ends him up in the hospital on a ward full of GI's from Vietnam. A wise nurse who saw a child enthralled by the drug of the day (marijuana), lost to a world of worldliness and experimentation, places the child at the bedside of another child , approx 19 yrs old to be exact. The child has track marks all over his body including between the toes and fingers. This GI (child) was going through withdrawal. The influence it had? Never did the 15 yr old allow anyone to stick a needle in her arm. Not so for many of her future friends. The time spent on that ward looking at "souvenir" photo's of bodies and "Jewelry" made of human parts. Meeting a young GI who turned her head around. Coming to the horrible realization that even though he had surived one gunshot wound he had not been injured severely enough to allow him to go "HOME". He had to go back to play some more. Falling in love with the ideal of this young man, only to have her heart broken when she walked home on a beautiful warm Spring day, reading his letter, to enter her home and hear that a barracks had been bombed in the same city he had been sent back to. NEVER to hear from him again and forever to wonder ... what happened ... are you alive ? Did you make it? Do you remember me? I became and ER nurse. I couldn't save you but maybe I can save another child from the war of drugs and violence. Did it have an affect on my life? You tell me. Sheri

Sheri Sutton-Watson
