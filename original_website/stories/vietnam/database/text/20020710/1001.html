Obviously, I wasn't in the Vietnam War, but my father Theodore Earl Bosanco, Jr. was. Unfortunately, my dad passed away December 30, 2001. I'm 18 years old and always heard my mom tell me that dad had a horrible time in Vietnam, but dad would never talk about it. Recently, I was looking through old medical VA charts of my dad's and found things that just pierced me. He served twice in Vietnam. He did the first time and then 6 months after leaving the first time, he went back in because he didn't feel fit to be in the "civilized world". He hillusinated and one stated he saw a "burning corpse sitting on the counter". That terrified me. Dad went through so much, but would never talk about it. Dad hadn't drank in over 12 years but the time he did later on, he would talk about "charlie" and how "charlie was getting him". Then, how he watched his friend get blown up by a granade. I can't even begin to imagine how much terror he felt and how much sadness he saw. The Vietnam War I believe was one of the worst wars fought in history. The permanent damage done to the veternans will stay with them forever. I don't think we give enough thanks to them. The government sent them over, while the people back here in the US were against them. How dare those so called "Americans" stand against the soldiers. Even though I wasn't even thought of, it still upsets me greatly that they blamed the soldiers and not our government. Whoever they are, I would like them to experience what those soldiers experienced in war.

krystle Bosanco
1984
