I left Vietnam in 1979 on one of many boats that so call "the boat peole", we landed in Malaysia after some ordeal as being towed back to the sea,beaten by Malaysian Marines to get back to dry land again, and finally reached UNHCR transit camp, and 6 months later being accepted by Australians.

As a 20 years old young man travelling with 2 sisters (one older and one younger)to here,we value our daily life to Australia, the country that save us from misery, and the freedom that we are considered to be "lucky" as from a Buddhist "point of view" as "Good Karma".

My relationship here is trying to build a good term with everyone in the community, occasionally we heard some unpleasant "remarks" about the Vietnamese, however our community (the Vietnamese) has to be blamed also,and take it to the chin.

I have two sons , 9 and 10 year old ,they were born here and my greatest gratitude is Australia, I would not be able to give them a "good life" had we being back in VN. My mother settled here in 1984, with thanks to the "Australian family reunion" program, she is now a Buddhist monk and helping the "The Buddhist Association" as a counsellor within the Vietnamese community. She always telling us "with the life that you are enjoying now, is from a good Karma'seed that you have grown from the previous life. Do not forget that and try to grow more good seed."

My political view is always with the "Free World" particularly with the U.S, without their sacrifices and other Allies countries during the War, our lives will be different now because "repression, dictator and tyrant" will reign, I believe the "now" Vietnamese regime has some difficulties to justify their sacrifices to achieve what? Although it takes time to remove the opression. I think that matter will become reality as VN progressing toward a "market economy", as a means to "enrich uncle's Ho boys" whom motto is "No shame anymore with US dollars". The younger generation in VN does not believe their "struggle" and will send them to "Lenin graveyard" to join "Uncle Ho's paradise".

Thank you Australia to having me! and personally for all the Vietnam VETS, your involvement has giving us a better future, we cherish your action and please accept my greatfullness to all of you. Once again thank you all!

Melbourne, 27/4/03

Mr Thong Huynh
1959
