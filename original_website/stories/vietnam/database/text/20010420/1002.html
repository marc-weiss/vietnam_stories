I always looked at Carl as my "father". Even though he had a drug and alcohol problem, he was always a decent person to me. I tried to overlook the fact that he got drunk and pinned my mom on the floor.. always apologizing the next day. I also overlooked Carl waking up in the middle of the night crying and wiping himself off from cold sweats after having nightmares about the children he had to kill. His most substantial nightmare was seeing his best friend shot in the head right next to him. I make no ex

cuses for what Carl did in 'Nam. And I make no excuses for what he did after he came back from 'Nam. Carl had his chance. He enrolled in many drug and alcohol programs that the V.A. has. He just happened to be a Vietnam Veteran that could not get over PTSD. Carl felt guilty until the day he died. I hated Carl when he laid a hand on my mom, but I loved him when he was sober. Carl died 10 years ago. I still think about him and what the Vietnam War has done to "our boys". Not everyone that served in Vietnam is like Carl- I know that. Although, I'm sure there are a lot more 'night sweats' out there than anyone is letting there out to be. I was born in 1974, and care so much about Vietnam Vets. Probably more than your country did when you came home. I'm doing this for Carl and every other Vet that has felt displaced or dishonored. You are all honorable in my eyes.

trina
1974
milwaukee wisconsin
