It was December, 1969, I was 21 years old, I was, ( 11B2P), a combat infantryman with the 173rd Airborne Brigade. I remember the events as if they happened only yesterday, the job assigned to me was "pointman" for the third platoon. Sometimes I feel angry that I was sent to a (police action) that had no objectives, by a Congress which was influenced mostly by special interests ( arms makers ). Today, 27 years later, I have a wonderful wife of 25 years and three healthly children, ages 20, 18, & 15. I remember the huey helicopter rides over a crater-pockmarked terrain( we sat with our legs hanging out the sides, our thoughts far away. I can still hear the monsoon rains and feel the jungle rot on my hands as DOC scrubbed the open sores with a brush. I will always remember, hoping... no, praying that greed and ignorance never again delivesr our sons into such a hell. Jack Nobles

Jack Nobles
1949
