I became an RA on September 7,1967. Tool Basic at Ft. leoarnard Wood. Mo. & AIT at Ft.Lee, Va.,MOS 76y30 Supplman. Went to Okinawa, left & appear in Vietnam, 11/15/69. I was assigned to the 101st Abn. Div., first at Camp Evans(I Corps area), then I volunteer(left the safety of the base camp) to go with my artillery batter, B/2/319th Arty, whichwas in the A-Shau Valley, at Firebase Berchtesgaden. Just in time(May 10-20th,1969)for the Hamburger Hill Battle, supported our troops going up that Hill at all hours of the day & night. The Hamburger Hill position was right across the valley from us....at first the Hill was green, then it turned brown from all the airstrikes & artillery strikes on it. But, I hated the artillery after one month beig there, stinking hot atop a ridge, our asses ALWAYS working, 3 or 4 hours of sleep only, and if one got attacked by the NVA forces....there was no place to run....you stayed & fought it out. Alas, I had found out that the infantry companies had gotten their FO(Forward Obersver Teams)teams from the artillery batteries that were supporting them in the field.MY BREAK! I therefore requested(volunteer...again)of my battery comdr. to let me go and become an R.T.O(Radioman)for one of the infantry companies in the field. REQUEST GRANTED! Did I ever feel that I made a mistake in this request....Hell no! I should have gone to Fort Polk,La., to be a 11Bravo, instead of chosing to be a supplyman!!! well, I was assigned to Bravo Company, 2 Bn. 506th Inf.(B/2/506th Inf., to be short about it) . I like it better than being on a scary Firebase. Well, I humped 7 months in the field with this company, made many fire missions, combats assaults(CAs), humped the whole f______, A-SHAU VALLEY, from 6/68 - 12/69. Oh yes, went into cold and hot LZs(landing zones). Made i back to the states, forgot about the war till 1995, when I wrote a narration of m y experiences in Vietnam(La Villita Publications), as a matter of fact, also wrote short stories on the war(what really happen to me & what I didn't put in my narration under fiction), and had printed two picture albums from some of the photos I had taken(b&w;color) Also from La Villita Publications',East Chicago, In. 46312. So, my friends, I'll leave for now, this from a Sceaming Eagle....ALWAYS, even after DEATH!!!!!
