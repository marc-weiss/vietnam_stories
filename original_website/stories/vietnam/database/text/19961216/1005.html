I was a student during the Vietnam era and I find it difficult to address myself to the particular questions that were suggested. But I do feel compelled to comment on the Memorial itself. The first time that I was in Washington D.C. I bypassed the Memorial, not even aware that it was there. The second time, I was aware vaguely of its location, and all of a sudden happened upon it. I must say, I was not prepared for the emotional impact that the Memorial would have upon me. There was a tangible presence there, at the Memorial. I did not have friends or loved ones whom I lost in during the Vietnam war. And yet I suddenly felt linked to all of those. whose names were there upon that wall. And as I observed others who came to that wall. Some who came silently, others who wept, others who brought flowers. Those who were directly linked to that wall because they had someone who had meant something to them, whose name was there engraved on that wall. I felt a stirring within me that I was little prepared to understand or comprehend at the time. It was an overwhelming tangible presence. . .

Carol Hoffine
