I have never seen war, but have always fought against it. I never realized how much the Vietnam War affected me until I went to Vietnam for the first time to study Vietnamese language, art and culture. Only then, when I saw the devastation that war had caused, did I realize that some strange twist of fate had brought me to study a country that did not deserve so much pain yet suffered so much. It was only then that I realized that I had come to the right place, that Vietnam deserved to be studied for its culture and not for the fact that it had been a victim of American diplomacy gone wrong. Living in Vietnam has taught me that only Americans are interested in war. If we suffered as much, would we be interested in it? Wouldn't we want to paint and write and sing songs rather than fight?

Nora Taylor

Ithaca, NY
