The Wall

The
Black
Granite
Begins small,
Buried in grass
Covered dirt, it grows
Taller, goes deeper along
A brick v-shaped path, in the
Vertex, the dark heart of war,
Surrounded by the names, fingers
Touch, letter edges still sharp, smooth
Drops to rough, angular depressions, opposite
Of braille; Reflections move across columns of names
And each finger-rubbing face is mirrored back in polished black.

P.S. Cutler
