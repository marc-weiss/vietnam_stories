How Viet Nam affected me...

Having been born in 1963, my first awareness of Viet Nam and the fact that
we were in a conflict there was in the late 1960's, probably after the Tet
Offensive. My father was a combat veteran from WWII and as a history and
social studies teacher, was always anxious to get home to see the news of
the war. It was from watching the news with him, and seeing the combat
footage....along with hearing him talk about being in combat himself that
led me to believe at an early age that war was a normal state, and that
this nation was always at war. I just assumed from the age of 5 or 6 that
when I grew up I would join the army and go off to fight a war. I was
probably 11 or 12 before it hit me that war was anything but normal and
that God willing I would never have to fight in one.

Timothy Whitson
