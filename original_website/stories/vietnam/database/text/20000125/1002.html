I sat watching the images across the TV screen of Viet Nam and heard the narrators voice--eating sunflower seeds all the while. I can watch this now and feel nothing. Time has pasted, I eat and watch my soul not stirred, sad but it is the past now- no more crying for the lost. Than the picture of the memorial I remember standing in fron of it, not being able to read the names. Fearing to see those I knew for ever inbedded as a name in a rock wall. Still not tears, maybe the pain is finally over-still have yet to finishm, "Where have all the flowers gone?" after 20 years. Than the words, "My husband came home without his soul, it just took him seven years for his body to catch up. The tears slowly filled my eyes, oh, this pain is gone, is it not? My husband came home without his soul. "I do not believe in God". And drugs and alcohol became his life. We lost him to the emptiness of his soul of terriers unknow, buried beneth the drugs and alcohol. The tears are now gut wrentching- for me and our son, and for him, and all the others who have suffered for this war; the solder, sailor, pilt, wife, son and daugher, grandmother, grandfather, father, mother, sister, brother, and friend. Touched by the pain of this war. May the holes in our souls the saddness and emptiness of dreams not lived, be filled with God's love and comfort, filling us to reach for healing for ourselves and the people we love today and tomorrow.
