This is my second and last thoughts on the matter of the Wall and Maya Linn. I remain firm in what I said earlier, but also want to add - The Wall has been and remains an instrument used to come to terms with the terrible mistake of VN. I think it has allowed us to remember, regret and thank those who lost their lives. It has allowed us to separate these feelings from our legitimate anger towards those who lead us and took the 50,000+ men and women away from us, for no good reason. Anyone who has visited The Wall can't help but feel it is a holy and sacred place . . . the names . . . all those names . . . when you touch a name, you feel the solider's presence passing from the stone thru your fingers, down your arm and into your body. You weep and cry because you feel the solider wanting to escape, if just for a moment, so s/he could say to his family and friends - - 'good-bye, I love you, I miss you. . .' You look around and you listen. You know it is an experience shared by all those who visit this sacred. And, as you leave, the sadness and pain turns to anger, because there was no reason why those kids died. They did not die to save us from the "Commies." They did not die because of the irrational "domino effect." They died because a bunch of stupid leaders got caught in a 'mano-mano game,' and were not honest enough with the public and themselves to admit "fighting over the size their penis has nothing to do with anything." McNamara's book confirms this, after too many men and women died.
