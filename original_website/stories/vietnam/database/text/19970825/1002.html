There's too much to tell in the short period of time I have right now, Monday evening, August 25, 1997. I'm just browsing on the computers at the univ of Colo library...while I'm working on letters to solicit funding for my project, Time to Heal: Images and Interviews Afte the Vietnam Conflict. I plan to travel in Oct & Nov to South Korea, Vietnam (mostly from DMZ to the Delta), and Australia. Purpose: to take final photographs of memorials and locations throughout the region where the war was fought, in order to show AMericans what those locations we knew then, look like now. A type of therapy for the nation, by offering images that contrast with the memories of the past, or enable others to see what some of the locations look like that their loved ones lived, fought and died.

Especially unique is an art exhibit I'm putting on in recognition of the 30 years since the Tet Offensive, to be held at the Boulder Public Library, Jan 16-Feb 23, 1998. Along with community art from the wa effects, and memoribilia from all segments of the community, we will have an open forum to share our thoughts and feelings in a spirit of reconciliation. my idea is, we may still have views, and we can also understand and accept those perspectives of others with which we disagree, and we can bind old wounds which hae kept us separate for many years.

I've been to Vietnam twice ('89. '95), taught about the conflict at secondary and college level, developed curriculum materials/activites that are unique, and ben around attenpting to help educators and communities understand their way to healing is unique and individual. That's the way it's been for m, and that's only recently come about.

I was with an air force Foward Air Control team stationed with the 3rd Bde, 1st Inf Div, at Lai Khe, March-Sept '68, then with Adv Tm #55 in Rach Gia, in the Delta. Wonder why I'm alive after the stupid stuff that I did under orders. As a sgt, didn't have much to worry about, but taveled a lot in III and IV corps. Saw a lot, and got very disillusioned with the wast and mess I as finding myself a part of.

I've since found myself being vulnerable to abuse by veteran groups who say they want to help others, but are so dysfunctional that they can't help themselves. I think I've learned my leson to keep away from organized Vietnam vet gps. They are generally unhealthy, emotionally. If they wee doing OK, they wouldn't have a need to belong to the groups.

Raised in a military family, I was ready to get involved and make career out of the service. That wan't good, and I went to teaching children, then adults.

I've lost most of the time of my life due to being set up for PTSD as a kid, then having the icing layered on by Vietnam. From that time on, I've really not been a happy person, not made very good chioces in job selection or relationships. I don't trust much, and I'm effected by loss, so i don't get close. At least that's what was happening until I decided to change last MAy ('97). I'm doing quite well now, and got a $500 Director' Grant from the Colorado Council on the Arts July 24, for my project, Time to Heal: Images and Interviews After the Vietnam Conflict. It was then I decided to snap out of a low-grade depression and make a move for creating the funds needed to take this last trip for final photos.

I'm writing to Robt. McNamara, Oliver Stone, JAne Fonda, Ross Perot, and tons of others who might have a connection with this issue, asking for financial support. Thai Air is giving me a reduced ticket. Things are happening, and I don't know if it will all work out to travel or not, but retreat is not an option.

I'm not sure what else to say, as I'm needing to move on to other work at the moment.

if anything comes of this statement, I hope people will do something for Tet, '98, to help Ameica heal. It'll be done one by one, yet we need public activities for people to process in order to get healthy. I just hope things move in a positive direction. there's still so much crap out there as fallout from this time and place.

good luck. My address is: P,O, Box 2331, Boulder, CO 80306; 303/575-1023, if you want to contact me.
