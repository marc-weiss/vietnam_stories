The Vietnam War meant a level of protest and loss of faith and confidence in the government by even moderate people like me. While I never marched or protested, I saw this protest as the next logical step after the civil rights protests of a few earlier years. I brought to my attention the need to address some unfinished business in America; the corrupt use of the government for economic and political purposes. It planted a deep mistrust of authority and law enforcement that has not changed much over the years. They were wrong, why not just admit it?

The Watergate mess just confirmed how wrong it had all been. Morale was so bad on Coast Guard Base Boston that the picture of the Commander in Chief, then President Richard Nixon was stolen from the ceremonial post. It appeared trhe very next night on a photocopy. Above President Nixon's picture was a simple question; Would you buy a used car from this man? It made a huge hit on the base and required several more visits to the government copier. That was my handiwork in case anyone wants to know. Shortly after that they appointed me to the base Shore Patrol/Security.

I served in the military during Vietnam and almost got orders for the place. We gave away a Coast Guasrd Cutter which was sent to the yards in Baltimore for a complete overhaul before being given to The South Vietnam Navy. Yet we had Coast Guard ships doing real duty that couldn't get into the yards for repairs. It just didn't make sense.

When I got out what we were subject to was unreal. I was called a "baby killer" by a white middle class college student in Boston just because I happened to be on duty in uniform under direct orders to go carry out Coast Guard business. I was a part time EMT on the side so I didn"t like that at all. I was training hard to save lives! That was just the beginning of hurts. A few months later I got out. There were no jobs for me in what I knew how to do. All I could get was security guard minimum wage work. Eventually I did go back to school and retrain, but it messed up my jobs for a long time. The people who had not been in the military had all the good jobs it seemed. There was no recognition of the veterans at all; it seemed a lot of people would prefer we just went away. I have never seen America own up to anything about Vietnam. Not that it would help now, why bother. The Gulf War Veterans got their well deserve welcome back and I am glad they did.

I see that the Vietnam War destroyed the sense of common values and shared traditions. It tore the country apart. Since the issue was never addressed, it only got worse. Could this perhaps be a part of the drugs, illicit sex and shattered families that so divide America today? That is my view any how as a small town moderate church going citizen who has served his community as a volunteer in fire, EMS and police roles. We have unfinished business to clean up the inequalities and corruption that still remain here in America. The choice is ours as if we don"t then this nation was changed forever....

Mark Gaines BSN RN RRT Critical Care Nurse
