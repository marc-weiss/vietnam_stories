This is sort of odd to me; putting my thoughts out there for whoever. But I shall try. I am 27 years old and wasn't even born during the Vietnam War but I have many thoughts about it. This is really not a story but a simple message to those who fought for our country. I work as a paramedic and happen to see death quite often which can at times be disturbing. And everytime I have a bad call that makes me want to cry or freeze I think of our vets. I've witnessed many murderous scenes though I know it wouldn't touch a warrior's memory. After graduating high school I signed for the Navy to try to enter the SEALS. A friend of mine went and made the first cut but his papers got screwed and he ended up on a ship and that didn't really appeal to me (being on a ship if shit happened). So I didn't go but it sparked my interest in the military. Had I known what the Army consisted of I would have gone there but there was also the most beautiful green-eyed girl that interested me more. I began reading on Vietnam and only non-fiction books. I found myself trying to get my hands on any material that I could, and still do. There is nothing more that I would rather do than read of the war and hear the stories of the men. Please let me say though, it is NOT for the shooting and the bloodshed. It is the reading of the friendships and the comraderie of the guys that many say only war can inject. It amazes me too that most were young but stepped to the plate and did what they had to do. I think back whan I was 19 and wonder if I would have had the balls that those guys did. I've read mainly of the LRRPs, Force Recon and members of SOG. And those experiences that I have read just makes me appreciate life. It gives me a different outlook on the generation before. And it makes the words "thank you" come to mind. I know many people have different views of the Vietnam War just as I have my own view. But the way I see it and this is my opinion which means little. Right or wrong, the war came to be and people were sent to fight. Many for something they believed in and others I'm sure cared less. When reading of all of those guys far away from home and wondering whether they would live or die it makes me proud to be who I am. And proud of where I come from. I have a deep respect for out veterans and I feel they deserve our respect. Truly honorable people. I don't know if anything that I've said makes sense or not. The point to me writing this is to say "my" thanks. That maybe some veteran might see this and know that to one person the many writings have made a difference in everyday life. When I think I'm in pain or the world is coming to an end in a sense, I think of the guys I've read about. I haven't known real pain. I haven't feared for my life or held a friend while he passed from this life. I know reading doesn't make me know how it was or what went on over there. But I do know how the stories make me feel. It makes me feel for the people that had to experience that. This has been my thanks and I hope it doesn't sound too dumb. It's hard to express thoughts about something like this I guess. Thank you for your time and for those of who have gone there and returned, you are appreciated by one. Charley
