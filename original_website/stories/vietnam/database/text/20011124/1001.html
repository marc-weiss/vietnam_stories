I was fire direction control for the 25th Divarty at Cu Chi in the fall of "67". We got mortared every night, sometimes several times a night , and we backplotted the rounds and found they were coming from the friendly little village , just outside our SE gate. We even warned them , but still it came. This night we lost 13 men. One of them our medic. Guy named Swift. An indian from Oklahoma, my best friend. A drunk officer came in, ordered me to fire the mission. I told him there were " Rules of Engagement" but he put a loaded 45 to my head and insisted. I did some volunteer work at one of the orphanagesin the village, I knew the kids and the sister that ran it. I told them what my job was on the base and I wonder , as they were getting blown out of their beds and straight into hell , that it was me who was doing it. I felt like shit until I finally realized that I died that night too. I walk around people who will still talk to me , but I'm numb inside. I can't love or be loved. Somebody please do me a favor and put a bullet in my head.

Jack Nunn
1947
Lexington,NC,USA
