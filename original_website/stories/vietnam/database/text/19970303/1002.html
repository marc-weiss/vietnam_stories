REFLECTIONS FROM THIS SIDE OF THE WALL


I stand and read the names chiseled in cold black stone
Each a life incomplete on this side.
Those who remain spend their lives wondering what difference you would have
made.
Those of you remembered here,
in old yellow photographs,
flimsy newspaper articles,
and dog eared high school annuals.
Vague memories of last words spoken before stepping onto a bus into eternity
Bundles of cherished letters,
the print mostly worn away by time and tears
read to those whose lives are changed by those remembered here.
These children who would be soldiers
selected by a date now painfully remembered as their joyous birth
to fight an unpopular war that no one could win.
Fifty-eight thousand died,
so that a select group of politicians could share a few lines in
history.
What remains for those left behind?
The mothers and fathers
the brothers and sisters,
the wives and children,
the lovers...
Were they remembered on those lines of history,
do they ever forget their losses,
does time truly heal?
I wonder if those who cross over weep for those who made them sacrifice,
the warrior spirit belongs to them.
I bid them peace in Heaven,
their hell has ended.

Rock Hilton

Claremont, NC
