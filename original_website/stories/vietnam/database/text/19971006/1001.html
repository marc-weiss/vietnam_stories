The Hunter and the Hunted

Imagine yourself as a grunt who is out in the jungle and hunting human beings. Ironically, while you are the hunter, your opponent is hunting you. You are the hunted. The consequences of failing to detect your quarry can put you in a world of hurt in a hurry. In less than ten seconds, your world can change forever.

Lying in the inky pitch black jungle darkness you cannot see. You are alone. Face down you lie on the dead and decaying rotting plants, animals, insects, flukes, leeches, mosquitoes, centipedes, and whatever else goes bump in the night. The smell of your own fear emanates from your pores. You smell the jungle rot and creeping crud of your body and of your buddies.

You think and hope nothing out there knows you're here. Your thoughts race through your mind. "What if they sense me first? What will happen to me? Will I see the morning sun? Will I make it through the night" When the shit hits the fan, can I make myself so small that I can fit under a postage stamp I hope so? I wish I were home and not here. Why me, Lord?"

There is nothing but night noises to keep you company. Tree frogs, insects, monkeys call out. Calling to one another and letting their own kind know all is well. Calling and letting each one know it is safe. But they never call out to you as you lay there waiting, listening, reaching out with all of your senses. Your other self reaches out of its body and projects to sense, feel, hear, and "see" what is around you. The black void exists. You're standing at the door of the abyss that once entered can never be returned from. You wonder silently, "How the hell did Dante do it?

Suddenly you hear something. The jungle night noises stop as a cone of silence approaches in the ink black night. The tension mouunts as something, someone, anything approaches the kill zone of your ambush site. Something is out there in the darkness. "It" has a darkness to it that you cannot see. It is a void of emptiness that doesn't exist, yet somehow it is there existing.

Now your worse fears come to life in an instant. "Is it a trick? Are me and my team going to be hit by a counter- ambush? Are we the ones in the trap? Are we going to die?" Your fear mounts and you try to stay calm. You strain in the dark to "see". There is nothing but the big black and the noise. Or is it noise?

You pull on the string tied to your partner next to you. You want to let him know that "it" is happening. You feel the string tug back. This thin umbilical cord is your only connection to the human race while you lie there at the brink of death's door. The steel scythe of the Grim Reaper is raised and about to come down for his harvest. Your racing mind goes on, "I hope it isn't me. I hope it's them."

Now your whole body and spirit cries out into the night. "Let them experience that ten seconds of the unnatural violent man made hell that comes from tripping an ambush. Let them feel the hot steel from the Claymore mines and the grenade. Let them feel the stinging zip of the steel bee that passes by your head or hits you and tears flesh and blood asunder. Let it be them that faces this violent act. Let them scream and die, but let me live."

But something is out there. Moving through the jungle. "It's the Boogeytman! Can you hear it? Is it real?" There is something and yet there is nothing. You hear the insects and animals go silent. The void of silence which is "It" moves across your front, nothing...yet something. "Is it live or is it Memorex?" your mind flashes out in morbid humor. "What is it? Can it be the bad guys or is it just a big cat of some type walking and hunting, crossing you zone of death. What is it? Listen...it stopped. Ahh, shit, we are gonna die!"

Your fears mount. Your body tenses up and the dark finality of death's chill comes over you. You try to conquer this and say, "I'm doing what I've been trained to do. Have no feelings and do the deed. Don't think. Just kill your enemy. But is it your enemy? I don't know."

Listen...the silence is moving away. The insects surrounding its being and essence start in again. The silence moves off into the black void of the night. There is peace in the jungle again. Yet is there peace? Your heart and spirit are trapped here. Hunting and hunted.

Will you ever forget the unnamed horror that prowls out6 there in the darki or will you always have to see its unnamed face in your dreams forever.

They say time cleanses all things. But to this day he sits there, "It". He just prowls around somewhere in the back of my mind, waiting, stalking, hunting. "It" comes from the darkness of a time gone past and hopefully is best forgotten. "It" just waits there, patiently stalking.

Sometimes "It" comes in my dreams, sometimes he'll just sit there on the edge. He is my worse nightmare. He is terror, death, horror. He is fear.

Yet he has taught me something invaluable. He taught me how to cope and understand. He taught me the meaning of life and how precious it is. He is the point and the counter-point of existence.

Without "It" you cannot exist because you must know fear to live in the jungle called life. As Walt Kelly's Pogo said, "We have met the enemy and he is us."

Without him we could not understand what it is to be alive and human. Without experiencing "It", we would not know and appreciate the meaning of fear and our nightmares.

He is always there. I know him and understand him. Maybe someday he will surface again. I hope not. But until then I will value what he has taught me. You see, "It" is my enemy and my friend, the hunter and the hunted.
