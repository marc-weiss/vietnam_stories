I was too young to be a part of the Vietnam War in any real way. The only personal involvement with it that I had occurred every evening at the dinner table when we would add a prayer for "the men in Vietnam" to our nightly supplications. I didn't realize at the time that Vietnam was a place; I thought it was some kind of very arduous state of being. Now I can see that it was both.

When I went to see the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in Washington in 1986, I was overcome by its profound sadness. It creates a public space for the most personal grief. In my experience, only natural landscapes and gothic cathedrals had successfully accomplished such a task before I saw Maya Lin's work.

I was riveted to your documentary about her. Her singularity of purpose and simplicity of intent is tremendously inspiring. Thank you so much.
