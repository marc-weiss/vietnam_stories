Letter to the Editor, New Mexican 11-8-1996

A VETERANS' DAY THOUGHT

Two weeks ago i visited the Vietnam Memorial Wall for the first time, and it happened to be the day after my 49th birthday. My high school and college years were filled with war news, war horrors, war photos, war tragedy. Friends from school were lost there. The computer at the Wall showed 48 died from my home town, Colorado Springs.....

The Sunday after i returned home, the insert in the newspaper contained an article on the Vietnam Memorial Wall and Maya Lin, whose truly inspired work has impacted us beyond words. But i was surprised to read her comments on Glenna Goodacre's sculpture for the Vietnam nurses --- 'misguided in its attempt...to cover all grounds. It's sad that (critics of the wall) felt that was what they needed. It's very badly thought out.' And since then, several people asked me if i, too, had thought the sculpture detracted from the Wall. ABSOLUTELY NOT!!! i had seen the small edition of Glenna's statue at Fenn Gallery, but was not prepared for the impact of the 'real' one....

My husband was in Vietnam for nearly 3 years in the early 70's; he was a medic, and doesn't speak of it often. It is in the past, and he has gone on. i had wondered what it would be like to visit the Wall, and i was very much looking forward to it! A beautiful autumn day, and i was one of dozens of others to look and touch and pray. A true impact, but the emotions weren't as strong as i had though they would be. i was with my aunt, whose children are also my age.

We then walked into the trees to see the Women's Memorial. It is tucked away, small in respect to the Wall, and very unobtrusive. Then we began walking around it; only a couple of others were there at the time. The impact of the life-size figures -- the love, concern, and caring on the faces of the nurses -- the wounded soldier -- it put us right there.

Tears began to swell in my eyes, just feeling what they were feeling... And then i looked down across the grass to the wall, looked at all the names of those of flesh and blood, not bronze, who had died there........ the flood of tears finally came........... the Memorial, the memories, were now complete...

Before anyone else criticizes the choice of either the Statue or it's location, i ask them to visit both sites and then make a decision.

Sincerely, Annie Whitney Downey Santa Fe, NM

Annie Whitney-Downey
