I wrote this the first time I saw the Wall..

silent stone

the souls cry from the silent stone names row on row an obsidian slash that will never speak of crimson mortality.... the lonely three, look to see, if they have joined their brothers in stone or move to the next ambush... tourists gaze, and silent-spoke for the missing and the dead mourning sacrifice for some unknown freedom's sake.. vets leave the darkness from their souls here relief from enduring agony... the Cong have no stone as this valiant as any troopers in millenia fought three armies and won no freedom a hollow victory at best... is there always war for man? where will the next slash go? only God knows...

Lawrence E. Nuckolls Co. B, 2nd Bn., 22nd Inf. (M), 25th Infantry Division November 1969- November 1970 / Vietnam and Cambodia 81mm mortar crewman on M113 APC

Lawrence E. Nuckolls
