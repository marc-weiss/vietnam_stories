I was in Viet Nam in 1961 and 62. I was in a helicopter company. Since the service, I have worked in various jobs. In my late 30s, I realized that I would have to deal with my experiences in the service. I went back to school, got a couple of Masters degrees, and was ordained an Episcopal Priest and acquired a degree in social work. I now work in a hospice to care for the terminally ill.

I serious feel that my war experience has moved me to work in the hospice arena. And, it has helped me to now work in the human service field.

Shortly after returning from the war, I read a great deal about the history of the country. I came to question our country's motives for being in the war. And, I became active working against our involvement in that war.

I believe that my philosophy is apparent from the field that I am working and my role as a priest.

The process of this site, and the reality created by the Wall has been very meaningful to me--thanks
