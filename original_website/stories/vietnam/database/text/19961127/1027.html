I was attached to the Naval Support Activity, DaNang, RVN 1968-69,with additional duty at Chu Viet, and other detachments of the Naval Support Activity. The killing and bodies are still vivid in my mind every time I see a killing on TV. I DO NOT own a gun, and probably never will unless it becomes critical to protect my home. I think it was easier on me than many of my friends in that I had served in Korea as an assault boat coxswasin during landings in Pusan. Our mission at Naval Support DaNang was to provide Logistic support to U.S. Marines fighting in I Corps, the only reason the U.S. Navy was incountry in the northern part of the country.

I served 34 years active duty in the U.S. Navy, and I believe that background hardened me to what the protestors were doing in the United States, while many of our comrades were unable to accept such non-support by Americans.

My greatest problem today is the anger I have when I see our draft dodging President on TV in ANY setting that has our military involved. His appearance at Arlington National Cemetary on Veteran's Day really upset me whern it is a given that we have combat veterans living in cardboard boxes on heating grates while we have a draft dodger living in the White House. I actually hate Clinton, and as a Christian that bothers me. I can't think of any other person in the world that I hate except this man. He is a radical product of the 60s, and that crowd is doing its best to destroy this country and turn it into a Liberal, free lunch for everyone, society.

Bill Paschall
