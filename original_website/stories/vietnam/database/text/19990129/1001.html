returning veteran persecution is a common theme, they returned and were spit on, called baby-killers, disrespected etc. But when the anti-war activities really first began to have momentum, these same veterans on their way out would routinely beat us (the anti-war vocals) and shave our heads (if we had beatle-hair etc) and call us ridiculous names such as pothead and faggot. I can still remember BOB HOPE during half-time on the SUPER BOWL saying that you didn't have to listen to those who didn't want to escalate our involvement in Vietnam because they NEEDED TO TAKE A BATH (HA HA!) That was the level of debate the proponents of this monstrous atrocity were living for. People who don't want to kick ass in Vietnam needed to take a bath. You would see these macho shit-heads on the return ( the lucky ones) and they didn't talk so loud. all of them were sullen and hollow eyed. full of bewilderment. Others would come back for help before they went, having come to their senses and gone a.w.o.l.- "where can I hide? etc. where is the fake ID?" they were sullen and hollow eyed too. The real tragedy is that our whole version of reality was exposed as complete horse-manure. The freedom country told us we couldn't go to school if we didn't cut our hair, and if we didn't go to school we would be jailed. I could not graduate with my high school class because I forgot my tennishoes one day. It was in the rooms of these bizarre schools that the anti-war movement really began to be weirdly powerful in exposing our parents and leaders as deluded or as monstrous war-hungry profiteers... because little miss prissy proper whose parents ran the local church and republican clubs etc would get assigned to do a research paper on "why we are in vietnam" and she would get up and give a report before the class that basically she'd done all the research she possibly could and concluded that we were not there for any good reason that she could think of, and the government's case for being there and escalating the death was riddled with lies and further that the lies were so obvious that the people behind them had no respect for anyone's intelligence and would say anything... in her opinion. My father was a pearl harbor survivor and a 23 year navy vet... I was groomed from minute one to be a marine.. but I would not fight for these pigs. and they were and remain pigs. I wanted to bomb the draft board and police stations, but got drunk instead. I am just a few years sober now, but wake up screaming routinely from traumatic memories of demonstrations and riots and idiots-from-hell in charge of your life, willing to kill us all to get rich selling napalm and copters... KNOWING all the time FULL WELL that the war could not be won, and indeed having no intentions of winning it... only of selling more weapons and death. Traumatic memories surface hourly, cause me to gasp and / or scream or shout rude or violent things inappropriately, then bury themselves back in my mind, leaving me to explain my behaviour to those around. what do I say? You don't have to have GONE to the damn war to have post-traumatic-stress-syndrome and related behavioural problems. God bless you all. God bless us all. be the peace. Larry.
