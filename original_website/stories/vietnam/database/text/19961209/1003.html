In 1967, I was 20 years old. Today, I am writing this installment at the age of 49. 1 have changed in many ways but most dramatically in the area of expression. Feelings that were buried for many years finally came to the surface when I was 40. Sitting with a counselor at our regional VA Hospital, I wept aloud and bitterly, reacting at last to the insanity that was Viet Nam.

Three years ago, in Grant Park, Chicago, I watched an old Vietnamese gentleman shuffling along the waterfront. Dragging a foot as result of an injury suffered perhaps long ago, in another land, I thought to myself. It could have been from one of my bombs. I felt a strange kinship to the elder Asian but I also felt very sad and very ashamed. My eyes overflowed with tears.

Yes, I have changed.

Gerald E. Logan

"VIETNAM CRUISE" is the book I am writing about my experiences aboard the aircraft carrier U.S.S. ORISKANY (CVA-34) while attached to photo reconnaissance squadron VPP-63, 1967-68.

Drawing on the diary I kept, the narrative integrates the perspectives of the two Gerry Logans: the 20- year-old and today's 49-year-old.
