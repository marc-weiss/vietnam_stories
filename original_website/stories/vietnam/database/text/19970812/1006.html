My grandfather served in one of the "big"wars,he was gassed and left in
a pile of dead soldiers,they found him when they began to bury the dead,
for his actions he received a purple heart.
My great uncle served in WW2 as a gunnery mate on a ship,it was hit and
he was injured-but he told his mother all was well,he didn't want her to
worry.He carries today shrapnel near his spine.
My aunt lost her brother in the second world war,he left on an air
mission and didn't return,she named her first son after him.
My uncle served in World War 2,my father was in the Korean
Conflict(never was declared a war).
I skipped school in 1972 to watch as the first soldiers came home from
Vietnam,I thought it was a beautiful thing and didn't understand the
thoughts and feeling of others.
I can never know your pain,anguish,suffering nor heartache,that you have
endured these long years.
But please know this-
I thank you and value my freedom that you and the others before you made
possible.And you may never hear it often enough and wonder if anyone
cared,we did and do.
Thank you.
