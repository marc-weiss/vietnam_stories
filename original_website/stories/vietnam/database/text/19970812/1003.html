My father, was in the 82nd airborne he voluntered when his cousin was
killed in the Normandy invasion. My father has this Irish fighting
spirit and he is very proud of the WWII accomlishments and sacrifices
of his generation. I wasn't going to go to Viet Nam my brother who was
4 years older had already broke ground with his CO and earlier deferment
for college, There was a constant battle when my dad got home from work
and laterwhen my mom brought up a story about my father, something he
did while still in the service but after the war, stateside, it was an
example of the rebellious, human nature and thumbing your nose at a
system that didn't mean much at the time, there were repercussions for
my fathers actions then and the humour that is a well kept family
secret brought out a comradeship that united our family at a time when
neighbors were divided,schools were battlegrounds , and others were
watching Bonanza.

Bernard Gray
