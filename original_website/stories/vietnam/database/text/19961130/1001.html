Friends: I just finished watching the POV program on Maya Lin. I was greatly moved by the portrayal of her efforts and the building of the wall and how it speaks to and for me. I was in Thailand in the Peace Corps from early 63 to Spring of 65 and watched with mounting horror and anger the buildup to the carnage to come. I happened to know the families of two or three USArmy helicopter pilots serving in Vietnam, the families living in Bangkok. Through them, I met their husbands and had a chance to learn what their lives were like, how high the casulty rates were and the irony of them not receiving combat pay. As I listened to their fears and frightened racism, I wondered what were we becoming and how would this finally end since we had so clearly set loose the tiger. Maya Lin provided the vision of that ending, the calming of the tiger. Through her vision and the efforts of many the chances are slighly improved that we won't forget. Thanks to PBS for the program

Michael Mery
