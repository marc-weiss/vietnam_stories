The nightmares have finally stopped-

I lived with a Vietnam vet for nine years, I understand what it means to hurt, or at least I always though I did. Carl died when I was 16 years old. He was my father figure; he was the one I always went to for advice, for anything. He was my father when my father wasn't there. Every time Carl hit my mom I hated him more. Every time Carl creeped into are lives... I hated him more. Although he taught me certain things that nobody else would teach you like "the Mongolian Death Grip", and other ways to keep yourself out of trouble. He was a fine man. Its just too bad that he was bitten by the Agent Orange bug, and with that came his addiction to alcohol, drugs, and anything else that helped him to his demise.

God bless his and every other Vietnam Veterans soul... Those who have come home or not...

Soul surviving “kin”

TG
