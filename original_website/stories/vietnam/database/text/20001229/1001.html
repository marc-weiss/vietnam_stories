being a vietnam veteran has given me a keener eye on the scapegoat game and a full view of the heirarchy...and the pains that can be brought on by a devided nation...much like the cival war...in retrospect it changed my life and for a long while I experienced a loss of sense of humour and felt aleinated from old peers and much of the family...I wore a cross for good luck all the time I was there....but somehow all the values I looked upon for solace in christianity just wasn't there anymore...it was as though all the foundations had been broken and I felt keenly aware that life isn't fair and at the same time life is a privilege and not a right...I simply couldn't accept my old ways of Jon boy on Waltons mountain...ironically I feel I have a deeper passion for life and I see people in a different way...I have learned that we must own and claim our anger before we can heal and as a writer it has been extremely hard for me to tell the dark side...and here again is the irony that as I have arrived in my fifties I am finding that I can complain and not be ashamed to be angry or hurt....The Maya Lin documentary really touched me and had a role in me facing my hurt side and anger side...more irony Maya Lin designs a wall that cuts right through the earth with its simplistic view...and more irony that in my case it would be a woman my wife who helped me see that she was willing to give me her respect....I am an artist and have come to peace in knowing that I work alone alot and have now more than ever a sense of self and other...in my case I feel like our body is where we begin with politics... how we treat our bodies in marriage making love is a act of changing and tuning ourselves to the drumming of life..the phrase the body of politics is no accident...I know what it is like to not feel and share love ...it is cold...I know that my feelings completely just turned off for quite sometime and I feel lucky to have made it through that empty phase of my life and I can write and paint and love loved ones...thank you vietnam veterans and veterans from all wars we are different because of that war and I am thankful to be alive to tell my story thank you for listening...Gary Alan Brumley
