I kept a diary when in Vietnam during 1969 and 1970. We were truck drivers, but had many extra duties such as patrolling outside our base camps. Little has been written about support personnel, and it's time they need to have their stories told. Sergeant William Seay in my unit received the Medal of Honor (See Vietnam magazine, April-99 issue), for example. I have a website and published book about the "Orient Express" and they tell the untold stories of those men I served with and respected in Vietnam over thirty years ago. More info is at URL://www.angelfire.com/la/domino or Barnes and Noble: "Don's Nam" or Amazon.com: "Don's Nam."

Franklin D. Rast
