Although I was not in Vietnam or even born for that matter I have a great understanding and pride for the men and women who fought and died there.My father was there and I have spoken with him about Vietnam. He told me what the reality of war is. One thing that i have learned is courage isn't just rushing into incoming enemy fire to save that guy laying there, courage is doing something even though you are scared to death you can't leave a man out there to die because if you don't you will have to live with it forever. Thre is a bond between all fighting soldiers. Even if they aren't great friends they would risk thier life for thier buddy laying out there. Any man who has been in combat should be given a pat on the back. The veterens of Vietnam were treated poorly.If it helps any I think of you men who fought side by side with your fellow man are true men with honor. Another thing I learned from my Dad is not everyone hates the enemy. The enemy is doing the same thing you are except for a diferent side.They also have families,children,wives and friends. Fighting him is your job and fight you is his. To all the American Vets of every war I extend my Thank you to you.
