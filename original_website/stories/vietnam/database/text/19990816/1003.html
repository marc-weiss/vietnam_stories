To whom it may concern:

Vietnam was the place that changed my entire outlook on life! It, not only, succeeded in destroying my until-then indestructible moral values, but, also, managed to change my personalilty, as well. However, it was very subtle for I kept it to myself, i.e., the traumas and horrors that I participated in. I, somehow, survived that crap of obeying incompetent assholes, only to be used later as a live cadaver for some incompetent medical doctors, trainees at that! I suffered a drastic personality change as a direct result of that botched brain surgery which turned me into an epileptic, and traumatically brain injured me ("mildly"), as well. However, not a soul, including my ex-wife told me about it. The medical people "forgot" to tell me that I suffered a stroke, which was the cause of my grande mal epileptic seizures!

Had I only known that I suffered a stroke that was THE cause of my seizures, I, DEFINITELY, would not, repeat not, have returned to PA, the state of my birth. I went from surviving the horrors of Vietnam, the walking guard with no ammo---in a war zone, where I was used as live bait for the VC, to be used, AGAIN, as a live cadaver for the failed experimentation of some neophyte, trainee neurosurgeons. "Simply" because I had the great misfortune of being a Vietnam veteran (of which, I still am ashamed!), did I choose the VA to repair any and all damage that was received because of a completely unannounced AVM (Arteriovenous Malformation). Since I was comatose at the time, I don't remember even going in there. The rupture took place on 6 Feb 72. The surgery transpired on 15 Feb 72. This then, was the "reward" this somehow honorably discharged veteran received for risking my priceless life in a totally unwinnable and immmoral war. The acronym, "UUUU" carries special significance for me. It stands for: "The Unwilling, led by the Unqualified, doing the Unnecessary, for the Ungrateful." However, that was in vogue before I "visited" there, in 1965. I, "merely" had "FTA" on my gear. I don't have to tell you what the "F" stood for, but I can tell you that the "TA" stands for "The Army." It still goes!

The traumatically brain injured Dick Dettrey
