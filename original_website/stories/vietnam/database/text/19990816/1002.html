Even though I was born in 1980 and my brother was born in 1984, our father's experiences on the USS Henry W. Tucker during the Vietnam War have left a lasting mark on our lives. Throughout our fairly short lives, my brother and I have been fearful to ask about "the war" for fear of hurting our father. We are very proud of our father, but we find it hard to express our gratitude without reminding him of the friends he lost or what it felt like to hold your breath while the enemy walked past. Just recently, our father has begun to share stories with us. I feel the war stole a part of our father from us. Due to the war, my brother and I have no real idea what our father was like as a carefree young man because he has carried the horrors of war with him for thirty years now. Sometimes I wish I could've have known him before he went to Vietnam, but even my mother didn't know him then. Other times, I wonder what our father would be like if he hadn't gone to war, but then, I wonder if one of the pilots my father helped to rescue would have lived to get home to his family and I am glad to have shared my father. To all of the Vietnam Veterans and their families out there, I would like to say thank you. I know first-hand what it is like to live with and to love a Veteran. On behalf of our generation, my brother and I would like to publicly thank our father, David, for all of his sacrifices through the years. We'd also like to thank all Veterans from all sides from all wars. Thanks daddy!!!!
