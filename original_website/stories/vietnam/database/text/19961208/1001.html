Columbus Day Weekend, 1996

Jim made it to Washington again this weekend, A city he loved as a child and as a vigorous young man. Our trip there five years ago was a bust: He could not reclaim the child or the vigor. But here we are again-- ...Jim looking out over the Mall.

We had walked the Mall from end to end. Visited the Viet Nam Memorial at night, me Claiming a mental image (flash not allowed) Of the angle where two sections of the Wall Came together, reflecting the hot August moon and the Washington Monument.

Walking away from the Wall through the darkness I was frightened -- Frightened for our safety in an unsafe city; Feeling the fright of young men in a distant country (Bill Dodd's name WAS there) Not knowing where the enemy was in the darkness Feet rotting in wet boots on a similar August night, Hot and humid. And the man walking beside me-- Victim of another unseen enemy.

Now, for a weekend, Jim's memory is in the sun and among the comrades of HIS war. This time he has a face and an identity: "Founder, HIV and AIDS Education Foundation". We should have put CEO on that panel-- It was the title he gave himself As Captain in this 'Conflict'.

My mother watches from the hill at Arlington, "Wife of..." on her headstone. (PFC Sara Frances Ward, U.S. Women's Army Corps, WWII)

Three generations of war scars In Washington, D.C., City of my birth.

Pat Nolan
1946
Urbana, IL
