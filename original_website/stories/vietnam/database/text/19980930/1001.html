Not a day has gone by since I returned from Vietnam in April 1970 that I haven't thought about Vietnam in one way or another. I was never afraid to talk about what I did as an infantryman & combat medic during the war, but I never really thought about why we were sent there until a friend gave me a book in 1981 or 1982 about POWs. From then on I started to read more and more about the war as I wanted to know why the US had gotten involved in such a mess. Finally, I went to graduate school & earned an MA in 1991. My thesis was on the OSS involvement with Ho Chi Minh in 1945. I went on to 2 different Ph.D programs, where I studied more about the war, but did not get my Ph.D. In one program I was older than all the rest of my professors & they just didn't know how to handle someone of my age & experience. They were used to yelling at 20ish grad students & making them jump. After Vietnam, I haven't jumped for anyone. I went on to another Ph.D. program where I ran afoul of the head of the history department. I had been wounded in Vietnam in the legs & needed a cane to climb stairs. The chairman harassed me about my disability, so I had him called on the carpet. When you do that, you are basically finished in a Ph.D. program so I left that school. Vietnam has affected me somewhat profoundly in many ways. I have never been able to have any long lasting relationships with men or women since the war. I was married once for 10 years (no kids thank goodness) & that was enough. I prefer to be foot loose & fancy free. I never got along with my parents after Vietnam. My father, who worked for General Marshall, as one of his aides-de-camp during World War II, wanted me to die in Vietnam. He preferred to have a dead hero than a live one who returned. I took over my family's business 3 years after returning from Vietnam when my father died. No one else in my family worked in the business, but they all "thought" they knew more about it than did it. I finally left the business in 1989 & retired. I went back to school & remained in grad school from 1989 until 1995. I now live in Richmond, VA & teach Chinese history & sometimes a course on the Vietnam War, part time at a local university. My attitude towards life is to just slide along - don't make waves, but I believe that I damn well have to stick up for myself & screw anyone who tries to screw over me. About death, well, I believe that somewhere there is a bullet with my name on it & when God says it's time to go, then it's time & there's not much I can do about it. Politically, I am a Democrat, but I DO NOT TRUST the US government in any way shape or form. My philosophy of life is basically "Don't let the bastards get you down." I returned to Vietnam for the first time last May (1998) & loved it. I hit it off so well with the owner of the tour company that he asked me if I would like to work part time for him leading tours back to Vietnam & I accepted! Thus, 1-2 times a year I will be headed back to Nam giving guided tours. Perhaps my trips to Vietnam will give me some piece of mind as that country & war have haunted me for 29 years.

Tayloe Wise
1945
Glen Allen, VA USA
