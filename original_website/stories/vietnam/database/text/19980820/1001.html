WOODSTOCK!!!!! I hate the name, the image , the reoccuring hype, the whole damn thing. Everytime I'm doing fine somebody has to shove all those memories back into my mind that I would rather forget. It must have been really great for all those hippies to experience free love, drugs, communal living and life back in '69. You see, I and many other young people in uniform experienced the same things, but in a different way. I would love to have had the Woodstock crowd come and play in the mud with us. You haven't lived until you're forced to live night after night in a rice patty up to your knees in mud, bugs, snakes, leeches, rats, mosquitoes and people who are trying to kill you. Yeah, I used to listen to rock & roll too, except it was from a small transistor radio earpiece stuck in one ear so that I could hear out of the other. And of course the food - nothing like cold C-rations in a Monsoon downpour. We, like the hippies, were also unfortunate not to have a potty for a bathroom. But then again, the old hippies that are so "responsible" now perpetuate the vision of suffering to their children and are the real heros. I can still smell and feel what my "Woodstock" experience was like. I wish people who were at Woodstock would have just once realized that their fun was a nightmare for a generation who was "responsible" and didn't need 30 years to grow up. So, whenever Woodstock is remembered, I remember all the experiences of being alone, scared, miserable and forgotten except to the few people who loved me and waited for my return. Have a nice reunion at Woodstock - I won't miss mine.

Albert Balliet Airmen First Class 377th Security Police Tan Son Nhut Airbase Republic of South Vietnam Oct '69 - Oct '70
