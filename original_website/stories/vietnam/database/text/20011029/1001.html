I hope the young men who return after fighting in the Middle East are welcomed and showered with hugs and kisses rather than rotten fruit and obscene names. I hope they can forget the noises, smells, and the moments of terror that seem to cling to memories. I still see Zap, Louie, Foots, Willy Boy, Hardup, ShortRound and ALL the rest just before they "bought the big one". Why couln't it have been me instead of them? Was I lucky? Was I unlucky? I guess at least they're not fighting the war anymore as I and my brothers still are. Every night! Every time it rains, a chopper goes over, a car backfires, etc.. I'm not as uncomfortable if I sit in the corner, facing out. I feel not as vulnerable when I have my pistol strapped on. I'm a lot older now, but I'm still scared. Why won't it go away?

Richard
1949
Harrisonville, MO, USA
