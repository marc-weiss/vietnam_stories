"What I've learned"

I can remember when Vietnam had an affect on me. Although, I was not alive during that era, I have an experience I will never forget. I was sitting by my Vietnam Vet Uncle during my grandfather's funeral (his father). Shots were fired for the military service that my grandfather had provided for this country. In a moments time, I was on the ground with my Uncle laying over me having flashbacks. He kept whispering to me,"Keep your head down dammit. Hear the pings? Keep your head DOWN." I remember my Mother trying to calm him down to make him realize that it was 1992, not 1972.

Later on that evening, I asked him about what happened at the funeral. Being a naive 14 year old girl, I didn't realize the pain and anger he still possessed about his tour in Vietnam. And he simply said this, "No one will understand what us GI's went through over there unless you were a GI yourself. And no one could possibly relate to the anger or pain or sadness about the War..even 20 years later, unless you were there. Darling, it's not something I want to talk about. Nor is it something I want to remember. But I can't stop the nightmares."

I never bothered my Uncle with questions about his tour in Vietnam again. As I've grown up, I've realized that no one will understand the Vietnam War or the blood shed that happened in a foreign country to our American boys. But what I do know is that all of us Americans...young and old, white and black, should give "Thanks" to the men who served in every War. They did not ask to kill. They were ordered to kill. They did not ask to see their friends die but they did. So, say your prayers and give your respect to the men and women who served/serves in our military. I know I do.

Sheree
1979
