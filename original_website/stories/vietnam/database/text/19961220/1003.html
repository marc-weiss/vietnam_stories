The Legacy of Vietnam for me...

Though I never served there, the Vietnam War has had a major impact on my life. Arising from the many questions that I have been asking myself about it over the years and seeking to come to some resolution, if only in a personal sense, I am now beginning to understand why I am still so emotional about it. It is only now, years after it has ended, that I am beginning to understand its legacy in a personal perspective.

You see, I never really agreed with the war and there-in lies my conflict for which I have been seeking a resolution for. No, I wasn't some flag burning hippie nor did I have some political agenda; I objected to the war because I honestly believed, and I still do, that my government, the same government that I was raised to respect and honor, and did, was actively betraying the brave young men and women who were being sent there. I couldn't and I still don't understand how my country could ask of its young for so much, ask them to make the ultimate sacrifice, to give so much of themselves unselfishly, and abandon them once they did so. I have never really been able to answer that question.

I guess that that frustration has manifested itself in me in much anger and mistrust. With age, though, comes some wisdom and I have come to understand that some things have no real answers, that the questions themselves may be the answers and that sometimes all we can expect to get from them is some sense of where we were then and who we are now.

That is the legacy of Vietnam for me.

Jeff Schwartz
