Memories of the Viet Nam War days are as vivid as a black and white TV will allow. Every night I would lay on the living room floor, watching the evening news as they projected the number of Viet Cong killed or wounded and then compared that number to the men that had died that day on soil that was not theirs. It seemed to me, as a very young teen, like the score in some huge ball game. As long as THEIR numbers were a lot bigger than OURS then we were winning. As I grew older I found it was not a game. Kent State, Mi Lai (sp?), protest songs, Chicago, all had an effect on the nation's , and my psyche. As a senior in 1972 I was relieved that I was not going to be drafted. I didn't have to go. My experience was through the black and white TV. Watching the brave men collect women and children into a chopper in Saigon.

Now I am 44 years old. I see the effects of Nam as I watch my neighbor collect cans to get enough money to buy enough anything to get him drunk. To dull the pain he still feels because of what he had to do in Viet Nam.

I've been to the Wall in D. C. many times. I cry. The wall is black, like the somber figures I saw each as I lay on the floor and watched the war through a black and white TV screen.

anon
1954
Woodbury, NJ USA
