I have no regrets about NOT serving in the War. I will never understand how many of my peers at the time were so 'gun ho' about going to VN. Despite my feelings, I never held those who went and came back in ill repute. In fact, while I was reasonably active in the anti-war stuff, I never recall being involved in anything targeted at those who served - except when VETs held events supporting the War. Although I would agree with those who say the VN Vets did not get the same welcome as those who served in WW2 and other wars - there was no way we could celebrate their return, because we felt so strongly about how wrong the War was. Today, anytime I go to DC I visit the Memorial. And, tears always come pouring out. I see the names and I think about how stupid the War was and what a waste of lives. I think about my lot in life and can't help about theirs and their families. I read McNamara's Book, and while I did not become angry with him, I am concerned we haven't learned our lesson. Mind you, I am not sure what the lesson is. For example, should we be in Bosnia? One part of me says, yes! This part is rooted in my belief, after what Hilter was allowed to do to the Jews and others, I believe we are morally obligated to stop the murder of a race by another race. If we don't, this country has no moral backbone. On the otherhand, I am concerned about body-bags coming home and wouldn't want my daughter in one. The VN War has left this nation feeling confused and conflicted about our role in the world. And, I don't seeing it changing, unless a bomb lands on our shore, or is planted on it by agents - not a percieved fringe group - of a another nation. Finally, I will always regret the loss of Bobby Kennedy. When I think about the chain of events - Chicago, Nixon, the War, Watergate, . . . I will always feel it was a turning for this Nation. I have never recovered from his death and I think we will never have another leader with as such a strong moral compass as Bobby. His death and the War, more than JFK's are two events in this nation's history that began our decline.
