War is terrible. It is a vacuum of morals that implodes when emotions have to deal with the many horrors the war has produced. Like the First World War, Vietnam was a war that was fought under horrifying conditions, causing traumatic stress disorders, adaptability problems for veterans and foremost death. War should be abanded.

Appeasement would have been the alternative. Letting the Communistic influence expand and form a power bloc, to heavy to withstand, but without war. Just like Chamberlain did in 1938 with Hitler. Peace in our time. But in 1938 it was just as bad an idea, as it was in in Vietnam. War is terrible, but not waging war would have been even more terrible. The Government was not blood thirsty, when it made its decision to fight. It had to make that decision. Or more blood would have been shed.

The mistake was not the decision to wage war, it was the irresponsibility of the media and the restricted means with which the war was fought, that were to blame for the number of casualties, both on US and on Vietnamese side. A quick and decisive action would have sufficed.

War and powerstruggle is programmed in man's brain; It will probably never end, but sometimes it is smarter to wage a war, than to stick to an even bloodier alternative of appeasement
