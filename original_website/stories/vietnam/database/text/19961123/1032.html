At The Wall this past Veterans Day weekend, at Panel 3-East, I had the great honor and privilege of lifting up Thomas Alexander Rudel, age 4 and 1/2, and introducing him to his grandfather, Capt. Thomas C. Metsker, who was killed on 14 November 1965 on Landing Zone X-Ray, the la Drang Valley, while trying to help another man wounded more badly than himself. Over my shoulder I could see the face of Tom's daughter and young Lex's mother, Karen. She has Tom's eyes and reflected in them was pride in her son and an overwhelming pain for the father who died when she was only 17 months old.

Not one of us left Vietnam unchanged; no one left exactly the same as he was on the day he arrived. The war scarred bodies. memories, hearts. Some it made stronger; others it weakened and is still destroying, just as forgotten and corroded old landmines in Indochina still claim the lives of children not born when the mines were planted.

Vietnam is indelibly stamped on us. On the second day of the ground war in the Persian Gulf I sat with an Army brigadier general, riding out a desert dust storm and waiting for the next phase of the offensive. He had been a lieutenant in Vietnam. I asked him what Vietnam meant to him. He looked startled for a moment, then replied: "It meant EVERYTHING. It was one of two defining moments in my life. It is the filter through which I see the world; by which I judge people and situations. Isn't it the same for you?" I nodded yes, then almost as an afterthought I asked what that second defining moment was in his life. "Raising my second daughter, who is a Downs Syndrome child," he answered. "I'm a military man and everything we do is a search for perfection. But that child taught me there are other ways to judge and appreciate perfection.'" How fortunate we are, as a nation, are to be served by men such as this, by men whose character was formed and forced in the fires of that terrible war.

Joe Galloway is Senior Writer for U.S. News and World Report and co-author of two books: "We Were Soldiers Once...and Young" and "Triumph Without Victory"
