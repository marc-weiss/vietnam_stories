The only time I skipped out of my senior year in high school, besides seeing the movie 2001 in 1970, was when I joined my classmates in a walkout to protest the war in Vietnam. We had recently had a "teach-in" at our school as a consciousness raising activity. Many people had become sympathetic to the cause of opposing the war. We felt that we had done the right thing. Years after, when Viet vets claimed that they had no support when they returned from duty, I couldn't believe my ears. Didn't they know that we had an appreciation for the hell they were going through, for the failure of leadership that kept them there, for understanding the things that caused them to have to be there? It hurt more to know that U.S. Viet Vets felt estrangement from their countrymen than to know that we were fighting an unjust war in the first place.

Renee M. Russell
