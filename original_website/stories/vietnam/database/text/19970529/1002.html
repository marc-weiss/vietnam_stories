My 18 months in Vietnam deeply affected my life. On the positive side I met my wife, Ruk-Long, in Thailand after leaving Vietnam in 1972 and returning to S.E. Asia to Thailand in 1973. We have five children and a very good life together. Also, I met several good people while in S.E. Asia. My fondest memory is of Sister Mary Goretti, nee Ngoc Lan. Putting aside gender, she was to me the "Robin Hood of Bien Hoah Province." She oversaw an orphanage and our unit, Headquarters and Headquarters Company, 1st Aviation Brigade, worked with the kids every Sunday. We helped fix up the orphanage by doing general electric and carpentry work, chopping wood, and cleaning the pig/chicken yard. We asked people in the U.S. to send food, clothing, and medicine. The number of boxes sent was phenomenal. Getting back to Sister Goretti. She knew the American psyche very well. She even tried playing the guitar like the "Singing Nun." Her most unforgettable character. She used to say, "Life is like a rose, very beautiful AND WITH THORNS." Best Wishes, Always, Terry Terry W. Colvin 300 Highland Drive Sierra Vista, AZ 85635
