Alone and dressed in nothing more than filthy rags it was obvious he was quite ill. I guessed his age to be about 12 years and already most of his teeth were gone and the rest rotting. He smelled awful, his stomach sticking out like a small balloon. We exchanged names and I showed him the picture of my wife I kept carefully wrapped in plastic in my pocket. He showed me a figure he had carved from a piece of soft wood using a sharpened spoke from a Honda motor bike wheel. It was the image of a woman and was quite nicely done. I would learn later that the name he had given his work was not a name but the Vietnamese word for grandmother.

Chuck Sowards
