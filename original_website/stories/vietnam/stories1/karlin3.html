I wondered if she was thinking of the millions of dead and hundreds of thousands of missing the Vietnamese still have, and how unequal the numbers are, and I thought how in the end it didn't matter, there was enough pain on both sides to allow connection and forgiveness.

"We've all suffered," Khue said, reading my mind.

Look who I brought to you, I thought, as I touched my friend's name on the Wall. And I thought: Rest in peace, Jim .

Wayne Karlin is a writer and co-editor with Le Minh Khue of The Other Side of Heaven, an anthology of stories by Vietnamese and American writers about the aftereffects of the war. © 1996
