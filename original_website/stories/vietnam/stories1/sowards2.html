In the fall of 1971 I sat idle at a fire base for several hours, kept company by a small Vietnamese boy.

It was a completely desolate place, thoroughly defoliated and pockmarked with shell holes. On the flight in I was stunned to see for the first time how large the defoliated areas actually were and how completely devastating these powerful chemicals could be. There was simply not anything green remaining at all. What had been a lush tropical jungle looked from the air like a forest of huge tooth picks in a desert. The boy had wandered in from the surrounding waste land and casually approached our helicopter.

Chuck Sowards
