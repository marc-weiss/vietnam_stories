I cannot pin down the trigger. But somewhere between the NEA censorship debates and the run up to the Gulf War, as I drilled, whittled and scraped to prepare for my sculpture show, the memory of gentle Dr. Ahn and his flute unfolded before me. Perhaps it was a flood of the same old ironies that haunt us still. Or, as Yogi Berra put it, it was "deja vu, all over again".

Ken Hruby is a sculptor and lives in Boston.
