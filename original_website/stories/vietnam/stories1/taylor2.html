In the sixties my younger brother and I immigrated to Canada in protest of that war. Our family was divided. Scars remain. Some years ago a small replica of the Vietnam memorial was exhibited in Vancouver. I asked an attending U.S.war amputee to check a few names from his list. In deference I remained silent but wanted to ask him about his loss of both limbs. To what avail? I wanted to ask him. To what avail would my intrusion have been? I think of courage when the Vietnam issue is reopened. Courage in terms of the fragile trust held by those vets even years later when they cling to the glory days of their military service. I reflect on the courage, or endurance, of the Vietnamese women, children and elders. I think they are the real heroes of that war.

Carol Taylor
