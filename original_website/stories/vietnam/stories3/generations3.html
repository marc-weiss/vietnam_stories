In October 1996 I visited the Wall for the first time... accompanied by my four year old son. Try explaining to a four year old the meaning, the pain, and the sacrifice represented by the Wall. Yet, without much help from me, he seemed to understand the overpowering sadness of the place. He did not ask "why did these people have to die?"

But he saw his dad cry for the first time. Later that day he saw his dad cry for the second time... as we walked amongst the AIDS Quilt being displayed in memory of another 50,000+ dead Americans. I will pray for the families of those who grieve. I will pray for a brighter future for my son.

Michael Lawell (excerpts)
Washington State.
