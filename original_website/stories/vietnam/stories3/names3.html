Michael E. Toliver

It is June, 1983, shortly after Memorial Day - rush hour on Friday afternoon in Washington, D.C., and Peg and I stand near the Viet Nam Memorial. I check the ledger for the names, but I'm unsure of the spellings. Most of them were known by nicknames, or by some corruption of their last names. Spelling was never a priority until we were leaving to go home and were swapping addresses. The names in the ledger didn't swap addresses with anyone before they left.
